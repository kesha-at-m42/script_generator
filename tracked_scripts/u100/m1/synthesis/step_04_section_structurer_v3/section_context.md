## s2_1_real_world_bridge_real_world

**VISUAL STATE:** A scenario_display tangible shows a bookshelf illustration with multiple rows containing 7 books each. Below the illustration, a multiple-choice prompt displays four options: "23 books," "28 books," "31 books," and "35 books."

**CONTENT:** This section introduces the concept of **multiples** in a real-world context. Students learned that when items are arranged in equal groups (rows of 7 books), the total number of items must be a multiple of that group size. The vocabulary term **"multiples"** was formally introduced, with examples: 28 = 7 × 4 and 35 = 7 × 5 are multiples of 7, while 23 and 31 are not.

**STUDENT ACTION:** The student selected one of four multiple-choice options to identify which total could represent books on a shelf with rows of 7. Both "28 books" and "35 books" were marked as correct responses, each triggering affirmation dialogue explaining the multiplication relationship (7 × 4 = 28 or 7 × 5 = 35).
