## s2_1_both_orientations_match_one_rectangle
# Section Summary: s2_1_both_orientations_match_one_rectangle

**VISUAL STATE AT SECTION END:**
Three tangibles are on screen: (1) Hundred Grid Mode B displaying a static 4 × 5 rectangle with 4 columns, 5 rows, and 20 total tiles; (2) Multiple Choice Checkbox interface showing four equation options (4 × 5 = 20, 20 = 4 × 5, 4 + 5 = 20, 4 × 20 = 5) with the first two selected; (3) Drop Down / Fill-in-the-Blank prompt with stem "These equations are correct because ___" and one of two correct options selected.

**CONTENT:**
Students learned that the same multiplication fact can be written in two equivalent forms using the commutative property of multiplication (4 × 5 = 20 and 20 = 4 × 5 both describe the same rectangle). The concept of the equal sign as representing equivalence (both sides have the same value) was formally introduced and reinforced.

**STUDENT ACTION:**
Students performed two interactions: (1) selected both correct equations from a four-option checkbox set, rejecting incorrect options (4 + 5 = 20 and 4 × 20 = 5); (2) completed a fill-in-the-blank sentence by selecting one of two reinforcing explanations about why both equations work.

## s4_1_supported_production_both_orientations_relational
# Section Summary: s4_1_supported_production_both_orientations_relational

**VISUAL STATE AT SECTION END:**
Three tangibles are on screen: (1) Hundred Grid Mode B—a 4 × 5 rectangle with 4 columns, 5 rows, 20 total tiles displayed in mode_b; (2) Mode C Paired Display—two equation panels showing left panel "4 × 5 = 20" and right panel "20 = 4 × 5" connected by an equivalence bridge; (3) Completed Fill-in-the-Blank stem with all six numeric slots filled: "I built a 4-by-5 rectangle, so I can write **4 × 5 = 20** AND **20 = 4 × 5** — they say the same thing, and the equal sign means both sides have the same value."

**CONTENT:**
Students learned that a single rectangular array can be expressed as two equivalent equations in different orientations: multiplication-equals-product form (4 × 5 = 20) and product-equals-multiplication form (20 = 4 × 5). The concept reinforced that the equal sign represents relational equivalence—both sides have the same value—and that these two orientations "say the same thing."

**STUDENT ACTION:**
The student filled in all six numeric slots in the drop-down stem to complete both equation orientations: slots 1–3 with 4, 5, 20 (for 4 × 5 = 20) and slots 4–6 with 20, 4, 5 (for 20 = 4 × 5), demonstrating understanding of bidirectional equation representation.
