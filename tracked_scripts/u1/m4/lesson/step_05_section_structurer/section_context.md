## s2_1_discovering_between_lines_worked_example
# Section Summary: Discovering Between Lines (Worked Example)

**VISUAL STATE:** A vertical bar graph titled "Stickers Collected" displays four categories (Emma, Jake, Mia, Leo) with values 30, 25, 40, and 35 respectively. The graph uses a scale of 10 with axis range 0–50, and includes ghost gridlines at intervals of 5 (lighter than the primary 10-unit gridlines). Jake's bar is highlighted throughout, ending between the 20 and 30 gridlines.

**CONTENT:** Students learned to read bar values that fall between marked gridlines by applying the concept of "half-symbols" from prior scale-of-2 work. The key vocabulary and principle introduced: when a bar ends between gridlines on a scale of 10, the midpoint represents half of 10, which equals 5. This allows students to calculate intermediate values (e.g., 20 + 5 = 25).

**STUDENT ACTION:** Students observed a worked example where Jake's bar (value 25) was highlighted and analyzed. The instructor modeled the reasoning process: identifying that Jake's bar falls between 20 and 30, recognizing that half of 10 is 5, and concluding that 20 + 5 = 25 stickers. No direct student input was required; this was a demonstration of the strategy.
