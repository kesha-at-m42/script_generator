## CROSS-REFERENCE TABLE B — TVP EXTRACTION

```
TVP: MODULE 9
===============
Learning Goal: Solve real-world and mathematical problems involving area.

Key Teaching Points:
1. Area isn't just a calculation — it answers real questions: "Will this fit?" "How much space do we have?" "Is this enough?"
2. Students choose their own strategy: draw squares, use tick marks, count groups, multiply dimensions — all valid approaches as long as the reasoning is sound
3. THE BIG INSIGHT: This module doesn't teach a new skill. It teaches students to USE their skills in context. The cognitive shift is from "find the area" (procedure) to "use area to solve this" (application). Students must read a situation, identify that area is relevant, select a strategy, and justify their answer.
4. Problems may have multiple correct answers — e.g., "Design a garden with an area of 24 square feet" has many valid solutions (builds directly on M8's reverse problems)
5. Community garden context from OUR — sustained real-world scenario across the module

CRA Stage: Abstract — dimensions-only throughout. No grid support. Students work at full independence.

Cognitive Focus:
- APPLY (Transfer) — using area skills to solve contextual problems
- CONNECT (Transfer) — linking area calculation to real-world decision-making
- CREATE (Procedural) — designing rectangles that meet area constraints

Misconceptions Targeted:
- PRIMARY: Picking numbers without understanding — Students grab numbers from the problem and multiply without reasoning about what the numbers represent. "The garden is 6 feet from the fence and the area is 24 square feet" → student multiplies 6 × 24 = 144. Addressed by requiring students to identify what each number represents before calculating.
- SECONDARY: M5: Not Multiplying Side Lengths — May resurface under cognitive load of word problems. Students who were fluent in M8 may revert to adding when the problem context demands extra processing. Addressed by consistent problem structure and Equation Builder availability.

Data Constraints:
- Forward problems: Factors 2–10, products within 100, all four unit types
  Examples: 8 × 3 = 24 sq ft, 7 × 4 = 28 sq cm, 9 × 5 = 45 sq m
- Missing-dimension problems: Target area given, one dimension given, missing dimension is a whole number
  Examples: area = 30, width = 5, length = ? (6); area = 28, width = 4, length = ? (7)
  Keep target areas to products with clean factor pairs — avoid remainders
  Missing dimension should be ≤ 10 (within multiplication fact fluency)
- Design problems: Same area targets as M8 reverse problems: 12, 18, 20, 24, 30, 36
  Design problem progression — MC scaffolding → free production:
    First 1-2 design problems (Mid, guided): Multi-select MC from factor pairs
    Subsequent design problems (Mid/Late, independent): Equation Builder with number palette (0-12)
    If product ≠ target: neutral feedback
    If product = target: Grid Rectangles displays resulting rectangle to scale
    "Can you find a DIFFERENT garden with the same area?" prompt
    After 2-3 failed attempts, guide may hint
- Comparison problems: Two rectangles with different dimensions
  Areas should be close enough to require calculation (not obviously different)
  Examples: 7×4=28 vs 5×6=30 (difference of 2); 9×3=27 vs 7×4=28 (difference of 1) [was 8×3=24 vs 9×3=27 — replaced per SF-2, shared dimension]
  Avoid same-area pairs (that's M8's point, not M9's)
- Multi-step problems: Two separate rectangles, total area ≤ 100
  Examples: 8×3=24 and 5×4=20, total = 44; 6×6=36 and 3×5=15, total = 51
  Keep individual areas ≤ 50 so the sum stays manageable
- Word problem contexts: Age-appropriate, real-world
  Gardens, rugs, bookshelves, bulletin boards, tablecloths, playgrounds
  Consistent sentence structure for Early; can vary more in Mid/Late
- Constraint problems (CHALLENGE): "At least / at most" inequality reasoning
  Guide models one during lesson; Practice includes 1-2 in MC format only
  Tagged CHALLENGE (bonus only)

Vocabulary:
- New: (none — consolidation module)
- Reinforced: area, length, width, dimension, square foot, square meter, square inch, square centimeter, factor, product, multiply
- Language pattern: "The area of [object] is ___ square [unit]." Also: "[Object A] has ___ more square [unit] than [Object B]."

PHASE-BY-PHASE FLOW:

WARM-UP:
Two word problems, same context, different questions:
  "A garden is 5 feet long and 4 feet wide. What is the area?"
  "A garden has an area of 20 square feet. It is 5 feet long. How wide is it?"
Students solve both, then compare: "Both problems are about the SAME garden. One gives you both sides. The other gives you the area and ONE side."
Purpose: Activate both forward (dimensions → area) and reverse (area + one dimension → other dimension) reasoning from M8. Establish that area problems come in different forms.
```

## DESIGN CONSTRAINTS (from Important Decisions)

```
DESIGN CONSTRAINTS (from Important Decisions)
=============================================

Decision #1: Unified Path with Adaptive Pacing
  Rule: Use a single instructional path following CRA progression. Differentiate through pacing and representation duration, not different approaches.
  Applies to M9? YES
  What it constrains: M9 uses a single Application-stage path. The Module Mapping's "multiple solution strategies accepted" refers to student-chosen approaches (draw, count, multiply), not different instructional paths. The guide does not teach alternative strategies — it validates them if offered. Differentiation occurs through Equation Builder availability (guide-activated for struggling students).

Decision #2: Explicit Spatial Structuring as Central Focus
  Rule: Make row-by-column "seeing" an explicit instructional target with dedicated activities.
  Applies to M9? NO
  Spatial structuring was mastered by M5-M6. M9 is at the Abstract stage — students multiply dimensions without needing to visualize rows/columns.

Decision #3: Progressive Grid Removal (Concreteness Fading)
  Rule: M8-M9: Dimensions only / text-only (abstract problem-solving).
  Applies to M9? YES — HARD CONSTRAINT
  What it constrains: No grids, no partial grids, no tick marks in any phase. All visuals are rectangle outlines with dimension labels only (or no visual at all for text-only problems). Exception: remediation pipeline may activate grid overlay for students who need it (from M7's remediation tool), but this is pipeline behavior, not authored content.

Decision #4: Array Knowledge is Helpful but Not Required
  Rule: Design Unit 2 to be self-contained. Do not assume Unit 1 completion.
  Applies to M9? PARTIAL
  By M9, students have been in the unit for 8 modules. Array knowledge is established through the unit itself, not assumed from Unit 1. However, the independence principle means M9 should not reference Unit 1 vocabulary or tools.

Decision #5: Perimeter Kept Separate — Do Not Introduce
  Rule: Do NOT introduce perimeter. Do not proactively address perimeter-area confusion.
  Applies to M9? YES — HARD CONSTRAINT
  What it constrains: No perimeter mention in any context. If students add dimensions instead of multiplying, address through multiplication reasoning, not perimeter contrast. M9's comparison problems ("Who covers more space?") must be framed as area comparisons, never as perimeter comparisons.

Decision #6: Dragging Over Tapping for Manipulatives
  Rule: Prioritize drag-to-place interactions. Exception: simple selection tasks can use tap.
  Applies to M9? PARTIAL
  M9 uses primarily MC selection (tap) and Equation Builder number selection. No drag-to-place manipulatives. The exception applies.

Decision #7: Standard Units Get Full Scaffolding
  Rule: Keep sq in/cm (L6) and sq ft/m (L7) as separate modules.
  Applies to M9? NO
  All four unit types were introduced in M5-M7. M9 uses them freely — no scaffolding needed.

Decision #8: Include Multiplication Table Connection (L11) as Module
  Rule: Include OUR L11 as a synthesis/connection module (M10).
  Applies to M9? YES (affects bridge)
  M9's Synthesis must bridge to M10, not M11. However, TVP notes that if M10 is cut, the "separate vs. connected" closing beat bridges directly to M11.

Decision #9: Keep Application Module (L15) Standalone
  Rule: Include OUR L15 as a standalone module.
  Applies to M9? NO
  L15 maps to a later module, not M9.
```
