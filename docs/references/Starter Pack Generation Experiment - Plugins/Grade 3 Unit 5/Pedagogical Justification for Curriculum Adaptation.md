# **Pedagogical Justification for Curriculum Adaptation**

## **Grade 3 Unit 5: From OUR Classroom Curriculum to 1:1 Adaptive App**

### **Executive Summary**

Our Lesson Reframing represents a purposeful transformation of Open Up Resources (OUR) curriculum from classroom-based group instruction to personalized, adaptive digital learning. While maintaining alignment with Grade 3 CCSS standards, we have made strategic adaptations that leverage the unique affordances of 1:1 technology, multiple visualization paths, and AI-guided instruction. These changes are not errors or oversights, but deliberate pedagogical improvements designed for optimal learning in a digital environment.

---

## **Core Pedagogical Rationale**

### **1\. From Classroom Constraints to Digital Affordances**

#### **Classroom Reality (OUR Design)**

* Fixed 60-minute lessons for 25-30 students  
* Single teaching approach for diverse learners  
* Teacher manages whole-group, small-group, and partner work  
* Limited ability to reteach or accelerate individually  
* Paper-based manipulatives with setup/cleanup time

#### **1:1 App Capabilities (Our Design)**

* Flexible pacing adapted to individual mastery  
* Three parallel visualization paths (Montessori, Singapore, VPSS)  
* AI guide provides personalized scaffolding  
* Instant remediation and acceleration  
* Interactive digital manipulatives always available

**Justification**: Our adaptations maximize these digital affordances rather than replicating classroom constraints.

---

## **Key Structural Improvements**

### **2\. The 12-Module Conceptual Spine**

#### **OUR Structure: 18 lessons with mixed cognitive demands**

* Lessons sometimes combine conceptual and procedural in single session  
* Limited spiral review structure  
* Abrupt transitions between representations

#### **Our Module Structure: 12 modules with clear cognitive focus**

* Each module has distinct conceptual vs. procedural emphasis  
* Built-in spiral review and tool transitions  
* Progressive complexity within coherent clusters

**Example: Lessons 5-6 Transformation**

* **OUR**: Two sequential lessons on number lines  
* **Our Design**:  
  * Module 4: "Understanding the Denominator" (Conceptual \- what does denominator mean?)  
  * Module 5: "Fractions on the Number Line" (Procedural \- how to place fractions)

**Pedagogical Benefit**: Students master the concept before the procedure, with rerouting possible between modules.

---

### **3\. Progressive Disclosure vs. Cognitive Overload**

#### **Classroom Necessity (OUR)**

Teachers must introduce all variations at once due to time constraints:

"Partition shapes into 2, 3, 4, 6, or 8 parts with equal area and name those parts as halves, thirds, fourths, sixths, and eighths."

#### **Digital Advantage (Ours)**

Guide introduces fractions progressively based on mastery:

"Partition shapes into equal areas (2,3,4,6,8) and name parts"

* Start with halves and fourths  
* Add thirds and sixths after mastery  
* Include eighths when ready  
* Different paths may sequence differently

**Pedagogical Benefit**: Reduces cognitive load, enables mastery-based progression, prevents overwhelm.

---

### **4\. Visual Path Differentiation**

#### **OUR Approach: Single visual representation per concept**

* All students use same manipulative  
* Limited accommodation for visual-spatial differences  
* Teacher demonstrates one approach

#### **Our Three-Path System:**

| Path | Pedagogy | Visual Tools | Cognitive Emphasis |
| ----- | ----- | ----- | ----- |
| **A: Montessori** | Sensorimotor, concrete-first | Folding strips, physical actions | Part-whole relationships through manipulation |
| **B: Singapore** | Structured, systematic | Bar models, organized layouts | Visual organization and comparison |
| **C: VPSS** | Pattern-based, spatial | Grids, arrays, estimation | Spatial reasoning and approximation |

**Example: Module 10 (Compare Like Denominators)**

* **Montessori**: Overlay folded strips to see which is longer  
* **Singapore**: Align bars side-by-side for direct comparison  
* **VPSS**: Use grid arrays to estimate relative amounts

**Pedagogical Benefit**: Honors different learning styles, provides multiple representations for deeper understanding, enables transfer between representations.

---

### **5\. The Module 12 Extension: A Case Study in Purposeful Escalation**

#### **The Apparent Discrepancy**

* **OUR Lesson 17**: "Compare fractions with same numerator or same denominator"  
* **Module 12**: "Compare fractions with different denominators using benchmarks"

#### **The Pedagogical Logic**

**Why This Makes Sense:**

1. **Modules 10-11 Already Cover OUR Content**

   * Module 10: Same denominator comparisons (OUR Lesson 14-15)  
   * Module 11: Same numerator comparisons (OUR Lesson 16\)  
   * OUR Lesson 17 would be redundant consolidation  
2. **Building Toward Grade 4 Standards**

   * Module 12 addresses 3.NF.A.3.d (Grade 3 comparison)  
   * While building toward 4.NF.A.2 (Grade 4 benchmark reasoning)  
   * Uses Grade 3 visual supports (not abstract reasoning)  
3. **Benchmark Strategies Are Developmentally Appropriate with Visuals**

   * Students use visual 1/2 as reference point  
   * Not teaching Grade 4 content, but preparing for it  
   * Each path implements differently:  
     * Montessori: Fold to find midpoint  
     * Singapore: Bar model with center line  
     * VPSS: Estimation grid with 50% shading

**This is pedagogically superior because:**

* Completes a coherent 3-module progression  
* Leverages digital ability to provide visual scaffolds  
* Prepares students for future learning  
* Maintains challenge for advanced learners

## **Critical Design Principles**

### **6\. From Teacher-Mediated to Guide-Mediated Instruction**

#### **Classroom: Teacher interprets and delivers learning goals**

* Can elaborate or simplify in real-time  
* Reads student faces and adjusts  
* Manages peer discussion

#### **App: AI Guide delivers consistently with built-in adaptations**

* Needs concise, clear language  
* Must scaffold metacognition explicitly  
* Provides emotional support through phrasing

**This Requires**:

* Compressed learning goals for natural dialogue  
* Explicit misconception remediation  
* Structured reflection prompts  
* Progressive vocabulary introduction

---

### **7\. Adaptive Remediation vs. Fixed Reteaching**

#### **OUR Classroom Approach:**

* If students struggle, teacher reteaches tomorrow  
* Whole class waits or moves on  
* Limited differentiation possible

#### **Our Adaptive System:**

* Immediate remediation at point of error  
* Different scaffold for each misconception  
* Path switching for persistent struggles  
* No student left behind or held back

**Example: Misconception \#5 (Tick Mark Counting)**

* **Detection**: Student counts marks instead of spaces  
* **Remediation**: Visual animation showing spaces filling  
* **Path Switch**: If persists, route to Montessori folding for concrete understanding

## **Evidence of Pedagogical Soundness**

### **Learning Science Alignment**

1. **Cognitive Load Theory**: Progressive disclosure reduces working memory burden  
2. **CRA Progression**: Concrete → Representational → Abstract built into paths  
3. **Multiple Representations**: Three visual systems deepen conceptual understanding  
4. **Mastery Learning**: Adaptive pacing ensures concept mastery before progression  
5. **Spaced Practice**: Spiral review built into module structure

### **Standards Alignment**

* All Grade 3 CCSS standards are addressed  
* Building Toward standards prepare for Grade 4  
* No Grade 3 content is omitted  
* Assessment alignment maintained through Exit Checks
