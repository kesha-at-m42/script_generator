OUR Lessons: 7, 8 (reframed) | Standards: 3.OA.B.6, 3.OA.C.7, 3.OA.A.4

Learning Goal: Represent situations involving equal groups using multiplication and division equations with a symbol for the unknown quantity. Generate related division facts from known multiplication facts across the full range with increasing complexity.

Key Teaching Points:
1. A fact family is a set of four related equations using the same three numbers — two multiplication and two division. Students previewed this in M3 Synthesis; M4 makes it systematic.
2. The inverse relationship from M3 becomes a STRATEGY: "If I know 7 × 8 = 56, then I also know 8 × 7 = 56, 56 ÷ 7 = 8, and 56 ÷ 8 = 7."
3. THE BIG INSIGHT: Fact families aren't just four equations to memorize — they're proof that multiplication and division are connected. The array makes this visible.
4. Word problems ground the connection: a real situation can be described by BOTH a × and a ÷ equation.
5. Unknown representation advances: □ introduced (per D7). Practice uses BOTH ? and □ interchangeably from this point forward.
6. Per SME: facts provided across the full range with increasing complexity — NOT gated by self-assessed fluency.

What Students DO:
- Build arrays from word problems, then produce BOTH × and ÷ equations for the same situation
- Produce complete fact families (all four equations) from given arrays with increasing complexity
- Produce remaining family members from a single given equation (no visual)
- Use think-multiplication as a named strategy: "□ × 7 = 35. I know 5 × 7 = 35, so □ = 5."
- Use check-by-inverse: "Is 42 ÷ 6 = 7 correct? Use multiplication to check."
- Identify dividend, divisor, quotient in both × and ÷ contexts (vocabulary retrieval from M3)

Cognitive Focus:
- CREATE (Procedural) — generating complete fact families
- CONNECT (Transfer) — applying inverse relationship as a deliberate strategy
- IDENTIFY (Conceptual) — recognizing that four equations share three numbers and WHY
- APPLY (Transfer) — using known × facts to solve ÷ problems

CRA Stage: Representational → early Abstract

Misconceptions Targeted:
