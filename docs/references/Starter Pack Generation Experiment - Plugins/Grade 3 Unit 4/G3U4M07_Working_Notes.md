TVP: MODULE 7
===============
Title: Multiplying by Multiples of 10
OUR Source: OUR Lesson 12 | Standard: 3.NBT.A.3 (multiply one-digit whole numbers by multiples of 10)

Learning Goal: Multiply one-digit whole numbers by multiples of 10 using strategies based on place value and the properties of operations.

Key Teaching Points:
1. This is a PLACE VALUE module, not a computation tricks module. Students must understand WHY 4 × 30 = 120, not just compute it.
2. Four-stage progression: (1) Base-10 Blocks Grouping Mode — see groups of tens concretely, (2) unit language — "4 groups of 3 tens = 12 tens = 120", (3) place value chart — see digit positions, (4) decomposition chain — 4 × 30 = 4 × 3 × 10 = 12 × 10 = 120 (associative reasoning made visible, property not named per D5).
3. THE BIG INSIGHT: "Multiples of 10 always end in 0" — students discovered this pattern in Unit 1 M9. Now they know WHY: when you multiply groups of ten, you get tens, and tens always have 0 in the ones place.
4. NEVER "add a zero" as a computation strategy (D6). Acknowledge, redirect to place value reasoning.
5. Cross-unit bridge: Opens with Unit 1 M9 pattern activation.
6. This is a DIFFERENT strategy from M6. M6 used the distributive property (breaking apart). M7 uses place value reasoning (associative property, unnamed). The tool switch (Grid Rectangles → Base-10 Blocks) signals the strategy switch.
7. CRA Stage: Concrete → Abstract. Base-10 Blocks make tens visible (concrete), then transfers to place value charts and decomposition chains (abstract).

Cognitive Focus:
- IDENTIFY (Conceptual) — recognizing that multiplying by a multiple of 10 means making groups of tens
- CONNECT (Transfer) — linking the Unit 1 M9 "ends in 0" pattern to place value reasoning
- CREATE (Procedural) — using unit language and decomposition chains to compute
- APPLY (Transfer) — applying to real-world contexts (e.g., dollar/coin contexts per SME suggestion)

Misconceptions Targeted:
- U4.5: Incorrect place value in ×10 (PRIMARY) — Students who "add a zero" without understanding will be caught when asked to explain WHY. Base-10 Blocks make tens visible as units.

What Students DO:

WARM-UP — Unit 1 M9 Pattern Activation
  Guide: "In Unit 1, you discovered that multiples of 10 always end in 0. 1 × 10 = 10, 2 × 10 = 20, 3 × 10 = 30... Why do you think that happens? Today you'll find out."
  Purpose: Activate prior knowledge (×10 pattern from Unit 1 M9), create the question that M7 answers.

LESSON
  Early activities (Base-10 Blocks — groups of tens made visible):
    - Base-10 Blocks (Grouping Mode — spatially separated groups of ten-rods) + running count in unit language
    - Key beat — unit language with visual: Show 4 spatially separated groups of 3 ten-rods. Running count as groups combine: "3 tens... 6 tens... 9 tens... 12 tens." Hold at "12 tens" — "12 tens, no ones — that's why the ones place is 0!" Regroup to show 120.
    - Place value chart displayed alongside showing digit positions (1 in hundreds, 2 in tens, 0 in ones).
    - 3–4 problems. Guide models unit language ("__ groups of __ tens = __ tens = __"). Students observe, then produce unit language with prompting.
    - Starter Pack note: This is the first module where students routinely produce three-digit products. The guide should acknowledge this milestone.

  Mid activities (Place value chart as primary, blocks as scaffold):
    - Place value chart (primary) + Base-10 Blocks (available as scaffold) + Equation Builder (decomposition chain)
    - Student solves ×10 problems using place value reasoning. Equation Builder shows the decomposition chain: 4 × 30 = 4 × 3 × 10 = 12 × 10 = 120. The chain makes associative reasoning visible (property not named per D5).
    - Question/test language target: "Why is 4 × 30 the same as 4 × 3 × 10?"
    - Unit language maintained: "4 × 3 tens = 12 tens = 120."
    - Base-10 Blocks available if student needs concrete support, but place value chart is the working tool.
    - 3–4 problems. Student produces unit language and decomposition chains with decreasing guide support.

  Late activities (Independent computation + "add a zero" redirect):
    - Equation Builder (decomposition chain mode) — student solves ×10 problems independently using place value reasoning.
    - No Base-10 Blocks, no place value chart (unless remediation needed).
    - "Add a zero" redirect (proactive, not reactive): Guide directly addresses the pattern students may have encountered. Position in Late when students have internalized the place value reasoning and can evaluate the shortcut against it.
    - 3–4 problems with increasing factor size (up to 9 × 90).

EXIT CHECK:
  3 problems:
  1. Compute with unit language: Given a ×10 problem (e.g., 6 × 40), student produces the product and states unit language.
  2. Decomposition chain: Given a ×10 problem, student builds the chain in Equation Builder: a × (b × 10) = (a × b) × 10 = product.
  3. Explain WHY (visual-selection preferred): Given a ×10 product (e.g., 5 × 70 = 350), student identifies why the ones digit is 0. A visual-selection format may assess this understanding more naturally than text-based MC.

PRACTICE:
  - Mixed ×10 problems across full single-digit range (2–9) × multiples of 10 (10–90)
  - Unit language expected in some problems, decomposition chains in others
  - Base-10 Blocks available as remediation scaffold
  - Error patterns to watch: computing single-digit product correctly but misplacing zero (4 × 30 = 12 instead of 120), "adding a zero" to the wrong number, inability to explain WHY the product ends in 0

SYNTHESIS:
  - Callback to warm-up: "Multiples of 10 always end in 0. Now you know WHY — because you're multiplying TENS, and tens always have 0 in the ones place."
  - Vocabulary consolidation: multiples of ten, place value, groups of tens
  - Bridge to M8: "You can multiply by multiples of 10 using place value. But what about numbers like 13 or 24 — numbers that AREN'T multiples of 10? You'll need a different strategy for those. Good thing you learned one in M6 — breaking apart."

SCAFFOLDING PROGRESSION:
  Early → Mid → Late within one module. Narrow concept (multiplying by 10s) permits full CRA within one session. Struggling students routed back to Mid (place value chart) or Early (blocks) — adjust representation, not number range.

## Section Plan

### Warmup: Unit 1 M9 Pattern Activation (~2-3 min)
- **Type:** Activation (Prior Knowledge Recall — cross-unit bridge)
- **Prior knowledge activated:** Unit 1 M9 pattern ("multiples of 10 always end in 0"), multiplication fluency from M1-M6
- **Hook:** "You discovered something about multiples of 10. Let's figure out WHY."
- **Bridge target:** Create the question that the Lesson answers: WHY do multiples of 10 end in 0?
- **M6 bridge constraint:** M6's IC said "you'll look at what happens when one factor is a multiple of 10... a different kind of strategy... place value instead of breaking apart." Warmup must honor this — the student KNOWS a place value strategy is coming, so don't frame it as pure mystery. Frame it as "you know the WHAT, now let's discover the WHY."
- **⚠️ AF4 — U1 M9 cross-unit alignment:** The current U1 M9 SP teaches "add a zero" as a named shortcut — which contradicts D6 and M7's Warmup frame ("transforms pattern observation into understanding"). SME must decide: (A) amend U1 M9 to align with D6, or (B) shift M7 Warmup frame to acknowledge what students were actually told, not just the pattern. **BLOCKING TASK 2 — requires Andrea's decision.**
- **Estimated interactions:** 2-3 (activation + judgment + bridge)

### Lesson: Place Value Strategy for ×10 (~10-12 min)

**Scaffolding Fade Plan (Grade 3 — minimum 2-3 Full examples before Partial):**

| Phase | Interactions | Scaffold Level | What's Removed |
|-------|-------------|----------------|----------------|
| S1 Early | 1.1–1.2 (+ grouping moment) | **Full** — Guide models unit language, running count, blocks visible | Nothing — full support |
| S1 Early | 1.3–1.4 | **Partial** — Guide prompts, student produces unit language | Guide no longer models; student produces |
| S2 Mid | 2.1–2.2 | **Partial** — Guide introduces chain, student builds with support | Blocks removed as primary; chart + EB |
| S2 Mid | 2.3–2.4 | **Partial → Independent** — Student builds chain, Guide confirms | Chart support fades |
| S3 Late | 3.1–3.2 | **Independent** — Student constructs chains alone | Chart removed; EB only |
| S3 Late | 3.3 | **Independent** — "Add a zero" redirect (~30-45s aside) | N/A — conceptual consolidation |
| S3 Late | 3.4 | **Independent** — Final problem, full range | All scaffolds removed |

- **Section 1 (Early — Concrete):** Base-10 Blocks Grouping Mode. Guide models groups of tens, running count in unit language. Place value chart alongside. 3-4 problems with small factors (2-5) × small multiples (10-40). Products ≤ 200. CRA: CONCRETE. Students observe/participate, produce unit language with prompting.
  - **Associative grouping moment (AF3 — confirmed):** MUST be a **separate interaction** from the first ×10 problem. Two distinct interactions: (1) students verify 3 × 2 × 5 = 30 both ways: (3 × 2) × 5 and 3 × (2 × 5) — informal language: "When you multiply three numbers, you can group them differently and still get the same answer"; then (2) first ×10 problem with blocks. Collapsing into one interaction risks cognitive overload (new toy + new concept + new grouping strategy on Early's first beat).
  - **M6 bridge connection (required):** S1's first ×10 interaction must explicitly honor M6's promise. Guide language: "In our last module, you broke apart to make multiplication easier. Now you'll discover a DIFFERENT way — using place value — that works perfectly for multiples of 10." This is the bridge, not the Warmup.
  - **Major pedagogical move:** Unit language introduction — "__ groups of __ tens = __ tens = __"
  - **Three-digit product milestone acknowledgment** per TVP Starter Pack note
- **Section 2 (Mid — Representational/Abstract):** Place value chart as primary scaffold display (populated as student produces unit language), blocks available as scaffold, Equation Builder shows decomposition chain. 3-4 problems with expanding factors. Products ≤ 400. CRA: REPRESENTATIONAL → ABSTRACT. Students produce unit language and build decomposition chains with decreasing support.
  - **Major pedagogical move:** Decomposition chain introduction — 4 × 30 = 4 × 3 × 10 = 12 × 10 = 120
  - **Key question:** "Why is 4 × 30 the same as 4 × 3 × 10?"
- **Section 3 (Late — Independent + "Add a Zero" Redirect):** Equation Builder only, no blocks, no chart. Full factor range (up to 9 × 90). Products up to ~800. CRA: ABSTRACT. Students apply independently.
  - **Major pedagogical move:** Proactive "add a zero" redirect — acknowledge the pattern, redirect to place value reasoning. **Scoped as ~30-45s Pattern 2 teaching-only aside, NOT a full interaction.** Position after 1-2 successful independent chains so students evaluate the shortcut against their own place value understanding. Don't let it become Late's center of gravity.
  - **Chain-format constraint:** Equation Builder template shape must enforce 3-step decomposition chain format. Late must require chain-format answers (a × b0 = a × b × 10 = product), not just correct products. Students who could produce correct answers via breaking-apart (from M6) must use the place value decomposition path.
  - 3-4 problems with increasing factor size

### Exit Check (~3-5 min)
- **Skills assessed:** Computation + unit language, decomposition chain construction, place value understanding (WHY ones digit is 0)
- **Cognitive types:** CREATE (chain building), IDENTIFY (visual selection for WHY), APPLY (computation with unit language) — M7 is M7, so all cognitive types available per EC Playbook M7-12 range
- **3 problems, each testing a different skill:**
  1. Compute + unit language (APPLY)
  2. Build decomposition chain (CREATE)
  3. Visual-selection WHY (IDENTIFY)

### Synthesis (~5-7 min)
- **Connection tasks:** Callback to warmup ("now you know WHY"), vocabulary consolidation, possible money/coin context (per SME suggestion), metacognitive reflection on strategy
- **Bridge to M8:** "What about numbers that AREN'T multiples of 10?"
- **Task types:** at least 2 different types
- **Closure:** Behaviorally specific achievement + M8 bridge
