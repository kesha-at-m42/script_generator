# G3U4 Modules 1–4: Cross-Unit Scope, Scaffolding & Coherence Analysis

**Date:** 2026-04-10  
**Evaluator:** Cross-module + Pedagogy evaluation pipeline (7 agents)  
**Modules:** M1 (What is Division?), M2 (Representing Division), M3 (Division as Unknown Factor), M4 (Fact Families & Inverse Relationship)

---

## Executive Summary

The first four modules of Grade 3 Unit 4 form a **well-architected learning arc** that moves students from concrete experience with division through representational fluency to early abstract strategy work. Cross-module coherence is strong — scope boundaries are clean, vocabulary progressions are systematic, and bridges between modules are (with a few exceptions) well-matched.

**Overall Verdict: PASS WITH CONDITIONS**

The conditions fall into three categories:

1. **Bridge precision issues (M1→M2)** — narrative language in M1's Synthesis doesn't precisely match M2's actual tool design
2. **Scaffolding cliff (M3 S2→S3)** — awaiting SME review of whether the verbal bridge is sufficient for Grade 3
3. **Vocabulary retrieval design (M4)** — clustering of retrieval checks limits spacing and genuine recall

No CRITICAL findings across any evaluation. The unit's pedagogical foundation is sound.

## The Learning Arc: M1 → M2 → M3 → M4

| Module | CRA Stage | Primary Tool | Core Cognitive Move | What Students Can Do After |
|--------|-----------|-------------|---------------------|---------------------------|
| **M1** | Concrete | Equal Groups (observation) | "Division means finding unknown group information from a total — two types" | Identify quotients from animations; distinguish partitive/quotitive via framing question |
| **M2** | Concrete→Representational | Equal Groups (construction) + Equation Builder | "Division can be represented as drawings, ÷ expressions, and word problems — one expression serves both types" | Build EG representations; read/write ÷ expressions; translate between representations |
| **M3** | Representational | Arrays + Equation Builder (with ?) | "Division IS finding a missing factor — ? × 3 = 15 is the same as 15 ÷ 3 = ?" | Read one array as both × and ÷; find unknown factors; produce equation pairs |
| **M4** | Representational→Abstract | Arrays (construction) + Equation Builder (□, letters) | "One set of three numbers generates four equations — knowing any one unlocks the others" | Produce complete fact families; use think-multiplication as a division strategy; verify with check-by-inverse |

**This progression is pedagogically strong.** Each module builds directly on the prior, with no gaps where students would need knowledge they haven't been given. The CRA progression across modules (Concrete → Concrete/Representational → Representational → Representational/Abstract) is gradual and well-paced for Grade 3.

### What's Working Well Across the Unit

**Scope boundaries are consistently clean.** Every concept deferred in Module X is picked up in Module X+1. No content falls through a gap, and nothing is double-taught. The "Must Not Include" lists are precise and the downstream modules honor them.

**Vocabulary staging follows a consistent architecture.** The unit uses a three-stage model — *informal use → exposure → active retrieval* — that's executed cleanly:
- "Quotient" is informal in M1-M2, formally exposed in M3, actively retrieved in M4
- "Dividend/divisor" are avoided in M1-M2, exposed in M3, retrieved in M4
- "Fact family" is previewed in M3 Synthesis, formalized in M4 Warmup
- "Inverse" is shown without naming in M3, named informally in M4

**Misconception strategies compound rather than contradict.** U4.2 (partitive/quotitive confusion) is prevented through 50-50 balance in all four modules. U4.3 (can't connect × and ÷) is prevented through arrays in M3 and tested through construction in M4. A1 (dividend/divisor confusion) is prevented through meaning-based grounding in M2. Each module's prevention strategy builds on the prior module's foundation.

**Data value ranges progress appropriately.** No module regresses to easier values than its predecessor. M1 (dividends 10-30, divisors 2-5) → M2 (10-40, divisors 2-6) → M3 (factors 2-10, products ≤100) → M4 (factors 2-10, products ≤100 with increasing difficulty within phases). The deliberate reuse of 3×5=15 across M3 and M4 warmups is documented as an intentional narrative bookend (KDD-1).

## Issues Requiring Attention

### PRIORITY 1: M1→M2 Bridge Terminology Mismatch (3 related MAJOR findings)

**What's happening:** M1's Synthesis closure says students will next be "drawing your own division pictures and learn a new symbol for dividing." But M2's primary interaction mode is *stepper construction* (tool-based building), not representational drawing. Additionally, M1's Synthesis implies M2's Warmup will callback to division, but M2's Warmup actually callbacks to the multiplication activation from M1's own Warmup. And M1 heavily emphasizes the framing question as a key takeaway, but M2's Warmup doesn't explicitly re-activate it until Lesson S1.

**Why it matters:** These three issues together create a "narrative seam" — the story M1 tells about what's coming doesn't precisely match the story M2 tells about where students are coming from. The *pedagogy* is sound on both sides, but the *bridge language* is imprecise.

**Recommended fixes:**
1. Revise M1 Synthesis closure: "building Equal Groups representations with new controls" instead of "drawing pictures"
2. Either revise M1 Synthesis to reflect M2's actual warmup sequence, OR add explicit framing-question re-activation to M2 Warmup constraints
3. Add the framing question ("Do you know the number of groups, or the number of items in each group?") as an explicit M2 Warmup constraint

### PRIORITY 2: M3 S2→S3 Scaffolding Cliff (AF3.1 — SME Decision Required)

**What's happening:** In M3, Section 2 ends with students working alongside visible partial-concealment arrays and equation displays. Section 3 opens with a verbal bridge only ("Think about what the array would look like...") and asks students to produce equations without any visual support. This represents a drop from MEDIUM scaffolding to LOW in a single step, with simultaneous increases in cognitive demand (internalize array + apply inverse + construct equation = 3 novel moves).

**Why it matters:** Grade 3 students typically need 2-3 steps before independence. A single verbal bridge may be thin. This is the most significant pedagogical concern in the unit.

**Status:** Documented as AF3.1 — awaiting Andrea's decision. If she confirms the verbal bridge + remediation tap is sufficient, accept as designed. If not, insert an intermediate step with a faded/ghosted array visual between S2 and S3.

### PRIORITY 3: M2 Assessment Vocabulary Ambiguity

**What's happening:** M2's §1.3 lists "quotient" in assessment vocabulary (line 129: "Assessment Vocabulary (appears on state test): divide, equal groups, quotient"), but M2's §1.2 explicitly states quotient is NOT formally taught — only the Guide may say "the answer to a division problem" informally. This creates confusion about whether M2 assesses quotient or not.

**Recommended fix:** Remove "quotient" from M2's assessment vocabulary list. Students don't formally encounter this term until M3 S2. The state test alignment for quotient should map to M3 or later.

### PRIORITY 4: M4 Vocabulary Retrieval Clustering

**What's happening:** M4's vocabulary retrieval checks for dividend/divisor/quotient are clustered in three consecutive sub-parts (2.1b, 2.2b, 2.4b) within Section 2, all using the same three-option MC structure. This allows pattern-matching (students learn the MC structure, not the terms) rather than genuine retrieval.

**Recommended fix:** Space vocabulary checks across more interactions — move at least one check to Synthesis or EC to create retrieval distribution. Consider varying the distractor structure in some checks (include distractors from adjacent concepts, not just the same three terms rotating).

### PRIORITY 5: 3.OA.A.2 Standard Ownership

**What's happening:** M2 claims to address 3.OA.A.2 ("interpret whole-number quotients") with "representational fluency with ÷ expressions." M3 skips 3.OA.A.2 entirely, jumping to 3.OA.B.6. It's unclear whether M2 alone fulfills 3.OA.A.2 or whether it's a cumulative standard spanning M1-M2.

**Recommended fix:** Clarify standard ownership. If 3.OA.A.2 spans M1 (concrete interpretation) + M2 (representational interpretation), document this explicitly in both SPs as a "multi-module learning standard." If M2 alone addresses it, confirm its Exit Check fully assesses the standard.

### Pedagogical Strengths Across the Unit

**Vocabulary timing is consistently excellent.** All four modules follow Known Pattern #11 — terms are introduced AFTER concrete experience, never front-loaded. The sequence is always: observe → identify → name.

**Think-aloud quality is strong.** All four modules provide first-person think-alouds that model *process* (not just answers), fade in frequency as scaffolding decreases, and use metacognitive tags appropriately for authoring clarity.

**Relational bridge interactions are high-quality.** M1's paired contrast (3.2-3.3, split-screen showing both division types), M2's "same expression, both types" (2.4), and M3's "THE reframe" (2.1, partial-concealment array revealing unknown-factor framing) are all dedicated relational interactions with explicit pattern-stating and student confirmation.

**Scaffolding fade curves are generally smooth within modules.** Each module follows Full → Partial → Independent within its lesson sections. The exception is M3's S2→S3 transition, which is the unit's primary scaffolding concern.

### Per-Module Pedagogical Notes

**M1:** The module's main risk is density — Sections 1 and 2 each have 4-5 interactions covering both vocabulary introduction AND demonstrated proficiency. This is at the upper limit for Grade 3. If user testing shows fluency dips in Section 2 (quotitive is harder), consider moving one interaction to Practice.

**M2:** Pedagogically the cleanest module. One minor finding: the relational bridge (Int 2.4, "same expression, both types") appears after students have already practiced matching equations (2.2-2.3), not before. Moving 2.4 earlier in S2 would create the ideal discover→practice→apply sequence.

**M3:** The most complex module pedagogically, and the only one with a documented scaffolding cliff (AF3.1). The inverse-relationship insight is well-grounded through arrays, but the transition to symbolic-only work in S3 may be too abrupt for Grade 3 without a faded-array intermediate step.

**M4:** Well-designed teaching arc with strong coherence. The two MAJOR concerns are: (1) vocabulary retrieval is clustered rather than spaced, and (2) the Warmup-to-S1 transition asks "could YOU build one?" but then immediately shows a worked example rather than letting students attempt construction first.

## Toy Progression Across the Unit

| Module | Primary Tool | Mode | Student Role |
|--------|-------------|------|-------------|
| **M1** | Equal Groups | Observation (Modes 1, 3, 4) | Watch animations, identify results |
| **M2** | Equal Groups + Equation Builder | Construction (Stepper Mode) + Tile Building | Build representations, write ÷ expressions |
| **M3** | Arrays + Equation Builder (with ?) | Display + Partial Concealment + Tile Building | Observe dual readings, find unknown factors, produce equation pairs |
| **M4** | Arrays (construction) + Equation Builder (with ?, □, letters) | Student-specified construction + Full tile bank | Build arrays from word problems, produce full fact families |

**The toy progression is one of the unit's strongest design features.** Equal Groups transitions from primary (M1-M2) to secondary/bridge (M3 Warmup) to retired (M4). Arrays transition from Unit 1 multiplication knowledge to division tool (M3) to construction-mode tool (M4). The Equation Builder accumulates capabilities: ÷ tiles (M2) → ? tile (M3) → □ and letter tiles (M4). Each transition is documented, justified, and bridged.
