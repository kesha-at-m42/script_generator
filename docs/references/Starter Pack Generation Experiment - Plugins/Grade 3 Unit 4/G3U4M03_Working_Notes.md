TVP: MODULE 3
=============
Learning Goal: Explain the relationship between multiplication and division equations. Interpret division equations and multiplication equations with a missing factor.

Key Teaching Points:
1. Division and multiplication are two ways of looking at the SAME relationship. If 3 × 5 = 15, then 15 ÷ 3 = 5 AND 15 ÷ 5 = 3.
2. THE BIG INSIGHT: "15 ÷ 3 = ?" is the SAME question as "? × 3 = 15." Division IS finding a missing factor.
3. Interpretation throughout, not just production: Every equation stays connected to what it MEANS in the array. Per SME: if interpretation drops out, module becomes fact family production (M4's job).
4. Bridge from Equal Groups to Arrays: "3 bags of 5 apples" becomes a 3×5 dot array. Arrays encode BOTH × and ÷ in one image.
5. Formal vocabulary: quotient, dividend, divisor — connected to factor and product from Unit 1 M9.
6. CRA Stage: Representational — arrays as primary, bridging from M1-M2's concrete Equal Groups.

What Students DO:
WARM-UP — Equal Groups to Arrays Bridge
  - Equal Groups (Stepper Mode, completed display — bags with pictures): 3 bags of 5 apples
  - Arrays (dot array, display): 3×5 dot array next to Equal Groups
  - Key beat: "3 bags of 5... and 3 rows of 5 dots. Same numbers, same structure."
  - Purpose: Bridge from EG to Arrays. Students recognize arrays from Unit 1 M11-M12.

LESSON — Early (Arrays show both operations):
  - Arrays (dot array, display with guide-controlled row/column highlighting) + Equation Builder (equation mode, display)
  - Key beat: Guide highlights rows for × reading (3 × 5 = 15), then reframes as ÷ (15 ÷ 3 = 5)
  - Both division types: Include at least one quotitive reading ("15 dots with 5 in each row — how many rows?")
  - 3 arrays. Scaffolding: full demo → second demo → guided response.

LESSON — Mid (Unknown factor framing + vocabulary formalization):
  - Arrays (dot array with partial concealment) + Equation Builder (equation mode, ? tile available)
  - Key beat — THE reframe: "? × 5 = 20. WHAT times 5 equals 20? Division is just finding a missing factor."
  - Both equations displayed simultaneously, connected back to array meaning.
  - Vocabulary intro: "The product is now the DIVIDEND. One factor is now the DIVISOR. The missing factor is now the QUOTIENT."
  - Student practice with unknown factor, mix of unknown positions, nonstandard formats per D8.
  - 5 problems total including vocab intro examples.
  - Interpretation required: student identifies what ? represents in array (not just finds number).
  - Vocabulary scope: exposure and contextual use, NOT assessed on identification (deferred to M4).

LESSON — Late (Independent application — symbolic, no array support):
  - Equation Builder (equation mode only — NO array displayed)
  - Given × or ÷ equation, produce the related equation of the other type (one pair, not full family).

  - 3 problems. Arrays available as remediation only.
  - Full four-equation production reserved for Synthesis.

EXIT CHECK — 3 problems:

  Mixed: array → both equations, × with unknown → find factor + write ÷, ÷ → equivalent × equation, word problem → equation
  Arrays as primary visual; Equal Groups for remediation
  Equation formats vary (standard and nonstandard per D8)
  Error patterns: can compute quotients but can't write equivalent × equation (U4.3); dividend/divisor position errors (A1)

SYNTHESIS:
  Student builds all four equations from single array, step by step:
  1. Row highlighting → first × equation (e.g., 4 × 7 = 28)
  2. Column highlighting → commutative × equation (7 × 4 = 28)
  3. Row highlighting → first ÷ equation (28 ÷ 4 = 7)
  4. Column highlighting → second ÷ equation (28 ÷ 7 = 4)
  "One array. Four equations. If you know one, you know the others."
  Vocabulary review: quotient, dividend, divisor — connected to factors and product
  Preview "fact family": "These four equations are a FACT FAMILY."
  Bridge to M4: "You discovered that division IS finding a missing factor. Next time, you'll build complete fact families and use your multiplication knowledge as a division STRATEGY."

Cognitive Focus:
  CONNECT (Transfer) — linking × knowledge to ÷
  IDENTIFY (Conceptual) — one array encodes both operations
  COMPARE (Conceptual) — structural relationship between × and ÷
  APPLY (Transfer) — using known × facts to find quotients

Misconceptions Targeted:

## Section Plan

### Warmup (~3 min)
**Type:** Activation + Representational Bridge
**Prior knowledge activated:** Equal Groups from M1-M2, arrays from Unit 1 M11-M12
**Bridge target:** Students see Equal Groups and Arrays as equivalent representations of the same structure. Creates anticipation for the ×/÷ connection.
**Warmup interactions:** 2 (W.1 bridge observation, W.2 array recognition check)
**Hook:** Delivers on M2's promise — "You noticed 15 ÷ 3 and 3 × 5 use the same numbers. Let's find out WHY."

| Interaction | Scaffolding Level | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| W.1 Bridge observation (EG ↔ Array side-by-side) | HIGH — Guide-directed, observation only | Activates M1-M2 + Unit 1 knowledge |
| W.2 Array recognition check (MC or click) | HIGH — Constrained response, confirms bridge | Verifies student sees structural equivalence |

### Lesson Section 1 — Arrays Show Both Operations (~4-5 min)
**CRA:** Representational (arrays alongside equations per D4)
**Focus:** One array, two equations — multiplication and division are two readings of the same structure
**Key beat:** Guide highlights rows for × reading, reframes as ÷. Both equations displayed simultaneously.
**Both division types represented:** At least one quotitive reading.
**Scaffolding:** Full demo → demo with guide narration → guided response (student identifies ÷ equation)

| Interaction | Scaffolding Level | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| S1.1 Full demo — Guide reads array as × then ÷ | HIGH — Observation, think-aloud | Student watches, no response required |
| S1.2 Second array — Guide narrates, student observes equations | HIGH → MEDIUM — Guide narrates, student identifies pattern | May include click to confirm which equation is ÷ |
| S1.3 Third array — Guided response | MEDIUM — Student selects/identifies the ÷ equation | First student action on inverse reading |

### Lesson Section 2 — Unknown Factor + Vocabulary (~5-6 min)
**CRA:** Representational → Relational bridge (arrays with partial concealment, vocabulary formalization)
**Focus:** THE reframe — "? × 5 = 20 is the SAME question as 20 ÷ 5 = ?"
**Vocabulary introduction:** quotient, dividend, divisor connected to factor/product (AFTER the inverse insight lands)
**Key beat:** Connection language: "The product is now the dividend. One factor is now the divisor. The missing factor is now the quotient."
**Interpretation push:** Student identifies what ? represents in the array context
**Scaffolding:** Worked example → vocabulary naming → guided practice with unknown positions varying

| Interaction | Scaffolding Level | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| S2.1 THE reframe — worked example with partial concealment | HIGH — Guided discovery, think-aloud | "? × 5 = 20. WHAT times 5 equals 20? Division IS finding a missing factor." |
| S2.2 Vocabulary naming moment | HIGH — Direct instruction after grounding | Connection language: product→dividend, factor→divisor, missing factor→quotient |
| S2.3 Guided practice — unknown in varied positions + interpretation | MEDIUM — Student acts, guide supports | Mix of factor positions. **Interpretation interaction:** Guide asks "What does the ? stand for in this picture?" Student selects from meaning-based options (e.g., "number of rows" / "number in each row" / "total dots" / "number of arrays") — NOT vocabulary labels ("dividend" / "divisor" / "quotient"). This keeps interpretation conceptual, not terminological, and avoids drifting into M4's vocabulary identification scope. |
| S2.4 Nonstandard format practice | MEDIUM — Student acts with nonstandard equation | At least one nonstandard format (e.g., 40 = ? × 5) per D8. Same interpretation pattern: "What does ? mean here?" with meaning-based options. |
| **S2.5 Confirmation of inverse pattern (PE5.4 fix)** | **MEDIUM → LOW — Metacognitive prompt** | **Student confirms understanding of WHY × and ÷ use the same numbers. MC or multiselect: "You saw that ? × 4 = 28 and 28 ÷ 4 = 7 use the same numbers and the same array. Why?" Options test conceptual understanding vs. procedural pattern-matching (U4.3 prevention). This is THE module's learning goal — must land before S3 releases scaffolding.** |

### Lesson Section 3 — Independent Application (~3-4 min)
**CRA:** Abstract (symbolic only — arrays fade, not vanish)
**Focus:** Given one equation type (× or ÷), produce the related equation of the other type
**Key constraint:** Pairs only — one × → one ÷ or vice versa. Full four-equation family reserved for Synthesis.

**S2→S3 Transition — Verbal Bridge:** Guide says something like: "This time, no picture. Just the equation. But think about what the array WOULD look like..." This maintains the mental model through language even as the visual scaffold is removed. The array doesn't disappear — it becomes internalized.

**Scaffolding Cliff Mitigation:** S3.1 is a "soft launch" — the array is displayed alongside the equation but de-emphasized (faded/small, not interactive). Student works from the equation, but the array is there as a visual anchor if they need it. S3.2–S3.3 remove the array entirely (available as remediation only). This creates a two-step transition instead of the all-or-nothing jump from S2's concealment arrays to pure symbolic.

| Interaction | Scaffolding Level | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| S3.1 Given × equation → produce matching ÷ (array visible but de-emphasized) | LOW-MEDIUM — Independent, array present but faded | Soft launch: equation is primary, array is passive visual anchor. Student builds equation pair. Verbal bridge: "Think about what array this would be..." |
| S3.2 Given ÷ equation → produce matching × with ? (no array) | LOW — Independent, symbolic only | Arrays removed. At least one nonstandard format per D8. Arrays available as remediation tap/click. |
| S3.3 Given unknown-factor × → find value + write ÷ (no array) | LOW — Independent, full construction | Combines unknown-factor + inverse pair. Symbolic only. |

### Exit Check (~2-3 min)
**Skills tested:** (1) Unknown factor → quotient with interpretation, (2) Array → both equations with meaning, (3) ÷ → equivalent × equation
**Cognitive types:** APPLY (EC.1), IDENTIFY (EC.2), CONNECT (EC.3) — within M1-M3 range (CREATE/IDENTIFY + APPLY/CONNECT)
**3 problems** per TVP

| Interaction | Scaffolding Level | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| EC.1 Unknown factor → quotient with interpretation | NONE — Assessment | Given ? × 4 = 28, find ?, build matching ÷, explain what ? represents |
| EC.2 Array → both equations with meaning | NONE — Assessment | Full array, produce both × and ÷ equations, identify number roles |
| EC.3 ÷ → equivalent × equation | NONE — Assessment | **Use nonstandard format (e.g., 28 = ? × 4 or ? × 6 = 42) to test for A2 misconception (SF3.3 fix).** Student must accept and work with result-on-left or unknown-on-left format. |

**A2 Distractor Design Note (SF3.3):** EC.3 uses nonstandard equation format to surface A2 (equals sign misconception). If student rejects or rewrites to standard format before solving, this is a signal. At least 1 of 3 EC problems must use nonstandard format per D8's ≥30% ratio. The specific format choice should be the most challenging — result on left (e.g., 42 = ? × 6) — since this is the format students with A2 most consistently reject.

### Synthesis (~5-7 min)
**Task types:** Pattern Discovery (four-equation family construction), Metacognitive Reflection, Identity-Building Closure
**Key moment:** Student builds all four equations from one array step-by-step with row/column highlighting
**Vocabulary review:** quotient, dividend, divisor connected to factors/product
**Fact family preview + Bridge to M4**

| Interaction | Scaffolding Level | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Syn.1 Opening frame — revisit inverse insight | MEDIUM — Guide frames, student recalls | Connects back to S2.5 confirmation |
| Syn.2a Guide models first ×/÷ pair (row reading) | MEDIUM — Guide-led with array highlighting | Guide highlights rows → builds first × equation (e.g., 4 × 7 = 28), then reframes as ÷ (28 ÷ 4 = 7). Models the row-reading pair. |
| Syn.2b Student builds second ×/÷ pair (column reading) | LOW — Student-led with array highlighting as only scaffold | Student must shift to column reading → builds commutative × (7 × 4 = 28) AND second ÷ (28 ÷ 7 = 4). This is the genuine cognitive step — the student produces a complete pair independently, requiring the column-reading perspective shift. Stronger assessment of understanding than building all four with Guide support. |
| Syn.3 Metacognitive reflection | LOW — Student reflects | "One array. Four equations. If you know one, you know the others." |
| Syn.4 Identity closure + M4 bridge | LOW — Guide narrates, student absorbs | Fact family preview, bridge to M4 |

### Estimated Total: ~18-23 min
**Note:** This is within the ~15 min module block target (Warmup 3 + Lesson 12-15 + EC 2-3 + Synthesis 5-7 = 22-28). The Lesson at 12-15 min is at the upper edge. Watch for interactions that could be deferred to Practice. The interpretation push in Mid and the independent pairs in Late are both core to M3's learning goal and cannot be cut — if timing is tight, consider reducing Mid from 5 to 4 problems (defer S2.4 to Practice Inputs).
