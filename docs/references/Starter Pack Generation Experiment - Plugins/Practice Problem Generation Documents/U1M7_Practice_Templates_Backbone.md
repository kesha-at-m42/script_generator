### Misconception Targeting

| Misconception | ID | Relevant Skills | Priority | Template Coverage |
|---------------|----|-----------------|---------|--------------------|
| Array Dimensions as Addition | 9 | IdentifyEqualGroups | PRIMARY | Student adds groups + items instead of recognizing multiplicative structure. "3 groups of 4 = 7" instead of recognizing 3 groups with 4 each. MC distractor: sum of group count + item count. |
| Groups vs. Size Confusion | 10 | IdentifyEqualGroups | PRIMARY | Student reverses groups and items per group. "3 groups of 4" reported as "4 groups of 3." MC distractor: reversed count. |
| Multiplication Only Adds | 11 | IdentifyEqualGroups | SECONDARY | Student thinks multiplication is just addition. Less directly assessable in M7 (notation not yet produced), but shows in how students describe groups. |

### Open Questions / Flags

1. **Single-skill module template count:** Pool target is 55-65. With ONE skill, this means 55-65 templates for IdentifyEqualGroups. That's a lot. The variety comes from contexts (bags/boxes/circles), group sizes, complexity, and prompt types. Consider whether some templates could include the graph-to-multiplication bridge from the warm-up as a stretch variant.

2. **Notation exposure but not production:** The guide shows "3 × 4" but students don't write it. Templates should NOT ask students to produce × notation. Assessment is purely structural recognition: "How many groups? How many in each group?"

3. **Graph callback opportunity:** The warm-up explicitly bridges graphs → multiplication ("4 symbols at scale 5 = 4 groups of 5"). A stretch/challenge template could show a scale-5 picture graph and ask "How many groups? How many in each group?" — connecting to prior knowledge.
