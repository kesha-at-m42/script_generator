### Misconception Targeting

| Misconception | ID | Relevant Skills | Priority | Template Coverage |
|---------------|----|-----------------|---------|--------------------|
| Unknown Must Be Last | 14 | SolveMultEquation | PRIMARY | Student insists product goes at end. Can't process ☐ × 4 = 12 or 12 = 3 × 4. MC distractor: moves unknown to product position regardless of what's asked. |
| Equal Sign as "Put Answer Here" | 19 | SolveMultEquation | PRIMARY | Student thinks = means "now write the answer." Can't accept 12 = 3 × 4. MC distractor: rejects valid reversed equations. |
| Can't Use Inverse Operations | 15 | SolveMultEquation | SECONDARY | Student can't find unknown factor. Knows 3 × 4 = 12 but can't find ☐ in ☐ × 4 = 12. |
| Groups vs. Size Confusion | 10 | IdentifyEqualGroups, SolveMultEquation | SECONDARY | Reverses factors in new contexts. "3 rows of 4" → writes "4 × 3." |

### Open Questions / Flags

1. **Context variety timing:** Activities 4-5 introduce variety. This is mid-lesson. Confidence/support templates should still use familiar bags/boxes. Baseline+ introduces new contexts.

2. **= sign flexibility emphasis:** The TF and SME feedback both stress teaching = as "same value as" throughout, not just at the end. Templates should include both orientations from baseline tier onward.

3. **Unknown position scaffolding:** The TF progresses product → second factor → first factor → mixed. Tier mapping should respect this: confidence = product unknown, baseline = mixed, stretch = first factor unknown (hardest).
