## §PT.2 Goal Decomposition

### Spine Skills Active in M9

| Skill ID | Status | Description | Component | Verb | Representations |
|----------|--------|-------------|-----------|------|-----------------|
| LearnFactorProducts | INT | Recall products of 2, 5, 10 through pattern discovery and practice | conceptual | identify | Equation Builder |

**Key M9 characteristic:** Single-skill module focused on fluency building. Pattern discovery (2s are even, 5s end in 0/5, 10s end in 0) THEN repeated practice. Factor structure: ___ × 2, ___ × 5, ___ × 10 (second factor always 2, 5, or 10 = skip-counting unit). Graph scale callbacks throughout. Turn-around facts previewed late (7×2 AND 2×7).

### Skill Decomposition

| Skill | Description | Verb | Component | TF Source | EC Match | Spine Status |

| LearnFactorProducts | Given a multiplication fact with factor 2, 5, or 10, identify the product. **Sub-decomposition by factor:**
- **×2 facts:** Products are always even. Skip-count by 2s.
- **×5 facts:** Products end in 0 or 5. Skip-count by 5s. Direct graph scale callback.
- **×10 facts:** Products end in 0. Skip-count by 10s. Direct graph scale callback.

These are NOT separate skills — they're parameter variants of the same skill. But templates should cover all three factor types with targeted misconception detection.

### Open Questions / Flags

1. **Single-skill module template count:** Same challenge as M7. 55-65 templates for one skill. Variety comes from: factor type (3), first factor range, assessment method, turn-around presence, and pattern-checking items.

2. **Turn-around facts preview scope:** TF says turn-around facts are "previewed" in late activities, not mastered. Templates should include them only at stretch/challenge tier. The commutative property is formally taught in M12.

3. **Skip-counting as strategy vs. multiplication as recall:** The TF presents three strategies (addition, skip-counting, "just know it"). Templates should accept all three pathways to correct answers. The Synthesis makes this explicit: "All three ways get the same answer."

4. **Graph scale callbacks:** The TF explicitly connects ×5 and ×10 to graph scales. Consider stretch templates that show a graph and ask "What multiplication fact does this show?" — bridging back to data domain.
