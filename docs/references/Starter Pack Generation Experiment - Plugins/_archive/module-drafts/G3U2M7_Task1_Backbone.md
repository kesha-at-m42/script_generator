# M7 Task 1 Backbone: Area Without a Full Grid
**Grade 3, Unit 2: Area and Multiplication**
**OUR Lesson: L8**

---

## §1.0 THE ONE THING

### **Testable Core**
By the end of M7, students can **determine the area of a rectangle from its dimensions alone** (no grid, no tick marks) **by multiplying side lengths**, and can **state the area with the correct square unit**.

### **CRA Stage**
**Abstract.** M7 is the first module in the Grade 3 progression WITHOUT full visual grid support. Students transition from "counting visible squares" to "inferring the complete rectangular array from side length dimensions." This is the critical abstraction step in the CRA spiral.

### **Critical Misconception**
**#5: Not Multiplying Side Lengths** (PRIMARY, HIGH PRIORITY)
- **Observable Behavior:** Student reads dimensions correctly (e.g., "5 and 4") but does not multiply them. May instead:
  - Add the dimensions (5 + 4 = 9, stated as area)
  - Leave the answer as two separate numbers ("5 and 4")
  - Count squares if any visual support is present, ignoring dimension labels
  - Fail to state the answer with a square unit
- **Why It Emerges in M7:** Without a full grid to count, students must infer the array. If they don't connect dimensions to multiplication, they lose the tool entirely.
- **Surfaces First:** M7 (Early, Mid phases); may persist into M8-M9 if not addressed

### **Success Indicator**
- **Early Phase:** Student sees partial grid and verbalizes: "I see 7 in a row. There are 4 rows. 4 times 7 is 28 square feet."
- **Mid Phase:** Student reads tick marks, builds equation (4 × 7), writes area with unit.
- **Late Phase:** Student sees dimensions only: "The rectangle is 6 meters long and 3 meters wide. 6 × 3 = 18 square meters."
- **EC/Exit Check:** Student solves text-only problem without visual: "A tile floor is 8 inches long and 5 inches wide. What is its area?" → "40 square inches."

### **Biggest Risk**
**Grid fading pace.** M7 must complete all three transitions (partial → tick marks → dimensions only) within a single module. If partial grid is extended too long, students may not reach dimensions-only reasoning. If fading is too fast, students may add or guess without understanding. The pacing MUST support the abstraction without scaffolding collapse.

## §1.2 SCOPE BOUNDARIES

### **MUST TEACH (Non-Negotiable)**

1. **Grid Fading Progression (Complete in M7)**
   - Warm-up: Full grid shown as reference for familiar rectangle (e.g., 4×6)
   - Early Phase: Partial grid (first row and first column filled; interior empty)
   - Mid Phase: Tick marks only (evenly spaced marks on top and left edges; no grid interior)
   - Late Phase: Dimensions only (rectangle outline with dimension labels; no grid, no tick marks)
   - **Critical:** All three transitions must complete within M7. No backsliding to M8.

2. **All Four Unit Types Across Phases**
   - Square feet (sq ft)
   - Square meters (sq m)
   - Square inches (sq in)
   - Square centimeters (sq cm)
   - **Requirement:** All four units must appear in Mid (tick marks) and Late (dimensions only) phases
   - Early (partial grid) uses square units generically (e.g., "square tiles")

3. **Text-Only Problems (Late Phase & Exit Check)**
   - Rigid sentence template: "A [object] is [number] [unit] long and [number] [unit] wide. What is its area?"
   - Examples:
     - "A carpet is 8 feet long and 5 feet wide. What is its area?"
     - "A tile floor is 6 meters long and 4 meters wide. What is its area?"
   - **No visual support** — student must extract dimensions from text and multiply
   - Introduction: Late Phase; extensive practice: EC (Exit Check)

4. **Side Length & Dimension Vocabulary (Introduced After Concrete Experience)**
   - **Side length:** How long one side of a rectangle is (not introduced before students have manipulated partial grids)
   - **Dimension:** A measurement; one of the factors in the area multiplication (length and width are the two dimensions)
   - **Vocabulary Teaching Notes:**
     - "Dimensions ARE the factors in multiplication."
     - Introduce after Early phase (partial grid experience), not before
     - Connect explicitly: "These two numbers are your dimensions. When you multiply them, you get the area."

### **MUST NOT INCLUDE (Hard Boundaries)**

1. **Perimeter Concept**
   - Even as a differentiation tool for the "add side lengths" misconception
   - Do not introduce perimeter language, formula, or concept
   - **Per Important Decision #5:** Misconception #5 is addressed through multiplication reasoning alone

2. **Composite Figures**
   - No L-shaped, T-shaped, or irregular figures
   - M7 is limited to simple rectangles in all four grid-fading states

3. **New Units**
   - All four unit types are from M5/M6
   - Do not introduce square kilometers, acres, or any new units in M7

4. **Ruler Measurement**
   - No student measurement activities with physical rulers
   - All dimensions are given or read from grid/tick marks
   - (Measurement tool skill is separate; M7 focuses on multiplication given dimensions)

## §1.3 VOCABULARY ARCHITECTURE

### **New Vocabulary (Introduced M7)**

#### **"Side Length"**
- **Definition:** How long one side of a rectangle is.
- **When Introduced:** After Early phase (partial grid experience). Students see rows and columns concretely, then introduce the label "side length."
- **Instructional Sequence:**
  1. Partial grid: "How many tiles are in one row?" (7) "How many rows are there?" (4)
  2. Mid phase: "We call the number of tiles in one row the side length across. We have 7 across and 4 down."
  3. Dimensions-only: "The side lengths are 7 and 4. We multiply them to find area."
- **Usage:** "The rectangle has side lengths of 6 feet and 3 feet."

#### **"Dimension"**
- **Definition:** A measurement; one of the factors in multiplication.
- **When Introduced:** Same timing as side length (after Early phase).
- **Instructional Sequence:**
  1. Early (after partial grid): "These two numbers tell us the dimensions of the rectangle."
  2. Mid: "The dimensions are 5 inches and 8 inches. To find area, we multiply the dimensions."
  3. Late: "What are the dimensions? 6 and 4. So the area is 6 × 4 = 24 square units."
- **Critical Connection:** "Dimensions ARE the factors in multiplication."
- **Usage:** "The rectangle has dimensions of 5 meters and 3 meters."

### **Reinforced Vocabulary (Taught in M5/M6, Consolidated M7)**

#### **Square Units (All Four)**
- Square feet (sq ft)
- Square meters (sq m)
- Square inches (sq in)
- Square centimeters (sq cm)
- **M7 Reinforcement:** Student must read and apply all four unit types correctly in Mid and Late phases. Emphasis: "Area is always measured in square units, not plain units."

#### **Area**
- The amount of space inside a rectangle, measured in square units
- Reinforced throughout M7 in all phases

#### **Multiply**
- The operation used to find area given dimensions
- **M7 Framing:** "When we know the side lengths, we multiply them to find the area. We don't count; we calculate."

#### **Expression** (from M4)
- A number sentence representing a calculation (e.g., 5 × 8, 4 × 7)
- **M7 Usage:** Equation Builder produces expressions; students read and complete them

#### **Factor** & **Product** (from M4)
- Factor: One of the numbers in a multiplication (e.g., in 5 × 8, both 5 and 8 are factors)
- Product: The result of multiplication (e.g., in 5 × 8 = 40, 40 is the product)
- **M7 Connection:** "The dimensions are the factors. The area is the product."

### **Vocabulary Staging by Phase**

| Phase | New Terms Introduced | Reinforced Terms | Notes |
|-------|----------------------|------------------|-------|
| **Warm-up** | (none) | area, multiply, square units | Familiar reference; reset expectations |
| **Early (Partial Grid)** | (none formally) | area, rows, columns, multiply | Concrete experience before terminology |
| **Early→Mid Transition** | side length, dimension | factors, product, all unit types | Introduce after concrete manipulation |
| **Mid (Tick Marks)** | (none) | side length, dimension, factor, product, unit types | Apply terms to abstract grid (ticks only) |
| **Late (Dimensions Only)** | (none) | side length, dimension, multiply, expression, unit types | Full abstract reasoning |
| **EC/Synthesis** | (none) | All vocabulary consolidated | Bridge to M8 reverse reasoning |

### **Terms to Avoid (Explicitly NOT Used in M7)**

1. **"Formula"** (standalone)
   - Avoid: "Use the formula length × width."
   - Instead: "Multiply the dimensions to find area."
   - Reasoning: "Formula" suggests rote memorization. M7 is about reasoning why multiplication works. M8 develops fluency; formula language acceptable post-M7.

2. **"Perimeter"**
   - Do not use, even for contrast or clarification
   - If student adds side lengths, address via: "We multiply to find area, not add."

3. **"Decompose"**
   - M7 does not decompose rectangles or use "break apart" strategies
   - Composite figures and decomposition appear in later units

## §1.4 MISCONCEPTIONS

### **PRIMARY MISCONCEPTION: #5 Not Multiplying Side Lengths**

**Severity:** HIGH | **Frequency:** HIGH | **When Surfaces:** M7 Early–Mid; persists M8–M9 if unaddressed

#### **What It Is**
Students read side length dimensions correctly but fail to connect them to multiplication. Instead, they:
- **Add** the dimensions ("5 + 4 = 9")
- **State as two separate numbers** without calculating ("The area is 5 and 4")
- **Count squares** when any partial grid is visible, ignoring dimension labels
- **Leave answer without unit** ("9" instead of "9 square feet")

#### **Why It Emerges in M7**
- In M6, students had full grids to count. Dimensions were labels on the grid, not the primary tool.
- In M7, grid support disappears. Dimensions become the ONLY information available.
- If students don't construct the connection: "I see the dimensions 4 and 7. That means 4 rows of 7 squares. So I multiply: 4 × 7 = 28," they have no fallback tool.
- The abstraction from "visible array" to "inferred array" requires this connection to hold.

#### **Observable Trigger Behaviors**
- Student reads tick marks or dimension labels: "6 and 5"
- When asked, "What is the area?" student responds:
  - "11" (addition)
  - "6 and 5" (repeats as two numbers)
  - "30" (correct, but if asked HOW they got it, cannot explain multiplication connection)
  - No unit stated

#### **Remediation Path**
1. **Immediate (During Early Phase):** Return to partial grid. "How many are in one row?" (count concretely) "How many rows?" (count concretely) "So if there are 7 in a row and 4 rows, how many altogether?" (guide to 4 × 7, not 4 + 7)
2. **Deepening (Mid Phase):** Partial grid → Tick marks (same rectangle). "We don't see all the squares now, but we still have 7 across and 4 down. The area is still the same. 4 × 7 = 28."
3. **Guide Reveal (Late Phase):** If student cannot solve dimensions-only rectangle, guide can activate grid overlay temporarily. "What do you see now? Now multiply the dimensions." (2+ reveals flag M3 reinforcement.)
4. **Vocabulary Anchor:** "The dimensions are your factors. Multiply them to find the product, which is the area."

#### **How M7 Instruction Targets This**
- **Early Phase (Partial Grid):** Concrete row/column count makes the structure visible. Student sees "I count 7 in the row, 4 rows down. 4 groups of 7. Multiply: 4 × 7."
- **Mid Phase (Tick Marks):** Grid is gone, but marks remain as a bridge. Equation Builder requires student to build the multiplication expression explicitly.
- **Late Phase (Dimensions Only):** Student must infer from dimensions alone. Equation Builder (with unit labels) forces the multiplication action.

### **SECONDARY MISCONCEPTION: #8 Square Unit Confusion**

**Severity:** MEDIUM | **Frequency:** MEDIUM | **When Surfaces:** M6–M7; peaks in M7 (mid-late phases)

#### **What It Is**
Without grids, students lose visual anchor for "square" units. They may:
- State area without a unit ("28")
- Use a linear unit instead ("28 feet" instead of "28 square feet")
- Use incorrect unit ("28 centimeters" when problem is in square centimeters)
- State two separate units for two dimensions ("5 feet and 4 feet" instead of "20 square feet")

#### **Why It Emerges in M7**
- Full grid in M6 showed squares visually. Each cell was obviously a square unit.
- In M7 Mid and Late, there is no visual grid. The word "square" is not as concrete.
- Students may remember "we measure area" but forget that area is always in *square* units, derived from multiplying two linear measurements.

#### **Observable Trigger Behaviors**
- Student calculates correctly: "5 × 4 = 20" but writes:
  - "20" (no unit)
  - "20 feet" (linear unit, not square)
  - "5 feet and 4 feet" (repeats input units separately)
- Student reads "6 square meters" and "3 square meters" but writes "18 meters" or "18 feet"

#### **Remediation Path**
1. **Vocabulary Anchor:** "Area is always in SQUARE units. When we multiply two measurements, we get a square unit."
2. **Explicit Labeling (Mid Phase):** Equation Builder requires unit labels. "6 × 4 = 24 square feet" is written, not stated.
3. **Reinforcement (Late Phase):** Text-only problems must be answered with full unit: "A garden is 7 feet long and 5 feet wide. Area is 35 **square feet**, not 35 feet."
4. **Guide Reminder:** If student omits unit, guide prompts: "What are we measuring? Area. Area is always in what kind of unit? Square units. So the answer is __ square __."

#### **How M7 Instruction Targets This**
- Mid Phase: All Equation Builder solutions include unit labels (e.g., "6 sq m").
- Late Phase: Text-only problems require full unit in answer.
- Synthesis: "Area is a multiplication of two dimensions. That's why it's always SQUARE units."

## §1.5 TOY SPECIFICATIONS

### **PRIMARY TOY 1: GRID RECTANGLES**

#### **Notion Link**
https://www.notion.so/ocpgg/Grid-Rectangles-2fb5917eac528035a19dc2b5b49aeeca

#### **Core Function**
Displays rectangles in four visual states to support grid fading: full grid (reference), partial grid, tick marks, and dimensions only. Primary tool for all phases of M7.

#### **Display Modes & When Used**

| Mode | Visual | Phases | Example |
|------|--------|--------|---------|
| **Full Grid** | All squares visible, grid lines clear | Warm-up only | 4×6 rectangle, all 24 squares shown, labeled "4 rows of 6 tiles" |
| **Partial Grid** | First row fully tiled, first column fully tiled, interior empty | Early Phase | 7 across (1st row filled), 4 down (1st column filled), rest white |
| **Tick Marks** | Evenly spaced marks along top and left edges; no interior grid or fill | Mid Phase | Top edge marked: 0, 1, 2, ..., 7. Left edge marked: 0, 1, 2, 3, 4. Rectangle outline only. |
| **Dimensions Only** | Rectangle outline with dimension labels only (e.g., "6 m" on top, "3 m" on left); no grid, no marks | Late Phase | Simple rectangle outline. Top labeled "6 meters." Left labeled "3 meters." |

#### **Key Pedagogical Transitions (Animated)**
- **Warm-up → Early:** Show same 4×6 rectangle with full grid, then fade grid interior (keep outline), show partial. Message: "The area didn't change. We just can't see all the squares."
- **Early → Mid:** Same rectangle with partial grid, then fade partial grid lines, leave tick marks. Message: "The numbers along the edges tell us what the grid would have looked like."
- **Mid → Late:** Same rectangle with tick marks, then fade tick marks, keep dimension labels. Message: "The number already tells us what the tick marks showed."

#### **Guide Reveal (Remediation, Not Visible to Student)**

**Trigger:** Student encounters Late Phase (dimensions-only) rectangle and cannot solve.

**Guide Action:**
1. First reveal: Overlay partial grid on top of dimensions-only rectangle temporarily.
2. Second reveal: Overlay full grid.
3. Logging: If ≥2 reveals used, flag student for M3 reinforcement review in guide notes.

**Constraints:**
- Reveal is for guide only; student does not see it unless guide explicitly activates
- Reveal is temporary and pedagogically framed: "Let's see the grid. Now, what do the dimensions tell us about the grid?"
- Purpose: Diagnostic (does student need M3 review?) and supportive (grid is a fallback, not the expectation)

### **PRIMARY TOY 2: EQUATION BUILDER**

#### **Notion Link**
In development

#### **Core Function**
Supports student construction of area multiplication expressions. Requires explicit connection between dimensions and multiplication. Used in Early (partial grid, unlabeled slots per TVP), Mid (tick marks, labeled with units), and Late (dimensions only, independent expression building) phases.

#### **Configuration by Phase**

| Phase | Equation Template | Factor Slots | Unlabeled or Labeled? | Unit Labels | Example |
|-------|------------------|--------------|----------------------|-------------|---------|
| **Early** | __ × __ = __ | 2 input (dimensions read from partial grid), 1 output (product) | Unlabeled (per TVP: "Equation Builder, multiplication, unlabeled slots") | No unit labels | "4 × 7 = 28 square tiles" (students build expression from partial grid count) |
| **Mid (Tick Marks)** | __ × __ = __ | 2 input (dimensions read from ticks), 1 output (product) | Labeled | Yes; required | "7 × 4 = 28 square meters" (unit labels shown for both factors and product) |
| **Late (Dims Only)** | No pre-structured template (per TVP: "No scaffolding") | Student builds full equation independently | N/A — no pre-labeled slots | Unit labels required in student's answer but NOT scaffolded by the tool | "6 × 3 = 18 square feet" — student writes/constructs independently. **Note:** "No scaffolding" per TVP means the Equation Builder's slot template disappears; students express the full equation on their own. Unit labels remain REQUIRED in the answer (misconception #8 targeting) but are not pre-populated. |
| **EC (Text-Only)** | Written equation within text response | Drag-to-construct or drag-to-check | Labeled | Yes; required | "A tile is 8 inches by 5 inches. [8 × 5 = 40 square inches]" |

#### **Interaction Model: Drag-to-Place**
- **Per Important Decision #6:** Dragging preferred over tapping
- Student drags dimension values (read from ticks or labels) into factor slots
- Student sees product calculated automatically OR student drags answer into product slot
- All unit labels are visible and required for completion
- Equation Builder confirms: "Yes, 6 × 3 = 18 square meters."

#### **Unit Label Display**
- Mid phase and later: All unit labels shown in Equation Builder
- Student sees: "6 [sq m] × 4 [sq m] = 24 [sq m]" or similar
- This reinforces that dimensions are in linear units (though still labeled with the space unit being counted) and area is in square units
- **Alternate Format:** "6 × 4 = 24 square meters" (unit name once at end)

#### **Progression (When Each Comes Online)**

1. **Warm-up:** No Equation Builder (reference only)
2. **Early:** **Equation Builder online with unlabeled slots (per TVP).** Student reads row/column count from partial grid, drags values into factor slots, sees product. No unit labels on the Equation Builder — generic "square tiles" for now.
3. **Mid (Tick Marks):** **Full Equation Builder online.** Student drag-places factors from ticks, Equation Builder displays equation and product. Unit labels required.
4. **Late (Dimensions Only):** **No Equation Builder template (per TVP: "No scaffolding").** Student reads dimensions from labels, independently constructs the full equation (no pre-structured slots). Unit labels required in student's answer but not tool-scaffolded.
5. **EC (Text-Only):** **Equation Builder embedded in text.** "A garden is 7 feet by 4 feet. [7 × 4 = __ sq ft]" Student fills product slot and unit label.
