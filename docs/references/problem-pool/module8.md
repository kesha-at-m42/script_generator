# MODULE 8 PATH B (SINGAPORE) â€” GOAL DECOMPOSITION

---

## LEARNING GOALS

**L1 (Verbatim):** "Identify equivalent fractions; two fractions are equivalent if they are the same size and refer to the same whole."

**L2 (Verbatim):** "Generate and justify simple equivalent fractions using visual models."

**Module Focus:** VISUAL PROOF of equivalence. Students recognize equivalent fractions by comparing same-sized bars, verify equivalence on number lines ("same point"), and create simple equivalents by shading. Vocabulary ("equivalent") is introduced early. The Same Whole requirement is explicitly taught.

---

## MASTERY ASSUMPTION

**Students entering M8 have MASTERED (from M7):**

- Recognizing that b/b \= 1 (numerator equals denominator means one whole) âœ“
- Explaining WHY b/b \= 1 using "all parts" reasoning âœ“
- Writing whole numbers as fractions with any denominator âœ“
- Placing points on 0-2 number lines fluently âœ“
- Connecting patterns across representations âœ“

**Therefore, M8 Practice does NOT need standalone templates for:**

- ~~Basic number line placement~~ (mastered in M5-M7)
- ~~Recognizing b/b \= 1~~ (M7 content)
- ~~Explaining why 4/4 \= 1~~ (M7 content)

**M8 Practice focuses on what's GENUINELY NEW:**

1. **Conceptual:** Recognizing equivalence between DIFFERENT fractions (1/2 \= 2/4), not just b/b \= 1
2. **Conceptual:** Understanding the Same Whole requirement
3. **Procedural:** Creating equivalent fractions by shading (new `paint` action)
4. **Transfer:** Verifying equivalence on number lines; applying to fractions \> 1

## COMPONENT A: CONCEPTUAL (35-40%)

*What students UNDERSTAND â€” the concept of equivalence*

---

### M8-01: Recognize that equivalent fractions show the same amount of the same-sized whole

**Builds on:** M7-01 (recognize b/b \= 1); M7-08 (connect across representations) **What's New:** Generalizing from "b/b \= 1" to "different fractions can be equal" (1/2 \= 2/4)

**Lesson Alignment:** Step 1 â€” "Check if they show the same amount or different amounts"; Step 2 â€” "Equivalent means equal"

**What It Looks Like:** Student compares two fraction bars and determines whether they show the same amount; selects "Same" or "Different."

**Verbs:** identify **Tiers:** confidence, support, baseline **Templates:** 8001, 8002

---

### M8-02: Understand that equivalence requires the SAME WHOLE

**Builds on:** NEW â€” not taught in M7 **What's New:** Critical constraint â€” fractions can only be equivalent when comparing same-sized wholes

**Lesson Alignment:** Step 6 â€” "1/2 of a granola bar and 2/4 of a school bus aren't equal because the wholes are different sizes."

**What It Looks Like:** Student identifies that comparisons require same-sized wholes; rejects equivalence claims when wholes differ.

**Verbs:** identify **Tiers:** support, baseline, stretch **Templates:** 8003

---

### M8-03: Explain WHY fractions are equivalent using "same amount" reasoning

**Builds on:** M7-02 (explain WHY b/b \= 1\) **What's New:** Extending explanation from "all parts \= whole" to "same total amount shaded"

**Lesson Alignment:** Step 8 â€” "The pieces get smaller, so we shade more pieces, but the total amount stays the same."

**What It Looks Like:** Student selects correct explanation for why 1/2 \= 2/4 (same amount shaded, different number of pieces).

**Verbs:** identify **Tiers:** baseline, stretch **Templates:** 8004

## COMPONENT B: PROCEDURAL (30-35%)

*What students DO â€” executing recognition, verification, and creation*

---

### M8-04: Select equivalent fraction bar from a set of options

**Builds on:** M7-01 (pattern recognition from sets) **What's New:** Selecting equivalent from multiple bars (not just identifying b/b \= 1\)

**Lesson Alignment:** Step 4 â€” "Which bar below shows an equivalent fractionâ€”the same amount as 1/2?"

**What It Looks Like:** Given a reference bar (e.g., 1/2), student clicks the bar that shows an equivalent fraction from 2-3 options.

**Verbs:** identify **Tiers:** confidence, support, baseline **Templates:** 8005, 8006

---

### M8-05: Place point on number line to verify equivalence

**Builds on:** M5-M7 number line fluency **What's New:** Using number line as PROOF tool â€” "same point means equivalent"

**Lesson Alignment:** Steps 10-11 â€” Place 1/2, then place 2/4 and see they land at the same point

**What It Looks Like:** Student places marker for a fraction on a number line; confirms it lands at the same position as an equivalent fraction.

**Verbs:** create **Tiers:** support, baseline, stretch **Templates:** 8007, 8008

---

### M8-06: Shade parts to create equivalent fraction

**Builds on:** NEW â€” `paint` action introduced in M8 **What's New:** GENERATION skill â€” student creates the equivalent, not just identifies it

**Lesson Alignment:** Step 7 â€” "How many eighths would equal one half? Click on parts to shade them."; Step 16 â€” "Shade to keep the same amount as two thirds."

**What It Looks Like:** Given a reference fraction (e.g., 1/2 bar), student shades an empty bar to create the equivalent (e.g., shade 4 of 8 parts for 4/8).

**Verbs:** create **Tiers:** baseline, stretch, challenge **Templates:** 8009, 8010

**Lesson Alignment:** Skill transfer â€” Lesson uses 0-1 lines; this extends to 0-2 range from M6-M7

**What It Looks Like:** Student verifies that 3/2 and 6/4 land at the same point on a 0-2 number line, demonstrating equivalence works past 1\.

**Verbs:** create **Tiers:** stretch, challenge **Templates:** 8012
