# **MODULE 12 PATH B (Singapore)**

## **LEARNING GOAL(S) (VERBATIM):**

Compare two fractions with different numerators and different denominators using benchmarks and justify symbolically

## **CORE CONCEPTS:**

* Benchmark comparison strategy (using 1/2 and 1 as reference points)
* Symbolic justification of comparisons
* Decision framework for strategy selection
* Capstone synthesis of all Grade 3 fraction concepts

## **STANDARDS: Building On:**

* Module 11 comparison strategies (same denominator, same numerator)
* Modules 1-10 fraction foundations (recognition, equivalence, number line placement)

**DESIGN NOTE:** Module 12 intentionally extends Grade 3 comparison work into Grade 4 benchmark strategies using grade-appropriate visual scaffolds. This completes a 3-module progression and serves as enrichment/acceleration content. Adaptive routing should account for student readiness.

## **Addressing:**

* 3.NF.A.3d: Compare two fractions with different numerators and different denominators

## **Building Toward:**

* Grade 4 fraction operations
* Advanced fraction reasoning

## **VOCABULARY STAGING SEQUENCE:**

1. Benchmark (Lesson Part 1 \- after discovery demonstration)
2. Justifying symbolically (Lesson Part 1 \- after first symbolic example)
3. Reference point (Lesson Part 1 \- reinforcement of benchmark concept)
4. Distance from 1 (Lesson Part 2 \- when comparing fractions both above 1/2)

## **MISCONCEPTIONS TO ADDRESS:**

* **\#8 \- Distance from Benchmark:** Student struggles to determine which fraction is closer to a benchmark (addressed throughout)
* **\#9 \- Symbol Direction:** Student confuses \< and \> symbols when writing comparisons (Exit Check \#2, Practice)
* **\#10 \- Multiple Fraction Comparison:** Student can't systematically compare more than two fractions (Practice \#6, Exit Check \#3)

**⚫ Guide:** "\[Transfer\_Thinking\] You've become pretty good at comparing fractions. When denominators match, you count the pieces. When numerators match, you compare piece sizes. Those strategies have worked every time."

**⚫ Guide:** "But here's a tougher challenge. What happens when NEITHER the numerators NOR the denominators match? Let's see."
