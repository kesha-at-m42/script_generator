## **MODULE 6 PATH B (Singapore) 10.08.25** **LEARNING GOALS (VERBATIM):**

"Locate non-unit fractions greater than 1 on number lines; connect iteration and distance."

### **CORE CONCEPTS:**

* Fractions can extend beyond one whole
* Denominators remain constant regardless of position on number line
* Systematic iteration creates improper fractions
* Every set of denominator-equal parts creates another whole

### **STANDARDS:**

**Building On:**

* 3.NF.A.2a (Fractions on number lines 0-1)

**Addressing:**

* 3.NF.A.2b (Fractions greater than 1 on number lines)

**Building Toward:**

* 3.NF.A.3a (Comparing fractions \- future module)

### **VOCABULARY STAGING SEQUENCE:**

1. Visual experience with fractions beyond 1 (no terms)
2. Pattern recognition of continued iteration
3. "Improper fraction" introduced after 5/4 discovery
4. Connection to "greater than one whole"

### **MISCONCEPTIONS TO ADDRESS:**

\#3 \- Denominator Changes Past 1: Students think denominator should change when passing 1 (e.g., 5/5 instead of 5/4)

\#4 \- Unequal Spacing: Students don't maintain consistent spacing when placing fractions
\#6 \- Numerator/Denominator Reversal: Students confuse which number represents parts vs whole

# **PRACTICE PHASE**

**Duration:** 10-15 minutes **Learning Goal:** "Locate non-unit fractions greater than 1 on number lines; connect iteration and distance." **Primary Tool:** Extended Number Lines

## **LEARNING GOAL DECOMPOSITION**

**COMPONENT A: Placing Improper Fractions (Core Skill)**

* What: Students must accurately PLACE fractions beyond 1
* Cognitive Type: CREATE
* Weight: 35%
* Minimum Success: 75%
* Problems Testing This: 1, 2, 3, 8A

**COMPONENT B: Understanding Position & Relationships**

* What: Students must UNDERSTAND where fractions go and why
* Cognitive Type: IDENTIFY only (no comparison of unlike fractions)
* Weight: 25%
* Minimum Success: 60%
* Problems Testing This: 4, 5

**COMPONENT C: Application & Transfer**

* What: Students must USE the concept in varied contexts
* Cognitive Type: APPLY/SYNTHESIS
* Weight: 40%
* Minimum Success: At least 1 must pass
* Problems Testing This: 6, 7, 8B

**CRITICAL GATES:** ☐ Must pass at least 1 APPLY problem (Component C) ☐ Cannot have spacing errors (\#4) more than 3 times ☐ Must demonstrate understanding that denominator doesn't change past 1
