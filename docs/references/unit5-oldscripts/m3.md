### **Addressing Misconception: Numerator-Denominator Dependency**

🔵 Visual: \[CLEAR\] Previous bars fade. New horizontal Shaded Rectangle Bar fades in, divided into 3 equal parts, all empty. Bar is 6cm wide, 2cm tall.

⚫ Guide: "Let me show you something important about how fractions work. This bar has 3 equal parts total."

🔵 Visual: \[MODIFY\] The number "3" appears below the bar with label "total parts"

⚫ Guide: "Now watch what happens if someone asks me to shade 5 parts to make five-thirds..."

🔵 Visual: \[MODIFY\] First three parts shade normally (left to right), then a red dotted outline appears where a 4th and 5th part would go, but extends beyond the bar's boundary. Red X appears over the dotted sections.

⚫ Guide: "I can't do it\! There are only 3 parts in this whole bar. The numerator \- that top number \- can never be bigger than the actual parts that exist. The numerator DEPENDS on how many parts we have to work with."

🔵 Visual: \[MODIFY\] Equation appears: "5/3 \= ?" with the "5" in red, then morphs to "3/3 \= 1 whole" with arrow showing correction

⚫ Guide: "So if this bar has 3 parts total, the most I can shade is 3 parts, making 3/3, which equals one whole. The top number and bottom number work together \- they're not independent\!"

*\[Pause: 2 seconds\]*

⚫ Guide: "Let's try one more to make sure this is clear."

🔵 Visual: \[CLEAR\] New bar appears with 4 equal parts, all empty

⚫ Guide: "This bar has 4 parts. Can I make 6/4?"

🔵 Visual: \[MODIFY\] Attempts to shade beyond the bar with red dotted outlines for parts 5 and 6, with red X

⚫ Guide: "No\! With 4 parts total, the biggest fraction I can make is 4/4. The numerator depends on what's actually there to shade. This is why fractions make sense \- you can't take more pieces than what exists\!"
