## **1.0 THE ONE THING**

**Students must understand that graphs are organized ways to show data, and that every part of a graph (title, categories, key, scale) tells us something specific we need to read accurately.**

**Critical Misconception:** Graph as Picture (\#16) — Students treat graphs as literal drawings rather than data representations, missing that each element carries specific meaning.

**Success Indicator:** Student correctly answers "how many more/fewer" questions by reading graph values and subtracting (not by visual estimation or treating the graph as a picture).

**Biggest Risk:** Students who don't solidify 1:1 scale understanding will struggle when M2 introduces scaled graphs where each symbol represents multiple items.

## **1.1 LEARNING GOALS**

*Verbatim from OUR Curriculum — Script Must Achieve These*

**L1:** "Interpret picture graphs and bar graphs to generate questions about the data."

**L2:** "Represent data using bar graphs and picture graphs. Solve one- and two-step problems using addition and subtraction with information from bar graphs."

**Module Goal (Student-Facing):** "You'll learn to read graphs —finding patterns, comparing categories, and answering questions about what the data shows."

**Exit Check Tests:**

* Can student read values from picture graphs and bar graphs (1:1 scale)?
* Can student identify "most," "least," and compare categories?
* Can student solve "how many more/fewer" problems using graph data?
* Can student solve "in all" problems using graph data?

### **1.1.1 Standards Cascade**

| Building On | 2.MD.D.10: Draw a picture graph and bar graph (single-unit scale) to represent data with up to four categories. Solve simple put-together, take-apart, and compare problems using bar graph information. |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Addressing** | 3.MD.B: Represent and interpret data. |
| **Building Toward** | 3.MD.B.3: Draw scaled picture graphs and scaled bar graphs to represent data. Solve one- and two-step "how many more" and "how many fewer" problems using scaled bar graphs. |

### **1.1.2 Module Bridges**

**From Grade 2:** Students created and interpreted single-unit picture graphs and bar graphs. They solved simple comparison problems. However, Grade 2 work was often collaborative (class graphs, physical manipulatives) and may not have emphasized independent interpretation or the specific language of "how many more/fewer."

**This Module:** Students activate and consolidate Grade 2 knowledge through:

* Creating ONE personal graph (Warmup) to establish ownership
* Interpreting multiple pre-made graphs (Lesson) to build reading fluency
* Explicitly learning that "how many more/fewer" means subtract
* Practicing with both horizontal and vertical orientations

**To Module 2:** Students will apply their solid 1:1 understanding when M2 introduces the critical concept: each symbol can represent MORE than one item (scaled graphs with 1:2 scale). The "how many more/fewer" language established here carries forward.

## **1.2 SCOPE BOUNDARIES**

### **✅ Must Teach**

* Reading picture graphs (1:1 scale, both orientations)
* Reading bar graphs (1:1 scale, both orientations)
* Graph components: title, categories, key, scale, labels
* Comparison language: "most," "least," "fewer," "fewest"
* Problem-solving language: "how many more," "how many fewer," "in all," "total"
* Explicit instruction: "how many more/fewer" \= compare \= subtract
* Explicit instruction: "in all" \= total \= add
* Creating ONE picture graph with personal data (Warmup only)
* Orientation vocabulary: "horizontal," "vertical"

### **❌ Must Not Include**

* Scaled graphs where each symbol \> 1 (Module 2\)
* Half-symbols or fractional representations (Module 2\)
* Scale selection or scale comparison (Module 5\)
* Line plots (separate unit)
* Data collection methods beyond the provided game
* Independent graph creation in Lesson (M2+ skill)

⚠️ **DIGITAL ADAPTATION PRINCIPLE:** Graph creation is limited to Warmup (ownership/engagement). Lesson focuses on interpretation of pre-made graphs. Full independent creation develops across M2-M6.

⚠️ **TWO-STEP PROBLEM APPROACH:** In M1, two-step problems are structured as sequential single-step submissions rather than requiring students to hold multiple operations mentally. Full two-step complexity develops across M2-M6 with scaled graphs.

## **1.4 MISCONCEPTIONS**

### **1.4.1 Graph as Picture (PRIMARY) — Misconception \#16**

**Trigger Behavior:** Student focuses on visual appearance of graph rather than data values. May describe graph shapes ("it goes up") rather than data meaning ("more people chose cats").

**Why It Happens:** Young students are accustomed to pictures representing objects directly. The abstraction that a graph REPRESENTS data requires explicit instruction.

**Prevention Strategy:**

* Explicitly name graph components and their PURPOSE
* Ask "What does this graph TELL us?" not "What do you SEE?"
* Model reading the key
* Use varied symbols to emphasize that the COUNT matters, not the symbol type

### **1.4.2 All Data Must Be Used (SECONDARY) — Misconception \#17**

**Trigger Behavior:** When asked "How many more chose cats than dogs?", student incorporates ALL categories into their answer.

**Why It Happens:** Students have learned that "using the information" often means using ALL the information.

**Prevention Strategy:**

* Model question analysis: identify which categories a question requires BEFORE solving
* Include questions that deliberately DON'T use all categories
* Visually dim irrelevant categories during remediation

## **1.5 TOY SPECIFICATIONS**

### **1.5.1 Picture Graphs — PRIMARY TOY**

**Purpose:** Primary visual representation for M1. Used in two modes: Creating (Warmup — students place symbols to build graph from their collected data) and Reading (Lesson, Exit Check, Synthesis — students interpret pre-made graphs). Establishes the 1:1 scale foundation that M2 will disrupt with "each symbol \= MORE than 1."

**Notion Spec:** [Picture Graphs](https://www.notion.so/ocpgg/Picture-Graphs-2fc5917eac52809fb9d0e501f3547ff0)

#### Core Specifications (M1)

| Aspect | Specification |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Mode 2: Creating** | Warmup only. Student clicks to place symbols in graph cells. Horizontal orientation. 3 categories (Guide models category A, student completes B and C). |
| **Mode 1: Reading** | Lesson, Exit Check, Synthesis. Pre-made graphs. Student hovers (values display), clicks category (highlight), clicks two categories (compare — difference displays). |
| **Scale** | 1:1 only (each symbol \= 1 item). Key reads "Each \[symbol\] \= 1 \[item\]." |
| **Orientations** | Both horizontal and vertical. Horizontal introduced first (Warmup creation, Lesson 1.1–1.3). Vertical introduced in Lesson 1.4. Both used interchangeably from that point forward. |
| **Categories** | Warmup: 3 categories. Lesson/EC: 3–4 per graph. Synthesis: 3–5. |
| **Values** | Warmup: 3–7 per category. Lesson/EC: 3–10 per category. |
| **Symbol** | System-assigned (e.g., stars). ONE symbol type for the entire graph (not per category). Displayed in key area before graph creation begins. No student selection. |
| **Component Highlighting** | Title, category labels, and key are individually selectable/highlightable by the system. Used in Lesson 1.3 for component identification task. |

#### M1-Specific Constraints

| DO | DO NOT |
| :---- | :---- |
| Use 1:1 scale exclusively | Use any scale \> 1 (M2+) |
| Show both orientations | Default to only one orientation |
| Use ONE symbol type per graph across all categories | Use different symbols per category |
| Allow student creation in Warmup only | Have students create graphs in Lesson or EC |
| Include key on every graph showing "Each \[symbol\] \= 1" | Omit key (even though scale is 1 — key establishes the convention) |
| System-assign symbol before graph creation | Offer symbol palette for student selection |

---

### **1.5.2 Bar Graphs**

**Purpose:** Introduced in Lesson Section 2 as a second representation format. Students learn that bar graphs show the same information as picture graphs using bars instead of symbols. Reading only in M1 — creation begins in M4.

**Notion Spec:** [Bar Graphs](https://www.notion.so/ocpgg/Bar-Graphs-2fc5917eac5280ddbd98fdc566e19243)

#### Core Specifications (M1)

| Aspect | Specification |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Mode** | Mode 1: Reading only. No creation. |
| **Scale** | 1:1 only (axis counts by 1s). |
| **Orientations** | Both vertical (bars extend up — introduced first in 2.1) and horizontal (bars extend right — introduced in 2.3). |
| **Categories** | 3–4 per graph. |
| **Values** | 3–10 per category (matching Lesson range). |
| **Axis Markings** | Tick marks at each whole number. Labels on every tick. |
| **Interaction** | Click bar to highlight. Click two bars to compare (difference displays). |
| **Helping Line Animation** | System-triggered. On correct answer or during guided instruction: system draws line from bar endpoint perpendicular to value axis, then highlights axis value. Not available on-demand. |

#### M1-Specific Constraints

| DO | DO NOT |
| :---- | :---- |
| Use axis scaled by 1s only | Use any scale other than 1:1 |
| Show both orientations (vertical first, then horizontal) | Introduce axis vocabulary formally ("axis/axes" — that's M4) |
| Explicitly connect bar height/length to the value it represents | Assume students know how to read bar graphs |
| Show same data as picture graph for format comparison (Section 2 transition) | Have students create bar graphs (M4+) |
| Use system-triggered helping line animation for first bar-reading interaction | Show bars at non-integer heights (M4+) |

### **1.5.3 Data Tables**

**Purpose:** Numeric data display that appears alongside graphs. Reinforces that graphs SHOW the same information as tables — "same information, different format." Data Tables are display-only in M1; they are not interactive. Cross-toy highlighting connections (e.g., hovering a table row highlights the corresponding graph category) are handled through app-controlled animations, not student interaction.

**Notion Spec:** [Data Tables](https://www.notion.so/ocpgg/Data-Tables-2fc5917eac528088bfa0d88a8761ea4c)

#### Core Specifications (M1)

| Aspect | Specification |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Mode** | Display only. Not clickable. |
| **Structure** | 2-column: Category name |
| **Rows** | 3–4 categories (matching graph categories). |
| **Cross-Toy Highlighting** | App-controlled animation. System can highlight a table row and corresponding graph element simultaneously. Not student-triggered. |
| **Paired With** | Picture Graphs or Bar Graphs — displayed adjacent to the corresponding graph when visible. |

#### M1-Specific Constraints

| DO | DO NOT |
| :---- | :---- |
| Display alongside graphs when specified in Visual: lines | Assume Data Table is always visible — check each interaction |
| Match table categories and values exactly to adjacent graph | Show different data in table vs. graph |
| Use app-controlled animations for cross-toy connections | Make table rows clickable or hoverable by students |

#### M1 Visibility Rule

Data Table visibility must be specified in each interaction's Visual: line. General pattern for M1:

- **Warmup:** Data visible as reference during graph creation (W.2, W.3)
- **Lesson Sections 1–2:** Visible alongside graphs during teaching (reinforces data ↔ visual connection)
- **Lesson Section 3:** NOT visible during problem-solving (students must read from graph alone)
- **Exit Check:** NOT visible (assessment of graph reading, not table reading)
- **Synthesis S.1:** Visible (format comparison task). S.2–S.3: Not visible.

### **1.5.4 Data Collection Game**

**Purpose:** Generates personalized data for students to graph in Warmup. Students count items in an animated Minis scenario, producing 2–3 category counts that feed into Picture Graph creation. Creates investment and ownership before Lesson shifts to interpreting others' graphs.

**Notion Spec:** None currently. Spec in development.

#### Core Specifications (M1)

| Aspect | Specification |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Context** | Minis characters in animated counting scenario. |
| **Categories** | 2 categories counted (feeds into 3-category graph with Guide-modeled first category). |
| **Value Range** | 3–7 per category. |
| **Output** | Category counts passed to Picture Graphs (Mode 2: Creating) for Warmup graph creation. |

#### M1-Specific Constraints

| DO | DO NOT |
| :---- | :---- |
| Generate values in 3–7 range | Generate values outside range or equal values across categories |
| Use Minis characters for engagement | Use abstract or decontextualized counting |
| Pass data seamlessly to graph creation | Require students to remember/re-enter counts manually |

---

### **Interaction Constraints (All Toys)**

* NO verbal/spoken student responses — Guide speaks, student acts
* NO keyboard/text input — all responses via click/tap/drag
* NO open-ended questions requiring typed answers — use selection or action tasks
* Questions in Guide speech must be either rhetorical (Guide answers) or answered through on-screen action

## **1.6 WARMUP (3-4 minutes)**

**Detail Level:** Structure \+ parameters (AI writes dialogue)

### **Purpose**

Activate prior knowledge of data collection and graphing from Grade 2\. Build ownership by having students create a graph with THEIR collected data before the Lesson focuses on interpretation.

### **Parameters**

| Element | Specification |
| :---- | :---- |
| Interactions | 3 meaningful interactions \+ bridge |
| Format | Data Collection Game → Symbol Introduction → Graph Creation |
| Cognitive Load | 20-30% (light, accessible) |
| Remediation | Light only |
| Tone | Medium-High energy, playful, inviting |
| Engagement Anchors | Narrative Setup (Minis), Personalization (their data), Anticipation (graph about to be built) |

### **Constraints**

| USE | AVOID |
| :---- | :---- |
| Informal language ("Let's count," "Check this out") | Formal phrasing ("Observe the following") |
| Observable acknowledgments ("You counted 5") | Assumed states ("You thought carefully") |
| 2 categories only | 3+ categories |
| Values 3-7 per category | Values outside range |

---

### **Warmup Type Rationale**

**Standard Playbook Types:** Binary Choice, WODB, Number Talk, Notice & Wonder, Estimation 180, Visual Patterns, Mystery Reveal

**M1 Deviation:** Data Collection Game \+ Graph Creation

**Why this serves the concept:**

- Unit 1 is about DATA—students need to experience collecting data before interpreting graphs
- OUR classroom version has students gather class data collaboratively; Data Collection Game provides equivalent "their data" experience in digital 1:1 format
- Graph creation in Warmup (not Lesson) creates ownership and investment
- Follows Playbook PRINCIPLES (low cognitive load, engagement anchors, judgment tasks, bridge) while adapting STRUCTURE for data unit context

**Test:** If we removed this Warmup, would students lose mathematical preparation for Lesson? YES—they would miss the foundational experience that graphs represent real collected information, not just pictures.

---

**Interaction W.1: Data Collection Game**

* **Purpose:** Generate personalized data. Create investment through active participation and Minis context.
* **Hook (first 15-20 seconds):**
  * **Visual:** Minis characters appear in animated counting scenario.
  * **Guide:** Brief framing for counting task.
* **Engagement Anchor:** Narrative Setup (Minis context creates investment)
* **Game Specifications (for script writer):**

| Element | Specification |
| :---- | :---- |
| Style | Mario Party-style counting game |
| Categories | 3 (design determines what's being counted) |
| Values | 3-7 per category |
| Duration | \~60 seconds |
| Student Interaction | Up/down counter to track count |

* **Task:** Student participates in counting activity, using up/down counter to record their count for each category.
* **On Complete \- Guide Reveal:**
  * **Guide:** "Let's see if you counted them all."
  * Guide counts visually (animation shows count), revealing correct answer
  * **Guide:** "There were \[X\] \[category A\] and \[Y\] \[category B\] and \[Z\] \[category C\]."
  * These revealed values become the data for graph creation

**Design Note:** Student's counter attempt is checked by Guide reveal. This ensures all students have correct data for graph creation regardless of counting accuracy. The counting game builds investment; the reveal ensures accurate data.

---

**Interaction W.2: Symbol Introduction**

**Purpose:** Establish that ONE symbol represents all data on this graph; introduce "each symbol = 1 item" rule.

**Engagement Anchor:** Anticipation (graph is about to be built)

**Visual:**

- Data from W.1 displayed as reference text: "You counted [X] [category A], [Y] [category B], and [Z] [category C]."
- Empty horizontal picture graph template appears with 3 category rows
- Symbol pre-assigned by system (e.g., stars) and visible in key area

**Guide [verbal]:** "Let's make a picture graph with your data. We'll use stars. Each star shows 1 item."

**No student interaction** — proceeds directly to W.3.

**Design Note:** Symbol is system-assigned (no student selection). This maintains the pedagogical point: ONE symbol type for entire graph, KEY shows what symbols represent. Student agency comes from graph creation in W.3, not symbol choice.

---

**Interaction W.3: Graph Creation (Worked Example)**

**Purpose:** Model graph creation before student attempts. Applies "each symbol \= 1 item" rule from W.2.

**Engagement Anchor:** Cognitive Apprenticeship (I do → You do → You do)

**W.3a: Guide Models First Category**

- **Visual: Picture Graphs (Mode 2: Creating).** Empty horizontal picture graph. Selected symbol ready. Key reads "Each \[symbol\] \= 1 \[item\]." W.1 data visible as reference. Data Table not visible.
-
- **Guide:** "Watch how I show our \[category A\] on the graph."
- **System:** Symbols appear one at a time as Guide narrates
- **Guide:** "You counted \[X\] \[category A\]. So I add \[X\] \[symbols\]. One... two... three..." *(counts aloud to X)*
- **Guide:** "\[X\] \[symbols\]. Each one means 1 \[category A\]."

**W.3b: Student Completes Second Category**

- **Visual: Picture Graphs (Mode 2: Creating).** Horizontal picture graph. Category A complete (Guide-modeled). Categories B and C empty. Key visible. Data Table not visible.
- **Guide:** "Your turn. You counted \[Y\] \[category B\]. Add \[Y\] \[symbols\]. Click to place them \- one for each \[category B\] you counted."
- **Interaction:** Student clicks to add symbols
- **On Correct (\[Y\] symbols):**
  - Guide: "\[Y\] \[symbols\]. One for each \[category B\]."
- **On Incorrect (wrong count):**
  - Guide: "Let’s count the \[symbols\]. We need \[Y\] — one for each \[category B\] we counted."
  - System: Allows retry

**W.3c: Student Completes Third Category**

- **Visual: Picture Graphs (Mode 2: Creating).** Horizontal picture graph. Categories A and B complete. Category C empty. Key visible. Data Table not visible.
- **Guide:** "Now show your \[category C\]. You counted \[Z\]. Click to add \[Z\] \[symbols\]."
- **Interaction:** Student clicks to add symbols
- **On Correct (\[Z\] symbols):**
  - Guide: "Right. \[Z\] \[symbols\]. You made a picture graph\!"
- **On Incorrect (wrong count):**
  - Guide: "Count the \[symbols\]. You need \[Z\] — one for each \[category C\]."
  - System: Allows retry

**Scaffolding Note:** Support fades across categories — full narration (A) → prompted (B) → lighter prompt (C).

---

**Interaction W.4: Bridge to Lesson**

* **Purpose:** Create anticipation for Lesson without teaching. Frame the shift from making to reading.
* **Visual: Picture Graphs (Mode 2: Creating).** Student's completed 1:1 horizontal picture graph (3 categories). Key visible. Data Table not visible.
* **Guide:** "You made a PICTURE GRAPH with your data. "Graphs can be used to show us all types of data\! Ready to learn more about them?"

## **1.7 LESSON (10-12 minutes)**

**Detail Level:** Full interaction specification (AI formats and tags)

---

### **Core Purpose**

Build fluency in reading and interpreting picture graphs and bar graphs (1:1 scale). Students learn to identify graph components, compare data across categories, and solve "how many more/fewer" problems by reading graph values and subtracting. Students also learn "in all" problems require addition and are introduced to two-step problems through sequential submissions.

**Scope Note:** This module combines OUR L1 (interpret graphs) and L2 (represent data, solve problems). Given variable Grade 2 retention, scaffolding is intentionally thorough.

---

### **Pedagogical Flow**

M1 is review/activation of Grade 2 knowledge, so the CRA sequence is modified:

| Standard CRA | M1 Adaptation |
| :---- | :---- |
| Concrete | Students already have concrete experience from Warmup (created their own graph) |
| Relational | Sections 1-2: Notice patterns across graph types, connect components to meaning |
| Abstract | Section 3: Formalize problem-solving language ("how many more" \= subtract, "in all" \= add) |
| Application | Section 3: Apply comparison and total strategies to multiple problems including two-step |

---

### **Lesson Structure**

| Section | Focus | Interactions |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Section 1 | Reading Picture Graphs: Components & Interpretation | 5 |
| Section 2 | Reading Bar Graphs: New Format, Same Information | 4 |
| Section 3 | Problem Solving: Comparison, Total & Two-Step | 6 |

**Total: 15 interactions**

---

### **1.7.1 LESSON SECTION 1: Reading Picture Graphs**

**Purpose:** Activate prior knowledge of picture graphs while explicitly naming graph components and their purposes. Build confidence through multiple reading opportunities before introducing bar graphs.

---

**Interaction 1.1: What Does This Graph Show?**

* **Visual: Picture Graphs (Mode 1: Reading).** Horizontal orientation. Context: "Favorite Fruits," 4 categories. Key: "Each fruit symbol \= 1 vote." Values: Apples=6, Bananas=4, Oranges=5, Grapes=3. Data Table visible alongside showing same values.
* **Guide:** "You made a graph with YOUR Minis. Now let's read some other graphs. A graph isn't just a picture to look at. Every part gives us information to READ. Look at this Favorite Fruits graph. Click on the category that got the MOST votes."
* **Prompt:** "Click on the category that got the MOST votes."
* **Correct Answer:** Apples (6 votes)
* **On Correct:** "Apples got the most. 6 people chose it. You read that from the graph."

---

**Interaction 1.2: Finding the Least**

* **Visual: Picture Graphs (Mode 1: Reading).** Same "Favorite Fruits" graph (horizontal, 4 categories). Data Table visible.
* **Guide:** "You found the most. Now find the opposite. Which fruit got the LEAST votes?"
* **Prompt:** "Which fruit got the LEAST votes?."
* **Correct Answer:** Grapes (3 votes)
* **On Correct:** "Grapes got the least with only 3 votes. You can compare categories by reading each row."

---

**Interaction 1.3: Graph Components Have Jobs**

* **Visual: Picture Graphs (Mode 1: Reading).** NEW graph. Vertical orientation. Context: "Animals at the Zoo," 4 categories. Key: "Each animal icon \= 1 animal." Values: Lions=5, Monkeys=7, Elephants=3, Penguins=6. Data Table visible alongside showing same values.
* **Guide:** "Every part of a graph has a job. Watch."
  * \[Highlight title\] "The TITLE tells us what the data is about: Favorite Fruits."
  * \[Highlight category labels\] "The LABELS tell us what each row shows."
  * \[Highlight key\] "The KEY shows the SCALE. Here, each fruit picture equals 1 vote. That's a scale of 1."
  * "Now you try. Click on the part that tells us what each symbol means."
* **Prompt:** "Click on the part that tells us what each symbol means."
* **Correct Answer:** Student clicks the key
* **On Correct:** "That's the key. It shows the scale. Each symbol equals 1 vote."
* **Vocabulary Introduced:** title, label, key, scale (casual use—"scale of 1" as foundation for M2)

---

**Interaction 1.4: New Graph, New Orientation**

* **Visual:** NEW picture graph (vertical orientation). Context: "Animals at the Zoo" with 4 categories. Each animal icon \= 1 animal. Values: Lions=5, Monkeys=7, Elephants=3, Penguins=6.
* **Guide:** "Here's a different picture graph. This one goes up and down—we call that VERTICAL. Same idea—use the key to read it."
* **Prompt:** "How many monkeys are at the zoo?"
* **Student Action:** \[Multiple choice: 5, 6, 7, 8\]
* **Correct Answer:** 7
* **Answer Rationale:**
  * 7 \= Correct (Monkeys value)
  * 5 \= Misread (Lions value—first category)
  * 6 \= Misread (Penguins value)
  * 8 \= Counting error
* **On Correct:** "7 monkeys. Each symbol equals 1, so you count 7 symbols."
* **Vocabulary Introduced:** vertical

---

**Interaction 1.5: Practice Reading Values**

* **Visual: Picture Graphs (Mode 1: Reading).** Same "Animals at the Zoo" graph (vertical, 4 categories). Data Table visible.
* **Guide:** "Let's read one more value from this graph. Which animal has FEWER than 4 at the zoo?"
* **Prompt:** "Which animal has FEWER than 4 at the zoo? Click on it."
* **Correct Answer:** Elephants (3)
* **On Correct (Elephants):** "Elephants—there are only 3, which is fewer than 4."

**→ SECTION 1 COMPLETE. PROCEED TO SECTION 2\.**

### **1.7.2 LESSON SECTION 2: Reading Bar Graphs**

**Purpose:** Introduce bar graphs as another way to show the same information. Explicitly connect bar height/length to values. Practice with both orientations.

---

**\[SECTION TRANSITION\]**

* **Visual: Picture Graphs (Mode 1: Reading).** Same "Animals at the Zoo" graph (vertical, 4 categories). Data Table visible.
* **Guide:** "You're reading picture graphs like a pro. Now let's see another way to show data: bar graphs."
* **Visual Transition:** Picture graph transforms/animates into bar graph with vertical axis and tick marks at 0 to 8 showing same data. Animation either transforms the picture graph to a bar graph OR (a) bar graph appears alongside picture graph
* **Guide:** "Same information, different format. Instead of counting symbols, we read where the bar ends."

**Implementation Note:** Visual transition can be side-by-side display OR transformation animation—either achieves the pedagogical goal of connecting the two formats.

---

**Interaction 2.1: Connecting Bars to Symbols**

* **Visual: Bar Graphs (Mode 1: Reading).** "Animals at the Zoo" data. Vertical orientation. Axis 0–8 by 1s, tick marks at each whole number. 4 categories. Data Table visible.
* **Guide:** "In a bar graph, the HEIGHT of the bar tells us the amount. Look at the Monkeys bar. How many monkeys does the bar show? Click on the Monkeys bar and find where the bar ends."
* **Prompt:** "Click on the bar for Monkeys and find where the bar ends."
* **Student Action:** Click on Monkeys bar.
* **System Animation (On Correct):** Helping line draws from top of Monkeys bar perpendicular to vertical axis. Value "7" highlights on axis. System-triggered, not student-initiated.
* **Correct Answer:** Clicks on Monkeys bar.
* **On Correct:** Animation shows line drawn from the top of Monkeys bar perpendicular to vertical axis. 7 highlights on axis. "7 monkeys. Same as the picture graph. The bar goes up to 7."

**Interaction 2.2: Reading Bar Graphs Independently**

### **1.7.3 LESSON SECTION 3: Problem Solving with Graphs**

**Purpose:** Explicitly teach that "how many more/fewer" means compare by subtracting. Teach "in all" means add for total. Introduce two-step problems as sequential submissions. This language appears on nearly every state test data question. Prevent Misconception \#17 (All Data Must Be Used).

---

**\[SECTION TRANSITION\]**

* **Visual: Bar Graphs (Mode 1: Reading).** Same "Favorite Colors" graph (horizontal, 4 categories) remains. Data Table not visible.
* **Guide:** "You found the most and the fewest. Now let's answer some different kinds of questions."

---

**Interaction 3.1: "How Many More" \= Subtract (Explicit Teaching)**

* **Visual: Bar Graphs (Mode 1: Reading).** Same "Favorite Colors" graph (horizontal). Data Table not visible. System highlights Blue (8) and Yellow (6) bars as Guide speaks.
* **Guide:** "~~Here's the question:~~ How many MORE students chose Blue than Yellow?"
* **Guide:** "This is a COMPARISON question. 'How many more' means we COMPARE two amounts. We find the DIFFERENCE by subtracting."
* **Guide:** "Blue has 8\. Yellow has 6\. Since more students like Blue, subtract Blue minus Yellow. How many more students chose Blue than Yellow?"
* **Prompt:** "How many more students chose Blue than Yellow?"
* **Student Action:** \[Multiple choice: 2, 8, 14, 3\]
* **Correct Answer:** 2
* **Answer Rationale:**
  * 2 \= Correct (8 \- 6 \= 2, the difference)
  * 8 \= Error: Used Blue's value only
  * 14 \= Error: Added instead of subtracted (8 \+ 6\)
  * 3 \= Error: Subtraction mistake or misread
* **On Correct:** "2 more students. You found the difference: 8 minus 6 equals 2."
* **Critical Teaching:** This is the KEY instructional moment. Explicit modeling of "How many more" means to compare which means to subtract.
* **Vocabulary Introduced:** compare, difference (explicit teaching)
* **Remediation (for this interaction specifically):**
  * **Light:** "Remember: 'how many more' means subtract. Blue has 8, Yellow has 6\. What's 8 minus 6?"

---

**Interaction 3.2: "How Many Fewer" \= Also Subtract**

* **Visual: Bar Graphs (Mode 1: Reading).** Same "Favorite Colors" graph (horizontal). Data Table not visible. System highlights Red (5) and Blue (8) bars.
* **Guide:** "New question: How many FEWER students chose Red than Blue?"
* **Guide:** "'How many fewer' is ALSO a comparison. We still find the difference. "
* **Prompt:** "How many fewer students chose Red than Blue?"
* **Student Action:** \[Multiple choice: 3, 5, 8, 13\]
* **Correct Answer:** 3
* **Answer Rationale:**
  * 3 \= Correct (8 \- 5 \= 3, the difference)
  * 5 \= Error: Used Red's value only
  * 8 \= Error: Used Blue's value only
  * 13 \= Error: Added instead of subtracted (8 \+ 5\)
* **On Correct:** "3 fewer students. Blue has 8, Red has 5\. The difference is 3."
* **Vocabulary Reinforced:** fewer, difference, comparison

---

**Interaction 3.3: "In All" \= Add for Total**

* **Visual: Bar Graphs (Mode 1: Reading).** Same "Favorite Colors" graph (horizontal). Data Table not visible. System highlights Yellow (6) and Red (5) bars.
* **Guide:** "Here's a different kind of question: How many students chose Yellow OR Red IN ALL?"
* **Guide:** "'In all' means we want the TOTAL. We ADD to find totals."
* **Prompt:** "How many students chose Yellow or Red in all?"
* **Student Action:** \[Multiple choice: 1, 6, 11, 14\]
* **Correct Answer:** 11
* **Answer Rationale:**
  * 11 \= Correct (6 \+ 5 \= 11, the total)
  * 1 \= Error: Subtracted (6 \- 5\)
  * 6 \= Error: Used Yellow's value only
  * 23 \= Error: Adds all data values
* **On Correct:** "11 students in all. Yellow has 6, Red has 5\. ~~You added to find the total:~~ 6 plus 5 equals 11."
* **Vocabulary Introduced:** total, in all (explicit teaching)

---

**Interaction 3.3b: Two-Step Introduction (Sequential)**

* **Visual: Bar Graphs (Mode 1: Reading).** Same "Favorite Colors" graph (horizontal). Data Table not visible. Yellow (6) and Red (5) bars highlighted from 3.3.
* **Guide:** "Sometimes you need TWO steps to answer a question. Let's try one together."
* **Guide:** "Question: How many MORE students chose Yellow or Red together compared to Green?"
* **Guide:** "Step 1: Find how many chose Yellow or Red IN ALL. How many liked Yellow or Red in all?"
* **Prompt:** "How many liked Yellow or Red in all?"
* **Student Action:** \[Multiple choice: 5, 6, 11, 14\]
* **Correct Answer:** 11
* **On Correct:** "11 in all. Yellow has 6, Red has 5\. 6 plus 5 equals 11."
* **Visual Update:** Yellow+Red total (11) displayed on screen. Green (4) bar highlights alongside. System shows both values for comparison.
* **Guide:** "Step 2: Now compare that total to Green."
* **Prompt:** "How many MORE is 11 than Green's 4?"
* **Student Action:** \[Multiple choice: 4, 7, 11, 15\]
* **Correct Answer:** 7
* **Answer Rationale:**
  * 7 \= Correct (11 \- 4 \= 7\)
  * 4 \= Error: Used Green's value only
  * 11 \= Error: Used total only
  * 15 \= Error: Added instead of subtracted
* **On Correct:** "7 more students like red or yellow compared to green. You used TWO steps: first you added to find a total, then you subtracted to compare."

**Purpose:** Introduces two-step problems as sequential single submissions. Explicit step labeling scaffolds the process.

**Practice Note:** Practice phase may experiment with combined two-step problems (both operations in single submission). This Lesson interaction establishes the sequential foundation.

---

**Interaction 3.4a: What Information Do You Need? (Metacognitive/Error Prediction)**

* **Visual: Picture Graphs (Mode 1: Reading).** NEW graph. Vertical orientation. Context: "Pets We Have," 5 categories. Key: "Each X \= 1 pet." Values: Dogs=7, Cats=9, Fish=4, Birds=6, Lizards=1. Data Table not visible.

* **Student Action:** \[Multi-select checkboxes: ☐ Dogs ☐ Cats ☐ Fish ☐ Birds ☐ Lizards\]
* **Correct Answer:** Dogs AND Fish selected (only)
* **On Correct:** "You found exactly what you need: just Dogs and Fish. The other categories don't matter for this question."

**Purpose:** Active metacognitive prompt (Error Prediction type). Prevents Misconception \#17 (All Data Must Be Used) by making selective data use explicit.

**Interaction 3.4b: Solving with Selected Data**

* **Visual: Picture Graphs (Mode 1: Reading).** Same "Pets We Have" graph (vertical). System highlights Dogs (7) and Fish (4) only. Cats, Birds, Lizards dimmed/unhighlighted. Data Table not visible.

* **Prompt:** "How many fewer students have fish than dogs?"
* **Student Action:** \[Multiple choice: 3, 4, 7, 11\]
* **Correct Answer:** 3
* **Answer Rationale:**
  * 3 \= Correct (7 \- 4 \= 3\)
  * 4 \= Error: Used Fish value only
  * 7 \= Error: Used Dogs value only

* **On Correct:** "3 fewer students. 7 Students have dogs and 4 students have Fish. The difference is 3."

**Remediation (for this interaction specifically):**

* **Light:** "You need Dogs and Fish. Dogs has 7, Fish has 4\. Subtract to find how many fewer."

**Interaction 3.4c:  Two-Step (Sequential)**

* **Visual: Picture Graphs (Mode 1: Reading).** Same "Pets We Have" graph (vertical). All categories at default state (no highlighting). Data Table not visible.
* **Guide:** "Question: How many MORE students have Cats than Fish and Lizards combined?

* **Guide:** "Next, how many MORE students have Cats compared to Fish and Lizards together?”
* **Prompt:** "How many MORE have Cats than the total of Fish and Lizards?"

* **Correct Answer:** 4

  * 4 \= Correct (9 \- 5 \= 4\)

  * 5 \= Error: Calculation error or subtracted only Cats and Fish

### **Required Phrases (Must Appear in Script)**

* "A graph isn't just a picture to look at—every part gives us information to READ" (or equivalent)
* "The KEY shows the SCALE"
* "Each symbol equals 1" / "scale of 1" (1:1 scale foundation)
* "We call that VERTICAL" / "We call that HORIZONTAL"
* "This is a COMPARISON question"
* "We find the DIFFERENCE by subtracting"
* "'How many fewer' is ALSO a comparison"
* "'In all' means we want the TOTAL. We ADD to find totals."
* "You found exactly what you need" (or equivalent selective data use)
* "You used TWO steps" (two-step problem acknowledgment)

---

### **Forbidden Phrases (Create Misconceptions)**

* ❌ "Each symbol represents 2/5/10" (scaled graphs are M2+)
* ❌ "Estimate the value" (all M1 graphs have exact 1:1 values)
* ❌ "The axis shows..." (formal axis vocabulary is M4)
* ❌ "You're so smart\!" / "Perfect\!" / "Amazing\!" (identity labels, overpraise)
* ❌ "You thought carefully about that" (assumed internal state)

---

### **Misconception Prevention**

**\#16 (Graph as Picture):**

- Proactive framing in 1.1: "A graph isn't just a picture to look at—every part gives us information to READ"
- Every interaction asks students to READ data values, not describe visual appearance
- Graph components explicitly named with their PURPOSE ("The key SHOWS the SCALE...")
- Guide language emphasizes reading: "You read that from the graph"

**\#17 (All Data Must Be Used):**

- Interaction 3.4a (multi-select) explicitly teaches selective data use
- Guide praises finding "exactly what you need"
- Visual dimming of irrelevant categories in 3.4b reinforces focus

### **1.7.5 Success Criteria**

**The One Thing:** Students understand that graphs are organized ways to show data, and that every part of a graph tells us something specific we need to read accurately.

**Ready for Exit Check:** Students can:

- Read values from picture graphs (1:1 scale, both orientations)
- Read values from bar graphs (1:1 scale, both orientations)
- Identify most/least and compare categories
- Solve "how many more/fewer" by finding the difference (subtracting)
- Solve "in all" by finding the total (adding)
- Use only relevant data for a given question
- Complete two-step problems when presented sequentially

**Ready for Module 2:** Students have solid 1:1 scale foundation. They understand that each symbol \= 1 item (scale of 1), preparing them for M2 where each symbol will represent MORE than 1\.
