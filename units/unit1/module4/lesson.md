## **1.7 LESSON (10-12 minutes)**

**Detail Level:** Full interaction specification (AI formats and tags)

---

### **Core Purpose**

Extend students' understanding of scaled bar graphs from scale of 5 to scale of 10. Connect scale of 10 to skip-counting by 10s. Introduce formal "axis" vocabulary. Introduce interpolation—reading values that fall BETWEEN the lines on the axis (multiples of 5 that aren't multiples of 10). Students create bar graphs with scale of 10, building on bar graph creation skills from M3.

---

### **Pedagogical Flow**

M4 extends an established skill (bar graph creation) to a new scale (10) and introduces a genuinely new skill (interpolation). The CRA sequence applies:

| CRA Phase | M4 Application |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Concrete** | Visual comparison: Picture graph (1:10) alongside bar graph (1:10)—same data, different format |
| **Relational** | Students notice efficiency (count by 10s); see how bar HEIGHT maps to axis |
| **Abstract** | Formalize "axis" vocabulary; introduce "between the lines" for interpolation |
| **Application** | Create bar graphs with scale of 10; read and create values at AND between axis lines |

---

### **1.7.1 LESSON SECTION 1: Scale of 10 + Reading Bar Heights**

**Purpose:** Establish scale of 10 through skip-counting by 10s. Students read and create bar graphs with scale of 10 where all values are multiples of 10. Build on Warmup's data collection. Guide uses "axis" naturally but it is not a teaching focus in this section. Worked example (1.1) before student attempts.

**Orientation:** All vertical

---

**Interaction 1.1: Reading Bar Heights \[WORKED EXAMPLE\]**

- **Vocabulary Introduced:** "axis," "height of the bar"
- **Visual: Bar Graphs (Mode 1: Reading).** Vertical. Bar graph from Warmup (student's collected data). 4 categories (colors: Red=40, Blue=30, Green=50, Yellow=20). Axis labeled 0–60 in 10s. All 4 bars pre-filled. Red bar highlighted with axis line at 40 glowing.
- **Guide:** "You saw this bar graph in Warmup. Now let's learn how to read it exactly."
- **Guide:** "See these numbers along the side? They show the scale—10, 20, 30, 40... That's counting by 10s!"
- **Guide:** "Red has 40. The HEIGHT OF THE BAR ends at the 40 line."
- **Guide:** "I can count by 10s to check: 10, 20, 30, 40. You already know how to count by 10s!"
- **No student prompt** (observation—skill introduction)
- **Vocabulary Introduced:** "height of the bar," "scale of 10"

---

**Interaction 1.2: Reading Bar Height**

- **Visual: Bar Graphs (Mode 1: Reading).** Vertical. Same bar graph as 1.1. Blue bar highlighted (height = 30, at 30 axis line). Axis available for reference.
- **Guide:** "Look at Blue. The bar's HEIGHT ends at a line on the axis. What value does this show?"
- **Prompt:** "What value does this show?"
- **Student Action:** \[Multiple choice: 3, 20, 30, 35\]
- **Correct Answer:** 30
- **Answer Rationale:**
  - 30 = Correct (bar height at 30 line)
  - 3 = Counted symbols from picture graph (Misconception #1)
  - 20 = Misread axis
  - 35 = Between-the-lines error (not applicable here—bar IS at line)
- **On Correct:** "30. The bar ends at the 30 line. Same as 3 symbols on the picture graph—each symbol shows 10."
- **Remediation (Light):** "Find where the top of the bar lines up with the numbers."

---

**Interaction 1.3: Skip-Counting Connection**

- **Visual: Bar Graphs (Mode 1: Reading).** Vertical. Same bar graph. Green bar highlighted (height = 50). Axis lines glow sequentially: 10, 20, 30, 40, 50.
- **Guide:** "Green shows 50. Let's count by 10s to check: 10, 20, 30, 40, 50."
- **Guide:** "You already know how to count by 10s. That's exactly what we do with scale of 10. The fourth bar shows Yellow. Count by 10s—what value does the bar show?"
- **Prompt:** "What value does the bar show?"
- **Visual (follow-up):** Yellow bar highlighted (height = 20). Axis available for reference.
- **Student Action:** \[Multiple choice: 2, 12, 20, 25\]
- **Correct Answer:** 20
- **On Correct:** "20. Two jumps of 10: 10, 20."
- **Remediation (Light):** "Start at 0. Count by 10s until you reach the top of the bar."

---

**Interaction 1.4: Your Turn — Create a Bar \[GUIDED\]**

- **Visual: Data Tables (Numeric Display).** "Favorite Recess Activities" — Swings=30, Tag=50, Slides=40, Jump Rope=20. Vertical.
- **Visual: Bar Graphs (Mode 2: Creating).** Vertical. Scale of 10, axis 0–60. 3 of 4 bars pre-filled: Tag=50, Slides=40, Jump Rope=20. Swings bar empty. Ghost gridlines at 10s visible. Snap-to-10 enabled. Click-to-set.
- **Guide:** "Now you create a bar. Swings has 30 votes. Set the HEIGHT for Swings to show 30."
- **Prompt:** "Set the HEIGHT for Swings to show 30."
- **Student Action:** Hover to preview, click to set bar height at 30
- **Correct Answer:** Bar at height 30
- **On Correct:** "10, 20, 30. The bar ends at 30 on the axis. 30 votes for Swings."
- **Remediation (Light):** "Find 30 on the axis. Set the bar HEIGHT to end there."

---

**Interaction 1.5: Create Another Bar**

- **Visual: Data Tables (Numeric Display).** "Books Read This Month" — Fiction=40, Nonfiction=60, Comics=20, Poetry=30. Horizontal layout.
- **Visual: Bar Graphs (Mode 2: Creating).** Vertical. Scale of 10, axis 0–70. 3 of 4 bars pre-filled: Fiction=40, Comics=20, Poetry=30. Nonfiction bar empty. Ghost gridlines at 10s visible. Snap-to-10 enabled. Click-to-set.
- **Guide:** "Nonfiction has 60 books. That's the biggest number. Set the bar for Nonfiction."
- **Prompt:** "Set the bar for Nonfiction to show 60."
- **Student Action:** Set Nonfiction bar to 60
- **Correct Answer:** Nonfiction at 60
- **On Correct:** "Nonfiction at 60. You found the 60 line and set the bar there."
- **Remediation (Light):** "Find where 60 is. Set the bar to end at that line."

---

**Interaction 1.6: Reading for Comparison**

- **Visual: Bar Graphs (Mode 2: Creating).** Vertical. Completed "Books Read This Month" bar graph: Fiction=40, Nonfiction=60, Comics=20, Poetry=30. All 4 bars visible. Data Table NOT visible.
- **Guide:** "Here's the completed graph. Use it to answer a question. How many MORE books were Nonfiction than Comics?"
- **Prompt:** "How many MORE books were Nonfiction than Comics?"
- **Student Action:** \[Multiple choice: 20, 40, 60, 80\]
- **Correct Answer:** 40
- **Answer Rationale:**
  - 40 = Correct (60 - 20 = 40)
  - 20 = Comics value only
  - 60 = Nonfiction value only
  - 80 = Added instead of subtracted
- **On Correct:** "40 more. Nonfiction is 60, Comics is 20. The difference is 40."
- **Remediation (Light):** "Find both values on the graph. Subtract to find how many more."

**→ SECTION 1 COMPLETE. PROCEED TO SECTION 2.**

### **1.7.2 LESSON SECTION 2: Interpolation Introduction**

**Purpose:** Introduce reading and creating values BETWEEN axis lines. Students learn that bars can end "between the lines" to show values like 15, 25, 35, 45. Connect to "half of 10 = 5" principle with brief half-symbol callback from M2. Ghost gridlines at 5s provide visual scaffolding. This is genuinely NEW—M3 used only values at axis lines.

**Orientation:** All vertical

---

**\[SECTION TRANSITION\]**

- **Visual:** Clean transition. New bar graph appears.
- **Guide:** "You can read bars that end right at the lines. But what if a bar ends BETWEEN the lines?"

---

**Interaction 2.1: Discovering Between-the-Lines \[WORKED EXAMPLE\]**

- **Visual: Bar Graphs (Mode 1: Reading).** Vertical. "Stickers Collected" — Emma=30 (at 30 line), Jake=25 (between 20–30 lines), Mia=40 (at 40 line), Leo=35 (between 30–40 lines). Scale of 10, axis 0–50. Ghost gridlines at 5s visible (lighter than 10s lines). Jake's bar highlighted.
- **Guide:** "Look at Jake's bar. It doesn't end at a line—it ends BETWEEN THE LINES."
- **Guide:** "Remember half-symbols from scale of 2? Half of 2 was 1. Same idea here—half of 10 is 5."
- **Guide:** "Half of 10 is 5. So between 20 and 30, that's 20 + 5 = 25. Jake has 25 stickers."
- **No student prompt** (observation only)

**Key Teaching:** "Half of 10 = 5" principle introduced.

---

**Interaction 2.2: Reading Between the Lines**

- **Visual: Bar Graphs (Mode 1: Reading).** Vertical. Same "Stickers Collected" graph. Leo's bar highlighted (height = 35, between 30–40 lines). Ghost gridlines at 5s visible.
- **Guide:** "Now look at Leo's bar. It's also between the lines. Leo's bar is between 30 and 40. What value does this show?"
- **Prompt:** "What value does Leo's bar show?"
- **Student Action:** \[Multiple choice: 30, 34, 35, 40\]
- **Correct Answer:** 35
- **Answer Rationale:**
  - 35 = Correct (halfway between 30 and 40)
  - 30 = Read lower line only (Misconception #5)
  - 40 = Read upper line only
  - 34 = Random guess between values
- **On Correct:** "35. It's halfway between 30 and 40. Half of 10 is 5, so 30 plus 5 is 35."
- **Remediation (Light):** "Find the two lines the bar is between. What's halfway? Half of 10 is 5."

---

**Interaction 2.3: Creating a Between-the-Lines Bar \[GUIDED\]**

- **Visual: Data Tables (Numeric Display).** "Points Scored" — Team A=45, Team B=30, Team C=25, Team D=50, Team E=15. Vertical.
- **Visual: Bar Graphs (Mode 2: Creating).** Vertical. Scale of 10, axis 0–60. 4 of 5 bars pre-filled: Team B=30, Team C=25, Team D=50, Team E=15. Team A bar empty. Ghost gridlines at 5s visible. Snap-to-5 enabled. Click-to-set.
- **Guide:** "Team A scored 45 points. Set the bar for Team A at 45—halfway between 40 and 50."
- **Prompt:** "Set the bar for Team A."
- **Student Action:** Hover to preview, click to set bar at 45 (halfway between 40 and 50 lines)
- **Correct Answer:** Bar at height 45
- **On Correct:** "45. Right between the lines—halfway from 40 to 50."
- **Remediation (Light):** "Find 40 and 50 on the axis. 45 is halfway between them."

---

**Interaction 2.4: Create Another Between-the-Lines Bar**

- **Visual: Data Tables (Numeric Display).** "Laps Completed" — Runner A=20, Runner B=50, Runner C=35, Runner D=45. Horizontal layout.
- **Visual: Bar Graphs (Mode 2: Creating).** Vertical. Scale of 10, axis 0–60. 3 of 4 bars pre-filled: Runner A=20, Runner B=50, Runner D=45. Runner C bar empty. Ghost gridlines at 5s visible. Snap-to-5 enabled. Click-to-set.
- **Guide:** "Runner C completed 35 laps. Set the bar for Runner C to show 35."
- **Prompt:** "Set the bar for Runner C."
- **Student Action:** Set bar at 35 (halfway between 30 and 40)
- **Correct Answer:** Bar at height 35
- **On Correct:** "35. Between 30 and 40—right in the middle."
- **Remediation (Light):** "35 is halfway between 30 and 40."

---

**Interaction 2.5: Mixed Values — At Lines AND Between**

- **Visual: Data Tables (Numeric Display).** "Minutes of Reading" — Monday=40, Tuesday=35, Wednesday=50, Thursday=25. Vertical.
- **Visual: Bar Graphs (Mode 2: Creating).** Vertical. Scale of 10, axis 0–60. NO bars pre-filled. Ghost gridlines at 5s visible. Snap-to-5 enabled. Click-to-set.
- **Guide:** "Some values are at the axis lines. Some are between. You decide where each bar goes. Try to complete the graph."
- **Prompt:** "Complete the graph."
- **Student Action:** Set Monday=40 (at line), Tuesday=35 (between), Wednesday=50 (at line), Thursday=25 (between)
- **Correct Answer:** All four bars correctly placed
- **On Correct:** "Monday and Wednesday are at the axis lines. Tuesday and Thursday are between the lines. You figured out which is which."
- **Remediation (Light):** "Check each value. 40 and 50 are at lines. 35 and 25 are between lines."

---

**Interaction 2.6: Reading Mixed Values**

- **Visual: Bar Graphs (Mode 2: Creating).** Vertical. Student's completed "Minutes of Reading" graph: Monday=40 (at line), Tuesday=35 (between), Wednesday=50 (at line), Thursday=25 (between). Data Table NOT visible.
- **Guide:** "Use this graph to answer. How many TOTAL minutes of reading for Monday and Tuesday?"
- **Prompt:** "How many TOTAL minutes of reading for Monday and Tuesday?"
- **Student Action:** \[Multiple choice: 35, 40, 75, 85\]
- **Correct Answer:** 75
- **Answer Rationale:**
  - 75 = Correct (40 + 35 = 75)
  - 35 = Tuesday only
  - 40 = Monday only
  - 70 = Calculation error (reading Tuesday as 30)
- **On Correct:** "75 minutes total. Monday is 40, Tuesday is 35. 40 plus 35 is 75."
- **Remediation (Light):** "Find Monday's value and Tuesday's value. Add them together."

**→ SECTION 2 COMPLETE. PROCEED TO SECTION 3.**

### **1.7.3 LESSON SECTION 3: Full Independence + Horizontal Orientation**

**Purpose:** Students demonstrate full independence creating bar graphs with scale of 10, including values at AND between axis lines. Horizontal orientation introduced. No pre-filled bars.

**Orientation:** All horizontal

---

**\[SECTION TRANSITION\]**

- **Visual:** Clean transition to horizontal bar graph.
- **Guide:** "You've made vertical bar graphs. Now let's try one that goes sideways—HORIZONTAL."

---

**Interaction 3.1: Horizontal Orientation Introduction**

- **Visual: Bar Graphs (Mode 1: Reading).** Horizontal. "Votes for Field Trip" — Zoo=45, Museum=30, Park=55, Aquarium=40, Theater=35. All 5 bars complete. Scale of 10, axis along bottom 0–60. Categories listed vertically on left. Ghost gridlines at 5s visible.
- **Guide:** "This bar graph is HORIZONTAL. The bars go sideways, but you read them the same way—finding where the bar ends on the scale."
- **Guide points to Zoo bar:** "Zoo's bar extends to 45. Between 40 and 50—halfway. What value does the Museum bar show?"
- **Prompt:** "What value does the Museum bar show?"
- **Student Action:** \[Multiple choice: 3, 25, 30, 35\]
- **Correct Answer:** 30
- **On Correct:** "30. The bar ends right at the 30 line."
- **Remediation (Light):** "Follow the bar to see where it ends on the axis."

---

**Interaction 3.2: Reading Horizontal — Between the Lines**

- **Visual: Bar Graphs (Mode 1: Reading).** Horizontal. Same "Votes for Field Trip" graph. Park bar highlighted (extends to 55, between 50–60 lines).
- **Guide:** "Look at the Park bar. What value does the Park bar represent?"
- **Prompt:** "What value does the Park bar represent?"
- **Student Action:** \[Multiple choice: 50, 55, 58, 60\]
- **Correct Answer:** 55
- **On Correct:** "55. Halfway between 50 and 60."
- **Remediation (Light):** "Which axis values is the bar between? It's halfway. Half of 10 is 5."

---

**Interaction 3.3: Create Horizontal Bar Graph — Full Independence**

- **Visual: Data Tables (Numeric Display).** "Miles Walked This Week" — Kai=35, Ava=50, Noah=25, Zoe=45. Horizontal layout.
- **Visual: Bar Graphs (Mode 2: Creating).** Horizontal. Scale of 10, axis 0–60. NO bars pre-filled. Ghost gridlines at 5s visible. Snap-to-5 enabled. Click-to-set (extends rightward).
- **Guide:** "Create the whole graph. Some values are at axis lines, some are between."
- **Prompt:** "Complete the graph."
- **Student Action:** Set Kai=35 (between), Ava=50 (at line), Noah=25 (between), Zoe=45 (between)
- **Correct Answer:** All four bars correctly placed
- **On Correct:** "Ava at 50—right at the line. Kai, Noah, and Zoe—all between the lines."
- **Remediation (Light):** "Check each value on the axis. 50 is at a line. 35, 25, and 45 are between lines."

---

**Interaction 3.4: Comparison from Horizontal Graph**

- **Visual: Bar Graphs (Mode 2: Creating).** Horizontal. Student's completed "Miles Walked" graph: Kai=35 (between), Ava=50 (at line), Noah=25 (between), Zoe=45 (between). Data Table NOT visible.
- **Guide:** "Use the graph you made. How many MORE miles did Ava walk than Noah?"
- **Prompt:** "How many MORE miles did Ava walk than Noah?"
- **Student Action:** \[Multiple choice: 15, 25, 50, 75\]
- **Correct Answer:** 25
- **Answer Rationale:**
  - 25 = Correct (50 − 25 = 25)
  - 15 = Miscalculation / partial difference
  - 50 = Ava's value only
  - 75 = Added instead of subtracted
- **On Correct:** "25 more miles. Ava walked 50, Noah walked 25. The difference is 25."
- **Remediation (Light):** "Find both values. Subtract to find how many more."

---

**Interaction 3.5: Final Challenge — Create and Analyze**

- **Visual (Part 1 — Creation): Data Tables (Numeric Display).** "Plants in the Garden" — Tomatoes=60, Peppers=35, Carrots=45, Beans=20. Vertical layout.
- **Visual (Part 1 — Creation): Bar Graphs (Mode 2: Creating).** Horizontal. Scale of 10, axis 0–70. NO bars pre-filled. Ghost gridlines at 5s visible. Snap-to-5 enabled.
- **Guide:** "Use the data table to complete the graph."
- **Prompt (Part 1):** "Create the bar graph."
- **Student Action:** Set all four bars: Tomatoes=60, Peppers=35, Carrots=45, Beans=20
- **On Complete:** Graph displays with all bars. **Data Table hides** (problem-solving uses graph only).
- **Visual (Part 2 — Problem-solving): Bar Graphs (Mode 2: Creating).** Same graph. Peppers and Carrots highlighted. Data Table hidden.
- **Prompt (Part 2):** "How many plants IN ALL for Peppers and Carrots?"
- **Student Action:** \[Multiple choice: 70, 75, 80, 90\]
- **Correct Answer:** 80
- **On Correct:** "80 plants in all. 35 plus 45 is 80."
- **Remediation (Light):** "Find Peppers and Carrots on your graph. Add them together."

**→ SECTION 3 COMPLETE. LESSON COMPLETE.**

### **Bridge to Exit Check**

- **Visual:** Final graph remains briefly.
- **Guide:** "You made bar graphs with scale of 10. You used the axis to find values—even values between the lines. Let's see what you know."

---

### **Required Phrases (Must Appear in Script)**

- "The axis shows the scale" (axis function)
- "Scale of 10" / "count by 10s" (scale meaning)
- "The HEIGHT OF THE BAR shows..." (bar reading instruction)
- "What value does this show?" (test language)
- "BETWEEN THE LINES" (interpolation language)
- "Half of 10 is 5" (interpolation principle)
- "Halfway between \[X\] and \[Y\] is \[Z\]" (interpolation explanation)
- "HORIZONTAL—the bars go sideways" (orientation acknowledgment in 3.1)
- "How many MORE" / "How many IN ALL" (problem-solving language)

---

### **Forbidden Phrases (Create Misconceptions)**

- ❌ "Tick marks" (conflicts with Fractions unit—use "the lines" or "marks on the axis")
- ❌ "Each symbol represents 2/5" (wrong scales—M4 is scale 10 only)
- ❌ "Multiply by 10" / "times 10" / "×" notation (multiplication is M7+)
- ❌ "Estimate the value" / "about \[X\]" (teach exact halfway, not estimation)
- ❌ "Scale of 2" / "Scale of 5" (avoid mixing; M4 uses scale of 10 only)
- ❌ "You're so smart!" / "Perfect!" / "Amazing!" (identity labels, overpraise)
- ❌ "You thought carefully about that" (assumed internal state)

---

### **Misconception Prevention**

**#5 Can't Interpolate Bar Heights (PRIMARY):**

- Ghost gridlines at 5s make halfway points visible
- Explicit "half of 10 = 5" teaching in 2.1
- Worked example (2.1) before student attempts interpolation
- Multiple practice opportunities before Exit Check
- Remediation explicitly walks through the halfway calculation

**#1 Counting Symbols Instead of Scaling (SECONDARY):**

- Picture graphs appear only for comparison (not creation)
- Guide explicitly narrates symbol-to-bar connection in 1.1
- All student creation is bar graphs (not picture graphs)

**#16 Graph as Picture (TERTIARY):**

- Axis vocabulary makes abstract representation explicit
- "The HEIGHT OF THE BAR shows the amount" reinforced throughout

---

### **1.7.4 Incomplete Script Flags**

**If ANY of these are true, STOP and complete the script:**

- [ ] "Axis" vocabulary not explicitly introduced in Section 1
- [ ] "Between the lines" / interpolation not taught in Section 2
- [ ] "Half of 10 is 5" principle not stated
- [ ] No worked example before student attempts interpolation
- [ ] No values between axis lines in Section 2+
- [ ] Horizontal orientation not introduced in Section 3
- [ ] Total interaction count below 15
- [ ] Missing any required phrase
- [ ] Contains any forbidden phrase

### **1.7.5 Success Criteria**

**The One Thing:** Students understand that bar graphs with scale of 10 represent data where each unit on the axis equals 10, and they can read/create these graphs including values that fall between the lines (using "half of 10 = 5").

**Ready for Exit Check:** Students can:

- Identify the axis and explain it shows the scale
- Read bar heights at axis lines (multiples of 10)
- Read bar heights between axis lines (multiples of 5: 15, 25, 35, 45)
- Create bars at axis lines AND between lines
- Work with both vertical and horizontal orientations
- Solve "how many more" and "in all" problems using scaled bar graphs

**Ready for Module 5:** Students have:

- Solid scale of 10 understanding (fourth scale in toolkit: 1, 2, 5, 10)
- Interpolation skills (reading/creating between-the-lines values)
- Formal axis vocabulary
- Fluency with bar graph creation at scale of 10
- Flexibility with both orientations
