## 1.7 LESSON (\~10-12 minutes)
### Core Purpose
Students learn to BUILD multiplication expressions from equal groups visuals using the Equation Builder, and to identify visuals that match given expressions. M8 does NOT teach computation—it teaches producing and interpreting the symbolic notation that represents equal groups structure. By the end of the lesson, students can independently build expressions using tiles and can match expressions to their corresponding visuals bidirectionally.
### Pedagogical Flow
M8 bridges from RECOGNITION (M7) to PRODUCTION. Students arrive knowing how to see equal groups and match them to expressions when given options. The Lesson teaches expression BUILDING through the CRA sequence, with M8-specific adaptations:
**CRA Sequence for M8:**
| CRA Phase | M8 Application |
| --- | --- |
| **Concrete** | Build expressions from bags—worked example + student attempts with Method A |
| **Relational** | Show pattern across contexts (bags AND boxes), introduce Methods C/D |
| **Application** | Independent tile building + reverse direction (meaning confirmation) |
| **Transfer** | Circles context—both directions, reduced scaffolding |
**Key Progression:**
- Method unlock: A (MC) → A + C/D (tiles)
- Direction: Visual → Expression (primary) + Expression → Visual (secondary)
- Context: Bags → Bags + Boxes → Circles
- Scaffolding: Full → Partial → Independent
### Lesson Structure
| Phase | Focus | Interactions |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **CONCRETE** | Bags: Worked example + Method A | 2 |
| **RELATIONAL** | Pattern across contexts + equal groups criterion + Methods C/D | 4 |
| **APPLICATION** | Independent building + reverse direction | 4 |
| **TRANSFER** | Circles: both directions, reduced scaffolding | 3 |
| **Total** |  | **12** |

## 1.7.1 LESSON SECTION 1: Building the Structure
**Purpose:** Establish the basic expression-building process with worked example, introduce "expression" vocabulary, and have student attempt with Method A (familiar from M7).
**Context:** Bags only (concrete, familiar from M7)
---
### Interaction 1.1: Transition from Warmup + Worked Example
- **Purpose:** Model the complete expression-building process step-by-step using a worked example. Introduce "expression" vocabulary formally.
- **Visual: Context Visualizations (Mode 2).** Concrete context (bags with apples). 4 bags with 3 items each. Full separation. \[WORKED EXAMPLE\] designation. Template shows `[___] × [___]` empty.
- **Guide:** "You've been matching expressions to equal groups. Now YOU get to build them — and you'll learn to go the other way too, figuring out what an expression looks like as a picture. Let me show you how building works."
- **Guide:** "First, I ask myself: how many GROUPS? Each bag is one group."
- **Visual:** Bags highlight sequentially as Guide counts
- **Guide:** "1, 2, 3, 4. Four bags. That's 4 groups."
- **Guide:** "Now I ask: how many in EACH group? Let me look inside one bag."
- **Visual:** Focus on first bag, apples highlight
- **Guide:** "1, 2, 3. Three apples. Let me check another..."
- **Visual:** Second bag highlights
- **Guide:** "Also 3. They're equal groups—same number in every bag."
- **Guide:** "So I have 4 groups of 3. Now watch how I build the expression."
- **Visual:** Equation Builder—first slot glows with subtle label "groups"
- **Guide:** "The first number is how many groups. I click 4."
- **Visual:** "4" appears in first slot (Guide demonstrates)
- **Visual:** Second slot glows with label "in each"
- **Guide:** "The second number is how many in each group. I click 3."
- **Visual:** "3" appears in second slot, complete expression shows: `4 × 3`
- **Guide:** "I built 4 times 3. This is called an EXPRESSION. An expression uses numbers and symbols to show the math. No equal sign — just the numbers and the times symbol."
- **Student Action:** Observation only (no response required).
	- This is a worked example to establish the thinking process.
> **Vocabulary Note:** "Expression" introduced explicitly after student has seen the construction process. Connects to concrete experience before naming.
---
### Interaction 1.2: Student Builds with Method A
- **Purpose:** Student attempts expression building using Method A (MC), which bridges recognition to production.
- **Visual: Context Visualizations (Mode 2) + Equation Builder (Method A).** Concrete context (bags with items). 3 bags with 6 items each. \[NEW\] initialization. Multiple Choice interaction.
- **Guide:** "Your turn to build an expression. First question: how many groups do you see? Second question: how many in each group? Now build the expression."
- **Prompt:** "Build the expression for the picture."
- **Student Action:** Multiple Choice selection.
	- **Options:** A: 3 × 6 ✓, B: 6 × 3 (reversal), C: 3 + 6 (addition), D: 6 × 6 (nonsense)
- **Correct Answer:** A (3 × 6)
- **On Correct:** "You built 3 times 6. That expression shows 3 groups of 6."
- **Remediation:** Pipeline
> **Design Note:** Two-question routine embedded in scaffolding. Options include reversal (#10), addition (#11), and nonsense distractor. This interaction completes Section 1's transition from observation to guided practice.
---
## 1.7.2 LESSON SECTION 2: Pattern Across Contexts
**Purpose:** Show that the SAME structure (equal groups) appears in different visual forms. Bags and boxes both show equal groups—different containers, same mathematical structure. This prevents context-specific understanding. Introduce Methods C/D (tiles) with partial worked example.
**Context:** Bags AND boxes (showing pattern across representations)
---
### Interaction 2.1: Show Pattern—Bags AND Boxes
- **Purpose:** Relational learning moment—students see that different containers can represent the same mathematical structure.
- **Visual: Context Visualizations (Mode 2) (×2).** Split screen: Left shows concrete (bags), Right shows concrete (boxes). 2 groups of 5 items each. Same structure in both. \[RELATIONAL COMPARISON\] designation.
- **Guide:** "Look at these two. On the left—bags. On the right—boxes."
- **Visual:** Bags highlight, then boxes highlight
- **Guide:** "Different containers. But notice what's the SAME."
- **Visual:** Both visuals show "2 groups" label appear
- **Guide:** "Both show 2 groups."
- **Visual:** Items in one bag highlight, then items in one box highlight
- **Guide:** "Both have 5 in each group."
- **Guide:** "Different containers—bags or boxes—but the SAME structure. Both are 2 groups of 5. Both build the same expression: 2 times 5."
- **Visual:** Both expressions `2 × 5` highlight simultaneously
- **Guide:** "Equal groups work the same way no matter what holds them."
- **Student Action:** Observation only (no response required).
	- This is relational teaching—building conceptual understanding that structure transcends visual form.
> **Design Note:** This is the RELATIONAL phase's core moment—showing the pattern exists across different visual forms. Prevents "3×4 only means bags" misconception.
---
### Interaction 2.2: Equal vs. Unequal Groups — Can You Write an Expression?
- **Purpose:** Establish that multiplication expressions describe EQUAL groups specifically — not any collection of items in containers.
- **Visual: Context Visualizations (Mode 2) (×4).** Four different bag scenarios labeled A-D. All use bags (isolates variable to contents). Different internal quantities showing equal and unequal configurations.
- **Guide:** "All of these show bags with items inside. But can I write a multiplication expression for all of them? Remember — multiplication shows EQUAL groups. Select the pictures where every bag has the SAME number inside."
- **Prompt:** "Select ALL the pictures that show equal groups."
- **Student Action:** Multi-select from A, B, C, D.
	- **Options:** A: 3 bags with 4 items each (equal — 3 × 4) ✓, B: 3 bags with 4, 2, 6 items (unequal), C: 4 bags with 3 items each (equal — 4 × 3) ✓, D: 4 bags with 3, 3, 1, 5 items (unequal)
- **Correct Answer:** A and C
- **On Correct:** "A has 3 bags with 4 in each — equal groups, 3 times 4. C has 4 bags with 3 in each — equal groups, 4 times 3. B and D? Different amounts in the bags so they have unequal groups. We can't write a multiplication expression for those."
- **On Partially Correct (selected A and C but also B or D):** "A and C are right — equal groups. But look inside every bag in \[B/D\]. Count each one. Are they all the same?"
	- **Visual:** Items in each bag of the incorrectly selected image highlight with counts displayed.
- **On Missed A or C:** "Check inside every bag. If each bag has the same number, that's equal groups — and we can write a multiplication expression."
- **Remediation:** Pipeline
> **Design Note:** Multi-select with 4 options avoids binary response patterns. Distractor B has a first bag that matches A (both start with 4 items), forcing students to check ALL groups. Distractor D has two correct bags and two incorrect, plus a total (12) that matches C's total — testing whether students check group equality, not just the total count. All four use bags to isolate the variable: the criterion is equal contents, not container type. Placed after 2.1's "different containers, same structure" lesson to complete the conceptual pair.
---
### Interaction 2.3: Introducing Methods C/D—Tile Construction
- **Purpose:** Demonstrate the tile-based construction process (Methods C/D) that students will use for more independent building.
- **Visual: Context Visualizations (Mode 2) + Equation Builder (Methods C/D).** Concrete context (boxes with items). 5 boxes with 2 items each. Tile palette visible (tiles 1 through 10). \[PARTIAL WORKED EXAMPLE\] designation.
- **Guide:** "You've learned that you need equal groups to write multiplication expressions and that equal groups work the same way no matter what holds them. These are important ideas for multiplication."
- **Guide:** "You've been choosing complete expressions to represent equal groups. Now you'll build them piece by piece using number tiles. Watch."
- **Guide:** "Notice all of the groups are equal. Same questions: How many groups?"
- **Visual:** Boxes highlight sequentially
- **Guide:** "5 boxes. That's 5 groups. I need the 5 tile in the FIRST slot."
- **Visual (Demo):** Guide demonstrates drag or click-pickup of 5 tile to first slot
- **Visual:** Tile snaps into first slot: `5 × [___]`
- **Guide:** "How many in each group?"
- **Visual:** Items in one box highlight
- **Guide:** "2 items. I need the 2 tile in the SECOND slot."
- **Visual (Demo):** Guide demonstrates placing 2 tile
- **Visual:** Complete expression appears: `5 × 2`
- **Guide:** "I built it piece by piece—5 times 2. Now you try with tiles."
- **Student Action:** Observation only (no response required).
	- Demonstration completes before student attempts.
---
### Interaction 2.4: Student Builds with Tiles—Decomposed Prompts
- **Purpose:** Student uses tiles (Methods C/D) to build an expression with full verbal scaffolding.
- **Visual: Context Visualizations (Mode 2) + Equation Builder (Methods C/D).** Concrete context (bags with items). 2 bags with 4 items each. Tile palette shows 1 through 10. \[NEW\] initialization.
- **Guide:** "Build the expression for these bags using tiles. First number: how many groups? Second number: how many in each group?"
- **Prompt:** "Build the expression."
- **Student Action:** Equation Builder Methods C or D (student choice of interaction method).
	- Validation: Expression must be `2 × 4`
- **Correct Answer:** 2 × 4
- **On Correct:** "You built it—2 times 4 shows 2 groups of 4."
- **Remediation:** Pipeline
> **Design Note:** Two-question routine embedded in scaffolding. Student manages full tile construction process. This completes Section 2's relational learning and introduction to Methods C/D.

## 1.7.3 LESSON SECTION 3: Independent Production + Meaning
**Purpose:** Students build expressions independently with reduced scaffolding, then reverse the direction (expression → visual) to confirm expressions MEAN something, not just procedural patterns.
**Context:** Boxes (application of relational learning), then reverse direction tasks
---
### Interaction 3.1: Independent Tile Building
- **Purpose:** Student builds expression independently with minimal scaffolding.
- **Visual: Context Visualizations (Mode 2) + Equation Builder (Methods C/D).** Concrete context (boxes with items). 3 boxes with 5 items each. Tile palette shows 2, 3, 5, 8, 9, 12, 15. \[NEW\] initialization.
- **Guide:** "Build the expression. Same two questions: how many groups? How many in each?"
- **Prompt:** "Build the expression."
- **Student Action:** Equation Builder Methods C or D.
	- Validation: Expression must be `3 × 5`
- **Correct Answer:** 3 × 5
- **On Correct:** "Nice—3 times 5."
- **Remediation:** Pipeline
> **Design Note:** Scaffolding reduced—no decomposed prompts in Guide dialogue. Student manages the full process. Tile palette constrained to relevant numbers (both factors) plus diagnostic distractors.
---
### Interaction 3.2: Worked Example—Expression to Visual
- **Purpose:** Model the reverse direction (Expression → Visual) to establish that expressions REPRESENT specific visual structures.
- **Visual: Equation Builder (expression mode) + Context Visualizations (×3 options).** Expression `2 × 6` displayed (complete, no slots). Three bag options showing different group structures. \[WORKED EXAMPLE - REVERSE DIRECTION\] designation.
- **Guide:** "We've been going from pictures to expressions. Let's flip it around. If I see 2 times 6, what does that MEAN?"
- **Guide:** "The first number is groups. So 2 groups."
- **Visual:** Option A (2 bags with 6 items each) flashes subtly
- **Guide:** "The second number is in each group. So 6 in each."
- **Visual:** Items in option A highlight
- **Guide:** "That means 2 groups of 6. This picture shows it."
- **Visual:** Option A highlights completely
- **Guide:** "The expression tells you what to look for. 2 times 6 MEANS 2 groups with 6 in each."
- **Student Action:** Observation only (no response required).
	- Worked example—demonstration of reverse direction thinking.
> **Design Note:** Reverse direction confirms that expressions represent meaning, not just symbol patterns. Options include: correct (2 × 6), reversal (6 × 2), sum of factors (8 items scattered), and product-same-wrong-expression (3 bags of 4 items, also totals 12).
---
### Interaction 3.3: Student Matches Expression to Visual
- **Purpose:** Student performs reverse direction task—given expression, selects matching visual.
- **Visual: Equation Builder (expression mode) + Context Visualizations (×3 options).** Expression `5 × 3` displayed. Three bag options with different group structures.
- **Guide:** "Which picture matches 5 times 3?"
- **Prompt:** "Select the picture that shows 5 times 3."
- **Student Action:** Multiple Choice selection (click visual option).
	- **Options:** A: 5 bags of 3 ✓, B: 3 bags of 5 (reversal), C: 8 items scattered (sum of factors - #11)
- **Correct Answer:** A
- **On Correct:** "That's the one—5 groups with 3 in each."
- **Remediation:** Pipeline
> **Design Note:** Options include reversal (#10 prevention) and sum-of-factors distractor (#11 prevention). Prevents pattern-matching without meaning.
---
### Interaction 3.4: Student Construction with Build Mode
- **Purpose:** Reverse direction application—student uses Build Mode to construct visual representation from expression.
- **Visual: Equation Builder (expression mode) + Build Mode Interface.** Expression `4 × 5` displayed. Empty Build Mode workspace with +/- controls for containers and items-per-container.
- **Guide:** "Now you build the picture. Show me: what does 4 times 5 mean?"
- **Prompt:** "Add boxes and items to show 4 × 5."
- **Student Action:** Build Mode - set container count to 4 and items-per-container to 5.
	- Validation: 4 boxes of 5 items
- **Correct Answer:** 4 boxes of 5 items (visual populates correctly)
- **On Correct:** "You built it. All of the groups are equal. 4 boxes with 5 items in each. 4 groups of 5."
- **Remediation:** Pipeline
> **Design Note:** Reverse direction spans 4 interactions (3.2 worked example, 3.3 MC selection, 3.4 Build Mode, 4.3 circles MC). Progression within reverse: observe (3.2) → select (3.3) → produce (3.4) → transfer (4.3). Build Mode is M7-familiar; only the input source (expression instead of verbal prompt) is new.
---
## 1.7.4 LESSON SECTION 4: New Visual Context
**Purpose:** Transfer expression-building skill to semi-abstract context (circles with dots from M7). Demonstrate that the structure works across abstraction levels. Practice both directions with reduced scaffolding.
**Context:** Circles with dots (semi-abstract, carried from M7)
---
### Interaction 4.1: Circles Introduction + Build
- **Purpose:** Transfer building skill to new visual context (circles). Establish that structure is context-independent.
- **Visual: Context Visualizations (Mode 2) + Equation Builder (Methods C/D).** Semi-abstract context (circles with dots). 2 circles with 3 dots each. Tile palette shows 1 through 10. \[NEW CONTEXT\] designation.
- **Guide:** "Equal groups aren't always in bags or boxes. Check out these circles. First question: how many groups do you see? Second question: how many in each group? Build the expression using those two numbers."
- **Prompt:** "Build the expression."
- **Student Action:** Equation Builder Methods C or D.
	- Validation: Expression must be `2 × 3`
- **Correct Answer:** 2 × 3
- **On Correct:** "2 times 3—same structure, different look."
- **Remediation:** Pipeline
> **Design Note:** Introduces circles context and demonstrates structure transfer. Full scaffolding maintained during context shift.
---
### Interaction 4.2: Circles—Varied Values
- **Purpose:** Continue transfer phase with different factor values in circles context.
- **Visual: Context Visualizations (Mode 2) + Equation Builder (Methods C/D).** Semi-abstract context (circles with dots). 4 circles with 4 dots each. Tile palette shows 3, 4, 5, 7, 8, 9, 10, 12, 14, 16. \[NEW\] initialization.
- **Guide:** "Build the expression. Remember the two questions: how many groups? How many in each?"
- **Prompt:** "Build the expression."
- **Student Action:** Equation Builder Methods C or D.
	- Validation: Expression must be `4 × 4`
- **Correct Answer:** 4 × 4
- **On Correct:** "4 times 4—both numbers are the same."
- **Remediation:** Pipeline
> **Design Note:** Demonstrates that factor order still matters (4 × 4 is not reversed, but factors happen to be identical). Scaffolding reduced slightly—no two-question routine in Guide dialogue.
---
### Interaction 4.3: Reverse Direction with Circles
- **Purpose:** Complete transfer phase with reverse direction task in circles context. Reduced scaffolding signals independence.
- **Visual: Equation Builder (expression mode) + Context Visualizations (×3 options).** Expression `5 × 6` displayed. Three circle options with different dot structures.
- **Guide:** "Last one. What does 5 times 6 look like?"
- **Prompt:** "Find 5 × 6."
- **Student Action:** Multiple Choice selection (click visual option).
	- **Options:** A: 5 circles of 6 dots ✓, B: 6 circles of 5 dots (reversal), C: 11 dots scattered (sum of factors - #11)
- **Correct Answer:** A
- **On Correct:** "That's it. 5 groups, 6 in each."
- **Remediation:** Pipeline
> **Design Note:** Completes Lesson with reduced scaffolding. Student has seen both directions in both concrete and semi-abstract contexts. Exit Check comes next to verify mastery before Practice.

### Required Phrases and Language
**Must Include in Guide Dialogue:**
- "Expression" (formal introduction after 1.1 worked example)
- "X groups of Y" structure (reinforced throughout)
- "How many groups?" → "How many in each group?" (two-question routine in CONCRETE through mid-TRANSFER)
- "Times" (when saying × symbol: "3 times 4")
- "First number" / "second number" (when describing factor positions)
- "Build the expression" (standard task prompt)
- "What does \[expression\] mean?" (reverse direction tasks)
**Vocabulary Staging:**
- "Expression" — Introduce formally in 1.1 after worked example, use throughout
- "Groups" / "equal groups" — Carry from M7, use naturally
- "Times" / "multiply" — Carry from M7 as × symbol names
- Avoid "factor" — Deferred to M9
- Avoid "product" — Deferred to M9
- Avoid "equation" — Deferred to M9 (M8 builds expressions only, no = sign)
---
### Forbidden Phrases and Concepts
**Must NOT Include:**
- "Factor" terminology (M9)
- "Product" or "total" or "answer" (M9 — no computation in M8)
- "Equals" or "equal sign" or "3 × 4 = 12" (M9 introduces equations)
- "Solve" or "find the answer" (M9)
- "Unknown" or "missing number" or "☐ × 3" (M10)
- "Array" or "rows and columns" (M11+)
- "Commutative" or "3 × 4 is the same as 4 × 3" (M12)
- "Repeated addition" as a method (becomes M9 strategy, but not taught as expression-building method)
- Skip counting to find products (M9)
- Any context variety beyond bags/boxes/circles (M9 expands contexts)
**Scope Reminders During Lesson:**
- Students BUILD expressions (`3 × 4`), they do NOT compute products
- Template is `[___] × [___]` with NO equal sign
- Both directions taught but Visual→Expression is PRIMARY (gets more time)
- Method A scaffolds, Methods C/D are target skill by Exit Check
