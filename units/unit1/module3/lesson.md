## **1.7 LESSON (10-12 minutes)**

**Detail Level:** Full interaction specification (AI formats and tags)

---

### **Core Purpose**

Extend students' understanding of scaled graphs from scale of 2 to scale of 5. Connect scale to skip-counting by 5s (a familiar Grade 2 skill). Introduce bar graph CREATION while maintaining familiar scale (students read bar graphs in M1). Students create both picture graphs and bar graphs with scale of 5, recognizing that both formats show the same data.

---

### **Pedagogical Flow**

M3 extends an established concept (scaling) to a new value (5) and introduces a new skill (bar graph creation). The CRA sequence applies:

| CRA Phase | M3 Application |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Concrete** | Grouping Animation transforms items into groups of 5, then into symbols |
| **Relational** | Students notice efficiency (fewer symbols than scale of 2); connect to skip-counting |
| **Abstract** | Formalize "scale of 5"; transition from reading bar graphs to creating them |
| **Application** | Create both picture graphs and bar graphs; solve comparison problems |

---

### **1.7.1 LESSON SECTION 1: Scale of 5 — Skip-Counting Connection**

**Purpose:** Formalize scale of 5 and explicitly connect to skip-counting by 5s. Students create picture graphs with scaffolding (1 of 4 categories pre-filled from animation). Build on Warmup's scale comparison discovery.

**Orientation:** All vertical

---

**Interaction 1.1: Building on Scale Comparison**

- **Visual: Picture Graphs (side-by-side comparison).** Brief callback — same data as 1:2 and 1:5 picture graphs (both vertical). Key highlighted on 1:5 graph: "Each ⭐ = 5." Data Table not visible.
- **Guide:** "In Warmup, you saw something important. Same data, but the scale-of-5 graph needed fewer symbols. That's because each symbol represents MORE."
- **Guide:** "Same data, fewer symbols. That's why scale of 5 is useful when numbers get bigger. What does each symbol represent when the scale is 5?"
- **Prompt:** "What does each symbol represent when the scale is 5?"
- **Student Action:** \[Multiple choice: 2, 5, 10, 1\]
- **Correct Answer:** 5
- **On Correct:** "Each symbol represents 5 items. That's the scale."
- **Remediation (Light):** "Check the key. It says 'Each symbol = 5.' That's the scale: 5."

---

**Interaction 1.2: Grouping Animation — Groups of 5 \[WORKED EXAMPLE\]**

- **Visual: Data Tables (Numeric Display).** "Favorite Playground Activities" — Swings: 20, Slides: 15, Monkey Bars: 25, Sandbox: 10. Vertical.
- **Visual: Picture Graphs (Grouping Animation).** Items appear for Swings (20 items). Key visible: "Each ⭐ = 5." Animation: 20 items → 4 groups of 5 → 4 symbols stacking on graph. Guide narrates skip-counting.
- **Guide:** "Let's see how 20 items become symbols when the scale is 5. Watch—we'll group by 5s."
- **Animation Sequence (auto-play, ~45 seconds):**
  - 20 items appear
  - Items group: 5, 5, 5, 5 (four groups form)
  - Guide counts aloud: "5, 10, 15, 20. That's 4 groups of 5."
  - Groups transform into 4 symbols stacking upward on picture graph
  - "20 items = 4 symbols. Each symbol shows 5."
- **No student prompt** (observation—animation teaches)
- **On Complete:** Picture graph shows Swings column with 4 symbols. Other columns empty.

---

**Interaction 1.3: Skip-Counting Connection**

- **Visual: Picture Graphs (Mode 2: Creating).** Vertical. Swings column: 4 symbols (from animation). Other columns empty. Key visible: "Each ⭐ = 5."
- **Visual: Data Tables (Numeric Display).** Same data visible alongside
- **Guide:** "It is easier to read this picture graph if you can count by 5s. That's exactly what we do with scale of 5."
- **Guide:** "Let's check: does the picture graph match our data table? 4 symbols, scale of 5. Count by 5s with me… 4 symbols, counting by 5s. What's the total?"
- **Prompt:** "What is the total represented by the picture graph?"
- **Student Action:** \[Multiple choice: 4, 9, 15, 20\]
- **Correct Answer:** 20
- **Answer Rationale:**
  - 20 = Correct (5, 10, 15, 20)
  - 4 = Error: Counted symbols only (Misconception #1)
  - 9 = Error: Added 5+4 (Misconception #2)
  - 15 = Counting error (stopped at 3 groups)
- **On Correct:** "20 items. 5, 10, 15, 20. Skip-counting tells you the total in the picture graph and that matches the value in the data table."
- **Remediation (Light):** "Count by 5s: 5, 10, 15, 20. That's 4 fives. 20 total."
- **Vocabulary Reinforced:** "count by 5s," "scale of 5"

---

**Interaction 1.4: Your Turn — Guided Creation**

- **Visual: Data Tables (Numeric Display).** Full data visible. Vertical.
- **Visual: Picture Graphs (Mode 2: Creating).** Vertical. Swings = 4 symbols (pre-filled from animation). Slides, Monkey Bars, Sandbox empty. Key visible.
- **Guide:** "Slides has 15. How many symbols is that?"
- **Guide:** "Think: count by 5s until you reach 15. How many 5s? Click to set the height of the Slides."
- **Prompt:** "Set the number of symbols for Slides."
- **Student Action:** Hover over Slides column to see preview fill, click to set at 3 symbols
- **Correct Answer:** 3 symbols placed
- **On Correct:** "3 symbols. 5, 10, 15. You used skip-counting to figure it out."
- **Remediation (Light):** "Count by 5s: 5, 10, 15. That's 3 fives. Place/Select 3 symbols."

---

**Interaction 1.5: Complete the Graph**

- **Visual: Data Tables (Numeric Display).** Same data. Vertical.
- **Visual: Picture Graphs (Mode 2: Creating).** Vertical. Swings = 4, Slides = 3 (student-created). Monkey Bars, Sandbox empty. Key visible.
- **Guide:** "Monkey Bars has 25. That's a bigger number. Click to set the height of the Monkey Bars."
- **Prompt:** "Set the height for Monkey Bars."
- **Student Action:** Hover to preview, click to set at 5 symbols
- **Correct Answer:** 5 symbols placed
- **On Correct:** "5 symbols for 25. You counted: 5, 10, 15, 20, 25."
- **Remediation (Light):** "Count by 5s: 5, 10, 15, 20, 25. That's 5 fives. Place 5 symbols."

---

**Interaction 1.5b: Continue Building — Sandbox**

* **Visual:** Same vertical picture graph. Swings=4 symbols (from animation), Slides=3 symbols, Monkey Bars=5 symbols (just placed). Sandbox column empty. Data table remains visible.
* **Guide:** "Let's complete the picture graph. Sandbox has 10. That's the smallest number. Click to set the height for Sandbox."
* **Prompt:** "Set the height for Sandbox."
* **Student Action:** Hover to preview, click to set at 2 symbols
* **Correct Answer:** 2 symbols placed
* **On Correct:** "2 symbols for 10. Count by 5s: 5, 10."
* **Remediation (Light):** "Count by 5s: 5, 10. That's 2 fives. Click at 2."

---

**Interaction 1.6: Reading Your Graph — Most and Least**

- **Visual: Picture Graphs (Mode 2: Creating).** Vertical. Completed graph: Swings = 4, Slides = 3, Monkey Bars = 5, Sandbox = 2. Key visible: "Each ⭐ = 5." Data Table NOT visible.
- **Guide:** "You made a scaled picture graph. Let's read it. Which activity is the most popular?"
- **Prompt:** "Click on the activity that is most popular."
- **Correct Answer:** Monkey Bars (5 symbols = 25)
- **On Correct:** "Monkey Bars has 5 symbols, that's 25. The tallest column has the most votes."
- **Guide (follow-up):** "And which got the fewest?"
- **Prompt:** "Click on the category with the fewest."
- **Correct Answer:** Sandbox (2 symbols = 10)
- **On Correct:** "Sandbox has only 2 symbols, that's 10 votes."

### **1.7.2 LESSON SECTION 2: Bar Graph Creation Introduction**

**Purpose:** Introduce bar graph CREATION (students read bar graphs in M1). Same scale (5), new skill. Students see that bars and symbols show the same information. Students create bar graphs with scaffolding (2 of 4-5 categories pre-filled).

**Orientation:** All vertical

---

**\[SECTION TRANSITION\]**

- **Visual:** Picture graph from Section 1 remains visible on left side of screen.
- **Guide:** "You made a picture graph with scale of 5. Now let's see ANOTHER way to show the exact same data."

---

**Interaction 2.1: Two Ways to Show Scale of 5**

- **Visual: Picture Graphs (Mode 2: Creating).** Completed "Favorite Playground Activities" graph from Section 1 (vertical). Swings=4 symbols, Slides=3, Monkey Bars=5, Sandbox=2. Key visible: "Each ⭐ = 5."
- **Visual: Bar Graphs (Mode 2: Creating).** SAME data shown as bar graph (vertical). Categories: Swings=20, Slides=15, Monkey Bars=25, Sandbox=10. Axis labeled 0–30 in 5s. All 4 bars pre-filled. Side-by-side with picture graph.
- **Visual: Data Tables (Numeric Display).** Same "Playground Activities" data visible alongside both graphs.
- **Guide:** "You've read bar graphs before. Here's the same Playground data as a bar graph."
- **Guide:** "In a picture graph, you count by 5s using SYMBOLS: 5, 10, 15, 20, 25."
- **Guide:** "In a bar graph, you count by 5s using the numbers written along the side of the graph on the AXIS. Look—5, 10, 15, 20, 25, 30. Same skip-counting, just written on the side. Both graphs count by 5s. Where do you see the 5s on the bar graph?"
- **Prompt:** "Where do you see the 5s on the bar graph?"
- **Student Action:** \[Multiple choice: "In the symbols" / "On the axis" / "In the title"\]
- **Correct Answer:** "On the axis"
- **On Correct:** "The axis shows the skip-counting: 5, 10, 15, 20, 25, 30. That's your scale of 5—built right into the side of the graph."
- **Vocabulary Introduced:** "axis"

---

**Interaction 2.2: Reading Bar Height**

- **Visual: Bar Graphs (Mode 2: Creating).** Vertical. Same side-by-side display from 2.1 (picture graph + bar graph). Slides category highlighted on both graphs. Bar height = 15, picture graph = 3 symbols. Data Table NOT visible.
- **Guide:** "In a bar graph, you read the HEIGHT, where the bar ENDS on the axis. Look at Slides. What's the HEIGHT of the Slides bar? Where does it end?"
- **Prompt:** "What's the HEIGHT of the Slides bar?"
- **Student Action:** \[Multiple choice: 3, 10, 15, 20\]
- **Correct Answer:** 15
- **Answer Rationale:**
  - 15 = Correct (bar height ends at 15 mark)
  - 3 = Error: Counted symbols from picture graph (Misconception #1)
  - 10 = Misread axis
  - 20 = Misread axis
- **On Correct:** "15. The bar's HEIGHT is 15. It ends at 15 on the axis. Same as 3 symbols on the picture graph. Both show 15."
- **Remediation (Light):** "Find the HEIGHT: where the top of the bar lines up with the numbers on the side, the axis."
- **Vocabulary Reinforced:** "height" (key bar graph term), "axis"

**Interaction 2.6: Comparison Question — Bars Work Too**

- **Visual: Bar Graphs (Mode 2: Creating).** Vertical. Completed "Favorite Lunch Foods" bar graph. Tacos (30) and Salad (15) bars highlighted. Data Table and picture graph NOT visible.

- **Remediation (Light):** "How many MORE means find the difference. 30 minus 15 is..."

**Interaction 3.2b: Three-Scale Toggle — Same Data, Your Choice of Scale \[OBSERVATION ONLY\]**

- **Purpose:** Extend M2's binary scale comparison to a three-way pattern. Student toggles between scales 1, 2, and 5 on their own graph data, discovering that "bigger scale = fewer symbols" is a general principle. Builds metarepresentational competence through direct manipulation—the student causes the transformation, making scale a visible choice rather than an invisible given.
- **Visual: Picture Graphs (side-by-side comparison).** Student's completed 1:5 horizontal "Books Read" graph displayed. Scale toggle control with three options: "Each ⭐ = 1," "Each ⭐ = 2," "Each ⭐ = 5." Defaults to scale of 5. Data Table visible as stable anchor (Liam=35, Emma=20, Noah=25, Olivia=40). Key updates dynamically. Toggle sequence: 5→1 (Olivia: 8→40 symbols), 1→2 (Olivia: 40→20 symbols; half-symbols appear for odd-divided values), 2→5 (return to original).
- **Guide:** "You built this graph with scale of 5. But what would it look like with a different scale? Try it."
- **Prompt:** "Toggle the scale to 1. What happens?"
- **Student Action:** Click scale toggle from 5 to 1. Graph animates: symbols multiply (Olivia: 8 → 40; Liam: 7 → 35; Noah: 5 → 25; Emma: 4 → 20). Key updates to "Each ⭐ = 1."
- **Guide:** "Olivia went from 8 symbols to 40! That's a LOT of symbols. Now try scale of 2."
- **Student Action:** Click scale toggle from 1 to 2. Graph animates: symbols reduce. Key updates to "Each ⭐ = 2."
- **Guide:** "Fewer symbols — but still more than scale of 5. Toggle back to 5."
- **Student Action:** Click scale toggle from 2 to 5. Graph animates back to original state. Key updates to "Each ⭐ = 5."
- **Guide:** "Same 40 books for Olivia every time. Scale of 1: 40 symbols. Scale of 2: 20 symbols. Scale of 5: just 8."
- **Prompt:** "Which scale uses the fewest symbols?"
- **Student Action:** \[Multiple choice: Scale of 1, Scale of 2, Scale of 5\]
- **Correct Answer:** Scale of 5
- **On Correct:** "Scale of 5. Bigger scale, fewer symbols — same data."
- **Remediation (Light):** "Toggle through all three again and count Olivia's symbols each time. 40, then 20, then 8. Which is fewest?"
- **Guide:** "All three showed the same information. The scale just changes how many symbols you need. Now let's use YOUR graph to answer some questions."
- **Duration:** 45–55 seconds
- **No student graph creation** (toggle is observation/exploration only)

**Design Notes:**

- Student toggles through all three scales in sequence (5→1→2→5). The full cycle makes scale of 5's efficiency feel earned, not asserted.
- Half-symbols appear naturally at scale of 2 for odd-valued results. Guide does NOT call attention to this—it's background observation. Students already learned half-symbols in M2.
- Animation between states should be smooth (~0.5s). The toggle becomes inactive after the MC question is answered.
- The data table stays visible throughout as the stable reference point. The graph transforms; the data doesn't.

### **Required Phrases (Must Appear in Script)**

- "Each symbol shows 5 items" (scale meaning)
- "Count by 5s" / "skip-counting" (strategy connection)
- "5, 10, 15, 20..." (explicit skip-counting modeled)
- "That's \[X\] groups of 5" (groups language for M7 foundation)
- "You've read bar graphs before" (M1 callback—not new format)
- "Now you'll CREATE one" (new skill framing)
- "Same data, different format" (format connection)
- "Bar ends at \[X\]" / "read where the bar ends" (bar reading instruction)
- "Same scale, different format" (synthesis concept)
- "This graph goes sideways—horizontal" (orientation acknowledgment in 3.1)

---

### **Forbidden Phrases (Create Misconceptions)**

- ❌ "This is called a BAR GRAPH" (they know this from M1)
- ❌ "Each symbol represents 2/10" (wrong scales)
- ❌ "Multiply by 5" / "times 5" / "×" notation (multiplication is M7+)
- ❌ "Half-symbol" (not applicable—all values multiples of 5)
- ❌ "You're so smart!" / "Perfect!" / "Amazing!" (identity labels, overpraise)
- ❌ "You thought carefully about that" (assumed internal state)
- ❌ "Estimate where the bar goes" (no interpolation—all values at tick marks)
- ❌ "Drag the bar" (interaction is click-to-height/width)

---

### **Misconception Prevention**

**#1 Counting Symbols Instead of Scaling:**

- Interaction 1.3 explicitly tests scale understanding with "4" as distractor
- Interaction 2.2 includes "3" as distractor (symbol count instead of value)
- Every reading task requires calculating actual value, not symbol count
- Skip-counting modeled aloud: "5, 10, 15, 20"
- Guide consistently narrates: "X symbols, that's \[total\]"

**#6 "More Than" Means Add:**

- Interaction 2.6 includes "45" distractor (sum instead of difference)
- Frame as callback: "You already know how to compare"
- Light remediation available: "Compare means find the difference"

**#2 Scale Means Addition:**

- Interaction 1.3 includes "9" distractor (4+5)
- "Groups of 5" language throughout
- Skip-counting as bridge (not addition)

### **1.7.5 Success Criteria**

**The One Thing:** Students understand that scale of 5 means each symbol represents 5 items, and they can use skip-counting by 5s to create and read scaled graphs in both picture and bar formats.

**Ready for Exit Check:** Students can:

- Explain that scale of 5 means each symbol = 5 items
- Use skip-counting to determine symbol counts (20 items = 4 symbols)
- Create scaled picture graphs (1:5) from data tables
- Create bar graphs (1:5) from data tables
- Read bar heights/widths from a scaled axis
- Solve comparison and total problems using scaled graphs
- Work with both vertical and horizontal orientations

**Ready for Module 4:** Students have:

- Solid scale of 5 understanding
- Fluency with bar graph CREATION (reducing M4's cognitive load)
- Skip-counting as established strategy (transfers to scale of 10)
- Experience with both picture and bar formats at the same scale
- Flexibility with both orientations
