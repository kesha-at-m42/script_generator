## **1.8 EXIT CHECK (3-4 minutes)**

**Detail Level:** Full interaction specification (AI formats and tags)

---

### **Purpose**

Verify Lesson understanding before Practice. Tests whether student can distinguish single-step from two-step problems, identify what to find first when they see "combined/together/altogether," and execute the two-step strategy accurately.

---

### **Parameters**

| Element | Specification |
| :---- | :---- |
| Problems | 4 (EC.1 single-step + EC.2-3 two-step + EC.4 transfer) |
| Cognitive Types | IDENTIFY and COMPARE (per Playbook: Modules 4-6 can use COMPARE) |
| Time | 3-4 minutes |
| Tone | Calm, low-stakes, neutral |
| Remediation | Full L-M-H added via Remediation Pipeline |

---

### **Constraints**

| MUST | MUST NOT |
| :---- | :---- |
| Use bar graphs only (Lesson format) | Use picture graphs |
| Use scales of 5 and 10 (EC.1-3); scale of 2 (EC.4 only) | Use only one scale |
| Use both orientations (both taught in Lesson) | Use only one orientation |
| Include 1 single-step AND 2+ two-step problems | Test only two-step (would miss discrimination skill) |
| Use chained submissions for two-step problems | Use non-chained (that's for late Practice) |
| Use 4-5 categories with 3 relevant | Use fewer categories (wouldn't test misconception #17) |
| Match Lesson difficulty (values multiples of 5) | Introduce non-multiples or larger ranges |

**Submission Format Note:** Exit Check uses CHAINED submissions (matching Lesson). Non-chained submissions are introduced in Practice Activities 10-12 to build toward state test independence.

---

### **Alignment Check**

| Problem | Cognitive Type | Tests | Lesson Source |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| EC.1 | COMPARE | Single-step comparison (verifies no over-application) | Section 1: Interaction 1.2 |
| EC.2 | IDENTIFY + COMPARE | Two-step: identify first step + execute | Section 2: Interactions 2.3-2.4 |
| EC.3 | IDENTIFY + COMPARE | Two-step: different context, same strategy | Section 3: Interactions 3.1-3.2 |
| EC.4 | COMPARE | Two-step with scale of 2 (transfer test) | Section 3: Interactions 3.1-3.4 |

---

### **Transition into Exit Check**

- **Visual:** Lesson graph clears. Clean transition.
- **Guide:** "You learned to solve problems that need two steps. You know to find the combined total first, then compare. Let's see what you know."

**Voice Note:** Low-stakes framing. References the strategy explicitly. Brief, calm.

---

**Problem EC.1: Single-Step Comparison (COMPARE)**

- **Visual:** Vertical bar graph. Scale of 5 (axis 0-50). Context: "School Supplies Sold" with 4 categories. Values: Pencils 40, Erasers 25, Notebooks 35, Markers 20.
- **Guide:** "How many more pencils were sold than markers?"
- **Prompt:** How many more pencils were sold than markers?
- **Options:** [A) 60, B) 40, C) 20, D) 15]
- **Correct Answer:** C (40 - 20 = 20)
- **Answer Rationale:**
  - 60 = Added instead of compared
  - 40 = Pencils value (didn't complete comparison)
  - 20 = Correct (40 - 20)
  - 15 = Computation error
- **On Correct:** "20 more pencils sold. This one asked about only two categories and was just one step—no 'combined,' so straight to comparing."
- **On Incorrect:** [Remediation Pipeline]

**Alignment:** Tests that student doesn't over-apply two-step strategy. Mirrors Lesson 3.3 (mixed single-step problem). Feedback echoes Lesson language.

**Design Note:** This problem has NO "combined/together/altogether" language. Students should recognize it as single-step and solve directly. If they look for a first step, they're over-applying the strategy.

---

**Problem EC.2: Two-Step Problem (IDENTIFY + COMPARE)**

- **Visual:** Horizontal bar graph. Scale of 10 (axis 0-60). Context: "Books Read This Month" with 4 categories. Values: Fiction 50, Nonfiction 30, Comics 40, Poetry 20.
- **Guide:** "How many more fiction books were read than nonfiction and poetry combined?"

**[CHAINED - Step 1: Identify First Step]**

- **Prompt:** "What should we find first?"
- **Options:** [A) Add nonfiction and poetry, B) Compare fiction to nonfiction, C) Add all four categories, D) Subtract poetry from nonfiction]
- **Correct Answer:** A
- **On Correct:** "Combine nonfiction and poetry first."
- **On Incorrect:** "The question says 'nonfiction and poetry combined.' Find that total first."

**[CHAINED - Step 2: Execute Combining]**

- **Visual:** Nonfiction bar (30) and Poetry bar (20) highlight.
- **Guide:** "What's the combined total of nonfiction and poetry?"
- **Prompt:** How many nonfiction and poetry books combined?
- **Options:** [A) 10, B) 40, C) 50, D) 60]
- **Correct Answer:** C (30 + 20 = 50)
- **On Correct:** "50 combined."
- **On Incorrect:** "Nonfiction is 30. Poetry is 20. Add them: 30 plus 20."

**[CHAINED - Step 3: Execute Comparison]**

- **Visual:** Combined value (50) displayed. Fiction bar (50) highlights.
- **Guide:** "Nonfiction and poetry combined is 50. How many fiction books were read? Find how many more."
- **Prompt:** How many more fiction books than nonfiction and poetry combined?
- **Options:** [A) 100, B) 50, C) 10, D) 0]
- **Correct Answer:** D (50 - 50 = 0)
- **Answer Rationale:**
  - 100 = Added instead of compared
  - 50 = Fiction value or combined total (stopped too early)
  - 10 = Used wrong values
  - 0 = Correct (equal amounts—no difference)
- **On Correct:** "Zero! Fiction is 50, combined is 50—same amount. No difference."
- **On Incorrect:** [Remediation Pipeline]

**Alignment:** Tests two-step strategy with scale of 10. "0" answer tests whether students complete the comparison rather than stopping at the combined total.

**Design Note:** Three-step chain matches Lesson format. Students execute both operations.

---

**Problem EC.3: Two-Step Problem (IDENTIFY + COMPARE)**

- **Visual:** Vertical bar graph. Scale of 5 (axis 0-60). Context: "Votes for Field Trip" with 5 categories. Values: Aquarium 35, Museum 25, Zoo 45, Park 30, Farm 15.
- **Guide:** "How many fewer votes did the museum and farm combined get than the zoo?"

**[CHAINED - Step 1: Identify First Step]**

- **Prompt:** "What should we find first?"
- **Options:** [A) Add museum and farm, B) Compare zoo to museum, C) Add all five places, D) Subtract farm from museum]
- **Correct Answer:** A
- **On Correct:** "Combine museum and farm first."
- **On Incorrect:** "'Museum and farm combined'—add those first."

**[CHAINED - Step 2: Execute Combining]**

- **Visual:** Museum bar (25) and Farm bar (15) highlight.
- **Guide:** "What's the combined total of votes for the museum and farm?"
- **Prompt:** How many votes did museum and farm get combined?
- **Options:** [A) 10, B) 30, C) 40, D) 45]
- **Correct Answer:** C (25 + 15 = 40)
- **On Correct:** "40 combined."
- **On Incorrect:** "Add museum and farm: 25 plus 15."

**[CHAINED - Step 3: Execute Comparison]**

- **Visual:** Combined value (40) displayed. Zoo bar (45) highlights.
- **Guide:** "Museum and farm combined is 40. Now find how many fewer votes they got than the zoo."
- **Prompt:** How many fewer votes did the museum and farm combined get than the zoo?
- **Options:** [A) 85, B) 45, C) 40, D) 5]
- **Correct Answer:** D (45 - 40 = 5)
- **Answer Rationale:**
  - 85 = Added instead of compared
  - 45 = Zoo value alone
  - 40 = Combined total (stopped too early—Misconception #7)
  - 5 = Correct
- **On Correct:** "5 fewer. Museum and farm combined got 5 fewer votes than the zoo."
- **On Incorrect:** [Remediation Pipeline]

**Alignment:** Tests same skill with different question phrasing ("how many fewer" instead of "how many more"). Uses 5 categories to verify student filters correctly (Misconception #17). Distractor C (40) catches students who stop at the combined total without completing the comparison.

**Design Note:** Three-step chain matches Lesson format. Combined total appears as distractor to catch incomplete strategy execution.

---

**Problem EC.4: Two-Step with Scale of 2 (COMPARE) [TRANSFER TEST]**

- **Visual:** Bar graph (vertical, scale of 2, 4 categories: "Books Read This Week"). Values: Maya 14, Ben 9, Carla 11, David 6.
- **Guide:** "Different graph, different scale. Use what you know."
- **Prompt (Step 1):** "How many books did Maya and Ben read together?"
- **Options:** [A) 23, B) 5, C) 14, D) 22]
- **Correct Answer:** A (14 + 9 = 23)
- **On Correct:** "23 books together."
- **On Incorrect:** "Find Maya's books and Ben's books. Add them."

**[CHAINED - Step 2]**

- **Prompt:** "How many MORE books did Maya and Ben read together than Carla?"
- **Options:** [A) 34, B) 12, C) 23, D) 17]
- **Correct Answer:** B (23 - 11 = 12)
- **On Correct:** "12 more. You found the combined total first, then compared."
- **On Incorrect:** "Maya and Ben together is 23. Carla read 11. What's the difference?"
- **On Incorrect:** [Remediation Pipeline]

**Alignment:** Scale of 2 with non-multiples-of-5 values (14, 9, 11, 6) tests transfer. Two-step structure verifies strategy applies across all scales.

**Design Note:** Chained submission matches EC.2 and EC.3 format.

---

### **Exit Check Closure**

- **Visual:** Final graph clears.
- **Guide:** "You're ready to practice."

**Voice Note:** Brief, confident. No overpraise. Student demonstrated understanding.
