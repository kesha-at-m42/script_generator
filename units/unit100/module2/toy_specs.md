## §1 The One Thing

> The equal sign is relational, so a multiplication equation can be written with the product on either side — `a × b = c` and `c = a × b` are equivalent forms of the same fact, and neither is preferred.

**Operational elaboration.** M02 teaches exactly one atomic Focus skill: a student who has checked a rectangle in M01 (or who has a multiplication fact in memory) can now read and write that fact in both orientations — `4 × 5 = 20` AND `20 = 4 × 5` — treat both as correct, and explain that the equal sign means *both sides have the same value*, not "write the answer here." The module reuses the canonical 4 × 5 = 20 rectangle the student already built in M01; no new rectangle builds occur in M02, and no rectangle other than 4 × 5 = 20 is shown for instructional beats. M02 establishes the read-direction prerequisite that every downstream module — every fact-equation interaction in M03–M08, and unknown-factor work in a later unit — depends on. Cross-national data ([Powell 2012, PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3374577/); [Li et al. 2008, ERIC EJ1184961](https://files.eric.ed.gov/fulltext/EJ1184961.pdf)) shows U.S. students accept reversed equations at ~28% vs. ~98% in China; this gap does not self-correct ([Knuth et al. 2008](https://alibali.psych.wisc.edu/wp-content/uploads/sites/371/2018/02/KnuthAlibaliHattikudurMcNeilStephens2008.pdf)). M02 is the targeted intervention.

**Hybrid acknowledgment (operations-on-both-sides recognition INCLUDED).** Per Mini Design Doc §3 and §9.1 (SME 2026-04-27, reaffirmed SME 2026-04-28), M02 includes a single recognition-only exposure to operations-on-both-sides equation form (`a × b = b × a`) at L.3 / Beat 4. This addresses [McNeil et al. 2006](https://cladlab.nd.edu/assets/250420/mcneiletal06.pdf) evidence that operations-on-both-sides equations promote relational equal-sign understanding more strongly than reversed-orientation alone. Production of operations-on-both-sides equations remains out of scope; recognition is the bounded exposure. The L.3 recognition beat is not exit-checked — EC.1 still tests only the reversed-orientation Focus skill.

**Mini-module designation.** M02 is the only Mini in Grade 4 Unit 1. It carries full Required-track status — Mini does not downgrade U1.6 stakes. The Mini contract specifies: 7 interactions (W.1, L.1, L.2, L.3, L.4, EC.1, S.1) within the 4–8 band; no mid-module calibration; one concentrated equation-orientation rehearsal arc with one bounded operations-both-sides recognition exposure; word-count band 800–1,800.

**Primary misconception target:** U1.6 / M6 — *reversed-equation rejection / equal-sign-as-operator*. Sole and primary target. 4-tier expansion candidate per Research_Summary §7.5.

**Standards:** 4.OA.B.4 (Addressing).

**CRA distribution (per Mini Design Doc §1):** 20 / 50 / 30 (Concrete / Relational / Abstract). CRA labels in this document are **Concrete / Relational / Abstract** — never "Representational" — per BrainLift v2.

## §4 Scaffolding Progressions

### 4.1 U1.6 Remediation Tiers (reversed-equation rejection / equal-sign-as-operator)

U1.6 is the sole and primary misconception target. Three tiers apply at launch; per Research_Summary §7.5, U1.6 is a 4-tier expansion candidate if prototype data shows persistent rejection of `c = a × b`. Engineering must implement all three tiers; placeholder codes are forbidden per BrainLift DOK3.

| Tier | Trigger pattern | Tutor move | Surface | Return condition |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Light — visual flip** | Student hesitates or selects only one orientation in an early recognition prompt | Tutor pulses the right-panel `c = a × b` equation in the Mode C strip and animates attention across the two equations on the same rectangle. Sentence stem: `Both forms show the same rectangle because ___.` Re-cue without giving the answer. No new rectangle | Hundred Grid Mode C highlight pulse + Drop Down stem | Student accepts both forms on the next recognition move |
| **Medium — balance scale** | Student rejection pattern persists across two consecutive recognition items (selects standard, rejects reversed); student treats `=` as a one-way signal | Re-invoke the balance framing: "The equal sign is not a 'go' button — it's a balance. This side and this side have the same value." Tap-and-hold cross-emphasis on Mode C confirms both equations point to the same rectangle. Uses the SAME canonical 4 × 5 = 20 rectangle, not a new one | Static balance-scale tutor asset + Hundred Grid Mode C cross-emphasis | Student names the equal sign as showing a balance |
| **Heavy — modeled both-forms walkthrough** | Exit Check (EC.1) response shows `U1.6-active` — selects only `a × b = c` in EC1 and rejects `c = a × b` (per Mini Design Doc §6 rejection-pattern flag) | Tutor presents the canonical 4 × 5 = 20 rectangle again with BOTH equations already displayed in Mode C (pre-filled, not student-built), narrates step-by-step: "The area is 20. The side lengths are 4 and 5. `4 × 5` makes 20 — so `4 × 5 = 20`. And 20 is the same as `4 × 5` — so `20 = 4 × 5`." Student then completes a near-copy stem. Re-delivers L.1 with the same canonical fact, then re-enters Exit Check. If second pass still returns Still Gathering, surface to SME / human tutor | Mode B + Mode C pre-filled display + Drop Down scaffolded production | Student completes the next stem with both forms present |

### 4.2 Powell 2012 + ERIC EJ1184961 evidence link

The 4-tier expansion candidate is grounded in the cross-national gap (~28% U.S. vs. ~98% China on reversed-equation acceptance — [Powell 2012](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3374577/); [Li et al. 2008](https://files.eric.ed.gov/fulltext/EJ1184961.pdf)) and the persistence finding ([Knuth et al. 2008](https://alibali.psych.wisc.edu/wp-content/uploads/sites/371/2018/02/KnuthAlibaliHattikudurMcNeilStephens2008.pdf), 54–71% of grade 6–8 students still weak). Heavy-tier escalation is required, not optional, because the misconception does not self-correct.

## §7 Transition into M03

**By the end of M02, the student can:**

- Read and produce a multiplication equation in both orientations (`a × b = c` and `c = a × b`) for any rectangle relationship they have built.
- Identify both orientations as correct and equivalent — "the same fact, written two ways."
- Explain that the equal sign means both sides have the same value (balance), not "compute and write the answer."

**What M03 expects from M02's exit state:**

- M03 (Finding Every Factor Pair) uses both equation orientations in every fact-equation post-Check display. M03 does not re-teach reversed orientation — it assumes M02's Focus skill is in place.
- M03 introduces "factor pair" as a new vocabulary term — the FIRST appearance of "factor" / "factor pair" in student-facing copy. It must not appear in M02.
- M03 introduces the T-chart organizer as a new representation for the factor-pair search procedure. M02 has no T-chart. The T-chart is M03's new thing; both-ways equations are M02's completed thing.
- Commutativity (e.g., `3 × 6` and `6 × 3` describe the same factor pair) surfaces naturally in M03's factor-pair search. M02's L.3 recognition exposure to `a × b = b × a` is a relational equal-sign exposure, NOT a commutativity teaching beat — M03 owns commutativity as a factor-pair property.
- Equivalent-product equations (`4 × 5 = 2 × 10`) are M03 territory and never appear in M02.
- Students entering M03 with a `U1.6-active` flag receive extra emphasis in M03's Mode C displays: every post-Check paired display invokes the balance visual for the flagged student, as a persistent in-context remediation.

**What M02 must NOT preview:**

- M03's systematic factor-pair search procedure
- The words "factor" / "factor pair"
- M03's T-chart layout or any two-column organizer
- Equivalent-product equation form (`a × b = c × d`)
- Prime / composite vocabulary (M04)

**Bridge statement (per Mini Design Doc §7):** M02 enables `read equations with the unknown on the left side (___ = a × b)` in the later unknown-factor module — the read-direction prerequisite established here is load-bearing for that downstream work. Within G4U1, M03 is the immediate consumer: both-orientation display in M03 post-Check interactions depends entirely on M02's Focus skill being in place.
